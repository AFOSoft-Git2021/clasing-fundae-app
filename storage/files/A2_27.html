Emotions

EMOTIONS -a conscious mental reaction (such as anger or fear) subjectively experienced as strong feeling usually directed toward a specific object and typically accompanied by physiological and behavioral changes in the body

Emotions vocabulary words can be really important in describing how we feel and how others are feeling.

EMOTIONS MEANING SAMPLE SENTENCE
afraid Feeling fear or being worried about something. I want someone who's afraid of losing me.
amused To feel entertained so that you laugh or smile at something. His jokes amused the whole table.
angry To feel dislike because of something or someone, to feel annoyed. Mark was stung by thousands of angry bees.
annoyed To feel slightly angry or irritated at someone. Tim really annoyed me in the meeting this morning.
appalled Feeling great shock or disgust at something or someone. I was appalled by what I saw.
astonished Feeling great surprise. I was astonished by how much she'd grown.
bored Tired and annoyed because you are uninterested in something. The boys were getting bored.
composed Calm and having one's feeling under control. Glen was calm and composed at the funeral.
confused Not able to think clearly or understand. They confused me by asking so many questions.
contented / content / contentment Feeling happiness or satisfaction. Two glasses of beer contented him.
curious / curiosity Wanting to know about something or someone. I was curious to know what would happen next.
excited very enthusiastic and eager. They were excited about the prospect.
delighted / delight Very pleased or happy. I know Frank will be delighted to see you.
miserable / depressed Sad and without hope. I've been so miserable since Patrick left me.
determined Feeling very strongly that you will do (complete) something you decided to do. She has determined on going this afternoon.
disappointed Feeling sad and unhappy that something did not happen or did not happen in the way that you wanted or expected. Dad seemed more disappointed than angry.
disgust / disgusted A strong feeling of disapproval or dislike, especially for something that looks, smells, tastes (etc.) unpleasant. Joan looked at him with disgust.
exhausted Extremely tired, completely without energy. The party were all exhausted.
grateful Feeling thankful for something. I can't express how grateful I am.
happy Feeling pleased or pleasure. Life's too short to be anything but happy.
hate / feel hatred Very strong dislike. I hate to see you unhappy.
hopeful Full of hope, feeling that something you want is going to happen. I am hopeful that she will come tomorrow.
hysterical In a state of uncontrolled laughter or extreme excitement. Janet became hysterical and began screaming.
modest Not being too proud or talking very much about your abilities, qualities or possessions. He is a very modest person.
outraged / feel outrage A strong feeling of shock and anger. Customers were outraged by the price increases.
sad Unhappy We all felt sad about his death.
scared thrown into or being in a state of fear, fright, or panic I was scared of the big dog.
satisfied Feeling happy about something that happened or pleased that you did something the way you wanted to. They have plenty of satisfied customers.
serene Calm and peaceful. She has a lovely serene face.
shy Feeling nervous or uncomfortable meeting or talking to other people. The child was shy and hid behind his mother.
silly Playful, having fun and not serious. How silly of me to expect them to help!
sneaky Behaving in a secret and dishonest way. I took a sneaky glance at my watch.
surprised Feeling or showing surprise about something you did not expect to happen. I was somewhat surprised to see him.
wonder A feeling caused by seeing something amazing, beautiful, special or surprising. Sometimes I wonder if he is honest.
