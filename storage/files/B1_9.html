Likely ↔ Probably

LIKELY                        

If something is likely, it will probably happen or is expected. It is more affirmative or nearness in possibility = high probability. It may refer to past events with known outcomes.        

                

USAGE: normally used as an adjective (it may be used as an adverb as well)                        

EXAMPLES:                        

  • He is likely to come at noon. (likely indicates more possibility)                        
  • I am likely to go on holiday in summer.                        
  • My boss is likely to let me work remotely.                
  • Emma’s likely to react very emotionally.                
  • Do remind him, because they are likely to forget.        
  • She’s likely to win the competition.                        
  • The study showed that one in three children is likely to develop drug addiction.        
  • The car is likely to break down soon.                        
  • My mother’s likely to be in the garden watering the plants.                        
  • There’s likely to be a hurricane later this month.                        
  • They aren’t very likely to raise our salary, are they?                        
  • I’m likely to be very busy tomorrow.                        
  • The builders aren’t likely to finish before the summer.                        
  • The meeting is likely to finish late.                        
  • There’s likely to be a lot of traffic this evening.                        
  • We aren’t likely to be here at the weekend.                
  • He is likely to win this game.                        
  • Stubborn children are more likely to become successful.                        
  • They are the most likely to win the World Cup.        
  • It is the most likely event to occur.
  • Which would be most likely to cut his fingers?
  • It's likely a mistake.
  • It's likely to rain soon.

PROBABLY                        

Probably indicates more doubt or less nearness. It refers to the occurrence of future events.                        

: used as an adverb        

                

EXAMPLES:                        

  • Probably he comes at noon. (there is more element of doubt)                        
  • I’ll probably be home on Sunday.                        
  • He probably didn’t even see that I borrowed his jeans.                        
  • She’ll probably win the competition.                        
  • Lucy spends all her spare time playing with her dolls but she’ll probably grow out of it.                        
  • It’s probably the best dish I’ve ever had.                        
  • I’ll probably be very busy tomorrow.                                        
  • James will probably be late. He always is.                        
  • The police say that the burglars were probably local people.                        
  • The weather forecast said it will probably snow tonight.                        
  • The jury will probably find him guilty.                        
  • We’ll probably never know what really happened.                        
  • He’s probably our best player.                        
  • They probably won’t find any evidence.                        
  • They will probably not find any evidence.        
  • I probably sat on it.        
  • Tom is probably busy.        
  • The house is probably haunted.        
  • Angela is probably tired.
