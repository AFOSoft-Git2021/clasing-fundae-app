Can’t + have + past

Can't have + past participleis used when we are sure that something did not happen in the past(the opposite of "must have"). 

  • He can't have been at the meeting, he's on a business trip this week.

  • You can't have seen Terry yesterday because he's in Paris all week.

  • She can't have understood, she doesn't speak English.

  • They can't have watched it all yet, the movie is too long.

  • She can’t have left the house yet because her car is still outside.

  • She usually takes her car so I deduce that she has definitely not left the house because her car is outside.

MORE SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • They can’t have finished the project yet because they only started work on it this week.

  • The prisoner can’t have escaped through that window because it is too small.

  • The kid can’t have been at the movie theater. He was at home all weekend studying for the exam.

  • Susie can’t have said that. She is too nice and wouldn’t say such mean things.

  • She can't have driven there. Her car keys are still here.

  • I thought I saw Adnan this morning but it couldn't have been him – he's in Greece this week.

  • She can't have cleaned the whole house on her own in two hours. She must have had some help.
