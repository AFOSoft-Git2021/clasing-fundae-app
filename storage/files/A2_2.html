Prepositions of Place

A preposition of place is a preposition which is used to refer to a place where something or someone is located. There are only three prepositions of place, however they can be used to discuss an almost endless number of places.

At – A preposition of place which is used to discuss a certain point.

  • Use "at" with places in a town, city or other community,
  • Use "at" with places on a page,
  • Use "at" with places within a room or large space:

In – A preposition of place which is used to discuss an enclosed space.

  • We use "in" with cities, regions, counties, states, and countries.
  • We also use "in" with spaces that you can physically walk into, or place something into.
  • Use "in" with bodies of water.
  • Use "in" with lines.

On – A preposition of time which is used to discuss a surface

  • Use "on" with vertical or horizontal surfaces that you can lay something onto, or attach something to.
  • Use "on" with islands.
  • Use "on" with directions.
Preposition EXAMPLES:
at Jessie waited for Jim at the corner.
The mall is located at the intersection of Main Street and Third Avenue.
You'll find the page number at the top of the page.
They're seated at the back of the bus.
We spent a quiet evening at home.
in I’m growing tomatoes in my garden.
Please stand in a row and let me count you.
I live in Portland which is a city in Oregon.
That duck is swimming in the water.
Marie was born in Paris, France.
I was so tired that I took a nap in the car.
on Please place the bouquet on the table.
Take the next turn on the left.
Have you seen the volcano on the smaller island?
I really wish you would stop throwing your dirty clothes on the floor.
What’s on the menu this evening?
