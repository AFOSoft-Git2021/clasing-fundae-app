Professions

Profession: a paid occupation, especially one that involves prolonged training and a formal qualification. Life has many important aspects and one of it is having a good job. By good job everyone has a different meaning. Some people see a good job as having high income while some see it as low working hours. Job or occupation is the paid task, duty or a responsibility.

Jobs and Occupations Vocabulary Words

OCCUPATION MEANING / DEFINITION SAMPLE SENTENCES
accountant a person that works with the money and accounts of a company. Ana's mother is an accountant.
actor a person that acts in a play or a movie He certainly is a marvellous actor.
architect a person that designs building and houses. He is the architect who planned the new shopping centre.
astronomer a person who studies the stars and the universe A new star attracted the notice of the astronomer.
author They write books or novels. Mark Twain is my favorite author.
baker They make bread and cakes and normally work in a bakery. A baker is kneading a dough.
builder a person that helps to build houses using bricks. Her father was a builder in Birmingham.
bus driver a person that drives buses. The bus driver is responsible for the passengers safety.
butcher a person that works with meat. They cut the meat and sell it in their shop. The butcher prepared a rack of pork.
carpenter A person who makes things with wood. The carpenter was hammering in his workshop.
chef a person that prepared food for others, often in a restaurant or café. The chef used an electric grinder in the kitchen.
cleaner a person that cleans/tidies an area or place (such as in an office) Take your clothes to the cleaner.
dentist a person that can fix problems you have with your teeth. You should visit your dentist at least twice a year.
designer a person who has the job of designing things. He'll only wear clothes with a designer label.
doctor a person you go to see when you are ill or have some type of health problem. The doctor forbids him to smoke.
dustman a person that collects trash/rubbish from bins in the street. He's dustman in our school.
electrician a person that works with electric circuits. We need an electrician to mend the iron.
engineer person who develops solutions to technical problems. They sometimes design, build, or maintain engines, machines, structures or public works. The young engineer rejoiced in his success.
factory worker a person that works in a factory. Luke did a lot to improve conditions for factory workers.
farmer a person that works on a farm, usually with animals. The farmer grows potatoes in this field.
fireman a person that puts out fires. The fireman sent jets of water into the burning house.
fisherman a person that catches fish The fisherman had caught a whopper.
flight attendant A person who serves people on an aeroplane. The flight attendant welcomed us aboard.
florist a person that works with flowers. The florist made up an attractive bouquet.
gardener a person that keeps gardens clean and tidy. They take care of the plants in the garden. The gardener mows the grass regularly.
hairdresser they cut your hair or give it a new style. My hairdresser has opened a new salon.
housekeeper a person employed to manage a household. Also a person employed to perform cleaning and other domestic tasks in a hotel or institution. You need a housekeeper to clean your property.
journalist a person that makes new reports in writing or through television. My job as a journalist is to expose the truth.
judge a qualified person that decides cases in a law court. Andrew's father is a well known judge,
lawyer a person that defends people in court and gives legal advice. The lawyer proved the innocence of his client.
lecturer a person that gives lectures, usually in a university. The lecturer illustrated his point with a diagram on the blackboard.
librarian a person that works in a library. The librarian entered a new book in the catalogue.
lifeguard a person that saves lives where people swim (at a beach or swimming pool). There is always a lifeguard to ensure that no one comes to any harm.
mechanic A person who repairs cars, motorbikes and other vehicles. The mechanic was busy repairing the machine.
model person that works in fashion, modeling clothes and accessories. The model was posing carefully.
newsreader person that reads the news, normally on television. Keep studying and you can become a newsreader.
nurse person trained to help a doctor look after the sick or injured. The nurse should try to develop empathy between herself and the patient.
optician a person that checks your eyes and try and correct any problems with your sight. The optician said I needed new glasses.
painter a person that paints pictures or the interior and exterior of buildings. The young painter has exhibited in several galleries.
pharmacist a qualified person that works with and dispenses medicine. The pharmacist made up the prescribed medicine.
photographer a person that takes photos. I think she will be successful as a photographer.
pilot A person who flies a plane or helicopter. The pilot inquired about the weather condition.
plumber a person that repairs your water systems or pipes. We had to call a plumber to unblock the drains.
politician a person who works in politics. He is a highly ambitious politician.
policeman a member of the police force. They (try and) prevent crime. I am a policeman in the city of New York.
postman a person that delivers mail to your house. The postman has brought a parcel for you.
real estate agent a person that makes money from selling land for development. My father commissioned a real estate agent to sell our house.
receptionist a person that is at the reception (entrance) of a company. Ask the receptionist to put your call through to my room.
scientist a person that works in the science industry. They do many experiments. Einstein was a great scientist.
secretary a person employed in an office who types letters, keeps records etc. My secretary will call you to arrange a meeting.
shop assistant a person that works in a shop or store selling products. The shop assistant was wearing a white overall.
soldier a person who works for the army. He wants to be a soldier when he grows up.
tailor a person that makes clothes for others, many times producing exclusive items of clothing. The tailor took my measurements for a new suit.
taxi driver a person who drives a taxi. The taxi driver leaned through his window.
teacher / professor a person that passes knowledge to students, usually at school. Tom reported his discoveries to the professor.
translator a person that translates from one language to another. She works as a translator of technical texts.
traffic warden a person that patrols areas to check that people do not park in the wrong place. A traffic warden asked him to move his car.
manager A person who is responsible for an organisation. I've spoken to the manager about it.
paramedic a person trained to give emergency medical care to people who are injured or ill, typically in a setting outside of a hospital. A paramedic bandaged his foot.
waiter a man whose job is to serve customers at their tables in a restaurant. The waiter brought up the next dish.

Remember:

1. For some jobs, it's important to distinguish between the feminine and the masculine uses:

Male Female
a policeman a policewoman
a waiter a waitress
an actor an actress
a steward a stewardess

The indefinite article:

- "a" is used before a consonant:

Examples: a postman, a teacher...

- "an" is used before a vowel:

Examples: an engineer, an actor...
