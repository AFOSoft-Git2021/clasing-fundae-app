Sentence Connectors to tell the truth 

In this way, you can present a fact, idea, or argument that sustains a total truth.

To tell the truth

Meaning:  I have to admit.

Sample Sentences:

  • To tell the truth, I couldn’t hear a word he said.
  • To tell the truth, I don't like disco.
  • To tell the truth, I don't know much about it.


In fact

Meaning: used to emphasize the truth of an assertion, especially one contrary to what might be expected or what has been asserted.

Sample Sentences:

  • The brook trout is in fact a char
  • In fact, his business career had distinctly unpromising beginnings.
  • He pretended not to know her but in fact they were in league together.


Actually

Meaning: As the truth or facts of a situation; really.

Sample Sentences:

  • We must pay attention to what young people are actually doing
  • Actually, it's I that owe you a lot.
  • Being rich doesn't actually increase your happiness quotient.


To be honest

Meaning: Speaking frankly.

Sample Sentences:

  • I've never been much of a movie buff, to be honest
  • To be honest , it was one of the worst books I've ever read.
  • I'm not that wild about rap music, to be honest.


Personally

Meaning: From someone's personal standpoint or according to their particular nature; in a subjective rather than an objective way.

Sample Sentences:

  • The show must go on, personally I think so.
  • He had spoken personally and emotionally.
  • Personally I think it's a waste of time.


It is true that

Meaning: In truth (often tends to intensify)

Sample Sentences:

  • It is true that a lot of hard work has been put in here but I really can’t see any reflection of that.
  • It is true that hydrogen is inflammable.
  • It is true that solving problems requires high concentration.


As far as I am concerned

Meaning: to indicate that you are giving your own opinion.

Sample Sentences:

  • As far as I am concerned, I am done with it.
  • As far as I'm concerned, the officials incited the fight. 
  • As far as I am concerned, this meeting is over.

From my point of view

Meaning: You can refer to the opinions or attitudes that you have about something as your point of view.

Sample Sentences:

  • The sin is terrific from my point of view.
  • From my point of view, nine-thirty would be better than nine o’clock, as I have to drop the children off at school for nine.

I try to be the most honest from my point of view.
