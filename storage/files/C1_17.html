PASSIVE GERUND in the PAST

A perfect gerund refers to a past action. The time it refers to is always prior to the time the main verb of the sentence refers to. 

Perfect gerund (passive voice): having + been + past participle

The perfect gerund phrase, here, is in the passive voice. The subject denied an action that he had received. In the passive voice, we focus on the receiver of the action, not the doer.

  • The man denied having been beaten in the jail.

The subject regrets an action that he received in the past. The perfect gerund, here, is working as the direct object of the verb ‘regret‘.

  • I regret having been sent to the new office.

Here, the gerund phrase is referring to a past action that worked out great for the speaker. The perfect gerund is working as the subject of the sentence.

  • Having been fired from the job turned out to be great for me.
