Transportation

Transportation means the action of transporting someone or something or the process of being transported. Talking about transportation is a frequent occurrence in everyday life

Means of transportation

WORD MEANING SAMPLE SENTENCE
tram an electric vehicle similar to a bus but goes along metal tracks on the road In my opinion, the tram system is not as efficient as other means of public transport.
coach a bus used to take groups of people on long journeys. Coach can also be used to talk about the cheapest type of seats on a plane or train. I’d like to tour the country by coach.
yacht a boat used for travelling around for pleasure or racing We spent our holiday on a private yacht in the Caribbean.
bus a vehicle carrying many passengers; used for public transport He always rode the bus to work.
car a vehicle moving on wheels He got into the car and drove away.
train a connected series of railway cars usually hauled by a locomotive Yesterday I retrieved the bag I left in the train.
taxi a car with a driver who you pay to take you somewhere. Do you know where I can get a taxi?
ambulance a special vehicle used to take sick or injured people to the hospital The injured were rushed to hospital in an ambulance.
skateboard a flat, narrow board with two small wheels under each end, which a person stands on and moves forward by pushing one foot on the ground One time I had a skateboard, but it wasn’t very fast.
bicycle a two-wheeled vehicle that you sit on and move by turning the two pedals My bicycle had a puncture and needed patching up.
scooter a child's vehicle with two or three small wheels joined to the bottom of a narrow board and a long vertical handle attached to the front wheel. It is ridden by standing with one foot on the board and pushing against the ground with the other foot Riding a motorcycle is safer than riding a scooter.
motorcycle a vehicle with two wheels and an engine He wheeled his motorcycle into the street.
subway a railroad system in which electric trains travel through tunnels below ground The subway is a public conveyance.
helicopter a type of aircraft without wings, that has one or two sets of large blades that go around very fast on top. It can land and take off vertically and can stay in one place in the air He was rushed to the hospital by helicopter.
airplane a vehicle designed for air travel that has wings and one or more engines The government has just bought a new airplane.
boat a small vehicle for traveling on water The boat should be operational by this afternoon.

Important words to use with Trasportation.

Get In vs. Get On

get in - For smaller vehicles that carry only a few people – cars, trucks, small boats, etc.. The opposite of “get in” is “get out of”. When you arrive at your destination, you get out of a car.

get on - We use these phrases for bigger vehicles like buses, airplanes, and large boats, but we also use them for small vehicles for only one person. We use “get on/off” for bicycles, motorcycles, and horses because you sit on top of them. You can use “get on/off” for anything you sit or stand on top of (skateboard, surfboard, elephant, etc.) The opposite of “get on” is “get off”.

Ride, Drive, or Take

- We use “drive” or “ride in” for the same vehicles

- Use ride for travel on a bicycle, or horse when we control its movements. Use ride or drive for travel on a motorcycle, ride is more common. Use ride for travel in a vehicle (car, bus, train) when we aren’t controlling (driving) it

- Use “drive” if you are operating the vehicle.

- Use “ride in” if you are a passenger.

-Use "take" for travel with public transport, we can replace take with ride
