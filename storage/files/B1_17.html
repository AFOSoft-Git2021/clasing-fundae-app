Should & Must

The words “should” and “must” are modal auxiliary verbs or simply modals. They provide information about the function of the main verb following it. Both “should” and “must” are similar in meaning except that “must” is a much stronger word as compared to “should.”

“Should” is the past tense of “shall.”

 “Should” is used to denote recommendations, advice, or to talk about what is generally right or wrong within the permissible limits of society.

EXAMPLES:

  • You should chew your food properly.
  • We should respect our parents.
  • You should stop smoking.

“Must” is used to talk about an obligation or a necessity. It is used when people are compelled to do something

EXAMPLES:

.

  • You must clean the house as your mom is not well.
  • You must clean our car regularly.
  • We must obey the law.
  • You must hurry if you have to catch the train.
  • You must clean our car regularly.

NEGATIVE FORM of MUST

The negative form of must is mustn’t. Or must not We don’t use don’t/doesn’t/didn’t with must:

We can use the full form must not in formal contexts or when we want to emphasize something.

Subject + must + not + verb

  • There mustn’t be any rubbish left.
  • You must not leave any rubbish.
  • You must not give my credit card details to anyone.
  • You mustn’t tell this to anyone. It’s a secret.
  • I mustn’t leave before they get home from work.
  • They mustn’t say anything to their friends about the project.
  • Students must not copy their work from the Internet. It's illegal!
  • You mustn't drink alcohol before you drive. You could cause an accident.
  • You mustn't use your smartphone while you are driving.
  • You mustn't get on the subway if you haven't paid for the ride.
  • You must not open the gift until it is your birthday.

NEGATIVE FORM of SHOULD

The negative form of should is shouldn’t. We don’t use don’t, doesn’t, didn’t with should:

Subject + should + not + verb

  • You shouldn’t go to the concert because you have a lot of homework.
  • He shouldn’t pay for dinner because he doesn’t have a lot of money.
  • They shouldn’t go out because they are unwell.
  • You shouldn't smoke so much – it's unhealthy.
  • You shouldn’t eat too much sugar.
  • The test shouldn’t take longer than an hour.
  • The flight shouldn’t cost more than $400.
  • I should not have bought this expensive jacket!
  • Julie shouldn’t have told everyone her friend’s secret.
  • You shouldn't have said that to her.
