Compound Adjectives for the Body

Fair-haired

MEANING: having light-colored hair.

EXAMPLES: 

  • Simon is an artist and curator of antiques, had studied in America and had loved a fair-haired girl and taught him to appreciate Western as well as Oriental beauty.

  • The fair-haired chairman repaired the pair of chairs.

  • As the cameras rolled and crowds gathered to stare at the fair-haired American with the local governmental leader, the villagers quietly whispered and joked among themselves.

  • Lacking financial resources, many gang members got their salary reduced, and some former fair-haired members of their bosses were even "laid off".


Curly-haired

MEANING: having hair that forms curls

EXAMPLES: 

  • Scarlett, a curly-haired swine, was invited to watch the family film, which features lots of farmyard animals.

  • Several figures were visible on the ship, especially one very active, curly-haired young man, who seemed to be giving orders.

  • Curly-haired dogs, black dogs, spotted dogs all jumped up, trying to reach me.


Green-eyed

MEANING:  jealous; envious; distrustful; suspicious or unduly suspicious or fearful of being displaced by a rival


EXAMPLES:

  • He knew that IsabelIa was a fair-skinned, green-eyed woman of the best bloodline.

  • I shone in the reflection of her green-eyed gypsy beauty.

  • A young man may suffer from the green-eyed monster if his girlfriend begins going out with someone else.


Long-legged

MEANING: having long legs

EXAMPLES:

  • Fairly large and long-legged wading birds, habitually flying with necks outstretched.

  • These long-legged canines have only four toes per foot, unlike other dogs, which have five toes on their forefeet.

  • An earlier campaign caused chaos in New York in 1998 when a giant poster of a long-legged woman wearing only a pair of the company's famous tights stopped traffic on Broadway.


Left-handed

MEANING: using your left hand to write and do most things

EXAMPLES:

  • Another unexpected finding, left-handed users tended to pick the same starting places as their right-handed counterparts.

  • Because of its dark meaning, left-handed people were forced to switch hands to avoid the stigma.

  • Some of these species will only interact with right-handed amino acids, and some will only interact with the left-handed variety.

  • The Dodgers have 13 left-handed pitchers in camp.


Right-handed

MEANING: using your right hand to write with and do most things:

EXAMPLES:

  • He's a veteran right-handed bat, who will help them if they contend, but could be benched or released if they falter.

  • The only one with no questions about the right-handed pitcher was Elmer.

  • Nature produces many chemicals in two forms that are mirror images of each other - so-called left-handed and right-handed molecules.


Flat-footed

MEANING: having feet whose bottom part is flat against the ground and not curved up in an arch

EXAMPLES:

  • Campaign leaders were caught flat-footed when the opinion polls suddenly started to swing the other way.

  • As with all the best American comedies, the sharpness of the script made the average British sitcom seem embarrassingly flat-footed.

  • All babies look flat-footed and when they walk the whole sole touches the ground. 

  • If you describe a person or action as flat-footed, you think they are clumsy, awkward, or foolish.


Bow-legged

MEANING: A bow-legged person has legs that curve out at the knees

EXAMPLES:

  • The bow-legged cook put his hand in his pocket and drew out a grimy, paper-covered note-book.

  • A bow-legged cowboy wearing wooly chaps burst out upon the sidewalk.

  • On the opposite bank another camp was being made by bow-legged men who wore heavy chaps over their trousers, and broad hats.


Short-sighted

MEANING: A short-sighted person can only clearly see objects that are close to them; not thinking enough about how an action will affect the future

EXAMPLES:

  • She has to wear glasses because she's very short-sighted.

  • My brother has good eyesight whereas I am a little shortsighted.

  • It's very short-sighted of the government not to invest in technological research.

  • This short-sighted alienation of their own supporters may lose them the election.

  • Many junior students have to wear short-sighted glasses for reading.


Cross-eyed

MEANING: having eyes that look in toward the nose; having each eye looking toward the middle of the face.

EXAMPLES:

  • Unless someone is naturally cross-eyed, it seems it would be extraordinarily difficult to keep one's eyes crossed for extended periods.

  • The cross-eyed composer was once again gnawing on a chicken bone, with a noodle dangling from his black beard.

  • She pushed her doll closer, so that Mrs Fanning aped a surprised, cross-eyed look.

Dark-skinned

MEANING: having a brown skin tone : having dark pigmentation of the skin.

EXAMPLES:

  • I was one of those dark-skinned country children who didn't matter.

  • Uncle Shelton was a thin, dark-skinned black man with a sharp conk and a soft-spoken voice.

  • Dark-skinned people rarely develop skin cancer.

  • In dark-skinned people, the discoloration is most visible in the lips, gums, and around the eyes, and it can be easier to spot.


Narrow-waisted

MEANING:  having a small waist

EXAMPLES:

  • The narrow-waisted girl over there is a rising film star.

  • The male can be recognised by its pincer-like appendages and its narrow-waisted body.


Freckle-faced

MEANING: having a lot of freckles on the face

EXAMPLES:

  • A red-haired, freckle-faced boy of fourteen, weighed down with the responsibility of his first essay, walked into a city library the other day.

  • Now in his usual impetuous fashion he conceived an instant dislike for the freckle-faced soldier, which he never had reason to change.

  • The current national poster child is a tiny, freckle-faced redhead.


Soul-destroying

MEANING: Used to describe a situation that makes you very unhappy because you think it will never improve

EXAMPLES:

  • The movie turned out to be about so much more: philosophy, faith vs. science, literature, fantasy, numerology, death-defying love and soul-destroying hate.

  • Always protect, guard, and keep me from soul-destroying words and deeds.

  • Multitudes of people of all classes are believers in this soul-destroying doctrine.

  • A soul-destroying activity and one calculated to make any cat lower its personal standards.


Heart-stopping

MEANING: Very frightening;giving you a sudden very strong feeling of fear

EXAMPLES:

  • For a heart-stopping moment, she thought the man was going to kill her.

  • Amid the heart-stopping spectacle and the pomp and circumstance, it was very much a family occasion.

  •  I need to summon my courage for its wild, exhilarating, heart-stopping ride.

  • The wings of the monarch butterfly, for example, contain powerful heart-stopping poisons called cardiac glycosides.


Heart-warming

MEANING: Making you feel happy, usually because other people are being kind

EXAMPLES:

  • The film is a heart-warming tale of triumph against the odds.

  • These demonstrators were not giving a heart-warming display of democratic values.

  • This film faithfully documents life in the prison, showing the heart-warming side of the prison.


Hair-raising

MEANING: very frightening

EXAMPLES:

  • She gave a hair-raising account of her escape through the desert.

  • She told us a hair-raising story about suddenly meeting a bear on her camping trip.

  • After many hair-raising adventures, most not only survive but emerge wiser and stronger as a result of their harrowing ordeal.

  • Evans had relayed some hair-raising stories about the exploitation of her rich patients.


Eye-catching

MEANING: Attractive or unusual and therefore noticed

EXAMPLES:

  • Solar-powered and sporting an eye-catching design, this gizmo is built to monitor soil conditions and provide water to thirsty plants automatically.

  • Dark wood floors, elaborate chandeliers and a glass-enclosed wine cellar are among the eye-catching details.

  • Meanwhile, 360 represents a novel opportunity for brands to create eye-catching marketing.

  • She loved to dress in expensive, eye-catching clothes and enjoyed reading romantic novels.


Mind-blowing

MEANING: Extremely impressive, exciting, or shocking

EXAMPLES:

  • We have to come up with mind-blowing ideas for new concepts.

  • Watching your baby being born is a mind-blowing experience.

  • It was a mind-blowing development for sports fans and young athletes who had previously been able to imagine themselves as their favorite players only during backyard pickup games.

  • We've gathered twelve of Cassini's most impressive, mind-blowing shots from the last few months.
