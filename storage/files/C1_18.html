Adverbs modifying the whole sentence

Adverbs modifying the whole sentence (also known as sentence adverbs) usually express a speaker’s or narrator’s opinion.

SENTENCE ADVERBS – POSSIBLE POSITION:

After a verb “to be”:

  • She is obviously intelligent.

Before the main verb or between auxiliary and main verb:

  • He actually sold his house!

  • He has actually sold his house!

At the beginning and the end:

  • Unfortunately, Mark didn’t pass the exam.

  • No one got hurt, fortunately.

Example of sentence adverbs:

Actually

MEANING: As the truth or facts of a situation; used to emphasize that something someone has said or done is surprising.

EXAMPLES: 

  • I'd like a cup of coffee, please - actually, on second thought, I'll have a beer.

  • It really irritates me to actually laugh at his pathetic jokes!

  • I didn't actually want any more dessert, but Julia forced it on me.

  • Peter might look fierce, but actually he's fairly harmless.

  • She appears to actually like the man, which I find incredible.

Apparently

MEANING: Used to say you have read or been told something although you are not certain it is true; used to say that something seems to be true, although it is not certain.

EXAMPLES: 

  • An 80-year-old woman was badly hurt in what the police describe as an apparently motiveless attack.

  • The train was delayed, apparently due to leaves on the line.

  • His resignation was apparently for personal rather than professional reasons.

  • He lives in Little Overington, apparently, wherever that is.

  • A series of apparently unconnected events led to his resignation.

  • I heard a rumor that she's leaving, but apparently it's not true.

Certainly

MEANING: Used when agreeing or disagreeing strongly to a request.

EXAMPLES: 

  • We certainly don't lead a life of luxury but we're not poor either.

  • The design certainly looks good on paper.

  • The campaign has certainly succeeded in raising public awareness of the issue.

  • I suppose you think that's funny. Well, I certainly don't.

  • His time in the army certainly toughened him up.

Fortunately

MEANING: Happening because of good luck.

EXAMPLES:

  • Fortunately, there are some positive steps you can take to reclaim your online history for yourself.

  • Fortunately, we had put in a fresh pair of contact lenses that morning and was quite observant.

  • Fortunately, by that point you'll likely have had 4 million each of the other two properties for that group, so you're good to go!

  • The rest of the apartment had fortunately remained unaltered since that time.

  • I was late, but fortunately the meeting hadn't started.

Naturally

MEANING: Happening or existing as part of nature and not made or done by people; If a particular skill comes naturally (to you), you are able to do it easily, with little effort or learning.

EXAMPLES:

  • A healthy body will be able to fight off the illness naturally without the use of medicine.

  • Most toxins are naturally excreted from the body.

  • She has a naturally pale complexion and dark hair.

  • The effects of the drug naturally wear off within a few hours.

  • Mental and physical deterioration both occur naturally with age.

  • Don't aim for success if you want it; just do what you love and believe in, and it will come naturally.

Obviously

MEANING: In a way that can be easily seen, recognized, or understood.

EXAMPLES:

  • Obviously the school cannot function without teachers.

  • She's obviously very intelligent, but her lectures are rough going.

  • You obviously didn't read the instructions properly.

  • There was obviously a great physical attraction between them.

  • You've obviously put a lot of work in your garden.

  • The report was obviously prepared with studious care and attention.

Presumably

MEANING: Used to say what you think is the likely situation.

EXAMPLES:

  • They can presumably afford to buy a bigger apartment.

  • Presumably he just forgot to send the letter.

  • Presumably they've decided to move back to Australia.

  • Presumably they can't afford a new car, or they'd have bought one by now.

  • Her mother will presumably stay with them until she's made a full recovery.

Undoubtedly

MEANING: Very likely; almost certainly; used to emphasize that something is true.

EXAMPLES:

  • Undoubtedly, stress has contributed to her health problems.

  • She is an artist whose work will undoubtedly withstand the test of time.

  • This is undoubtedly the best chocolate cake I've ever tasted.

  • He is undoubtedly England's best captain of recent times.

  • The highlight of the evening was undoubtedly the speech by the guest of honor.

Unfortunately

MEANING: Used to say that something is sad, disappointing, or has a bad effect.

EXAMPLES:

  • Unfortunately, I didn't have my credit card with me, or else I would have bought it.

  • Journalists may enter the danger zone but unfortunately we cannot guarantee their safety.

  • Unfortunately we can't come this evening after all.

  • Unfortunately there's nothing I can do about your problem.

  • We tried to get tickets, but unfortunately they were already sold out.

  • Unfortunately the plane has been delayed - the new departure time is 16.20.

Thankfully

MEANING: Used, usually at the beginning of a sentence, to show you are happy or grateful about something.

EXAMPLES:

  • Thankfully, you have a good friend that makes sure you get home safe, and drink some water and take ibuprofen.

  • Thankfully, the boy was taken to the hospital and his injuries were not believed to be life-threatening.

  • By the end of the 13th century, they were "antiquated curiosities" -- ones we can thankfully still enjoy today, through the magic of television.
