EXPRESSING EMPHASIS: OTHER MEANS TO EXPRESS EMPHASIS 

1. Time phrases, such as “day after day”, “day in, day out”, “time and time again”, “over and over again” etc.

day after day

MEANING: on each successive day over a long period.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • We see, therefore, that chimpanzees are capable of crying for extended periods of time, day after day.

  • All members of the expedition worked as long as daylight and the weather lasted, day after day until the operation was complete.

  • They stood around day after day not trying to put down an insurrection, but trying to start one.

  • Then came mineralogy and petrology and day after day we lifted and looked at lumps of mineral and lumps of rock and put them down again.

  • It also chases the unemployed day after day and week after week and is supposed to do so with fewer and fewer staff.

day in, day out

MEANING: repeatedly, all the time, incessantly, constantly

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • She wears the same jeans day in, day out. I’m starting to dislike them. 

  • My roommate works day in and day out so I never see him anymore.

  • I hate it but I go to the gym day in, day out to stay healthy.

  • This is your last warning. You need to be on time day in and day out from now on or you'll be fired.

  • My secretary is wonderful. She's cheerful and outgoing day in and day out and it makes the work environment so pleasant.

time and time again

MEANING: very ften

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • I’ve told you time and time again to lock both doors before you leave.

  • I've told you time and time again - make sure you look before you cross the road.

  • They may keep this up time and time again until, with luck, the predator finally gives up the chase.

  • Time and time again, women seem to lose out on promotions and career opportunities.

  • Their nature changes over time and requires couples to renegotiate their covenant with each other time and time again.

over and over again

MEANING: happening or done many times.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • I read the article over and over till it made sense.

  • Insanity is doing the same thing over and over again and expecting different results.

  • Over and over again in the past few weeks he has shown himself to be leading a rudderless, aimless Government.

  • In short, it would seem that the relationship must have evolved over and over again.

  • I have chosen the most costly problems, those that practitioners see over and over again.

in the nick of time

MEANING: This expression means that you arrive or finish something just before it is too late. At the last possible moment.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • My team scored in the nick of time. The game was in the last few seconds.

  • In the nick of time, they discovered her in a state of hapless stage fright.

  • She escaped from her smoke-filled home just in the nick of time.

  • I caught the baby in the nick of time before he fell down the stairs.

  • If you don't hear the milk boiling over, you won't dash to the rescue in the nick of time.

It’s high time

MEANING: This phrase can be used to say that it is time to do something that should have happened a long time ago.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • You’ve been struggling with your math homework for months. It’s high time we got you a tutor.

  • It’s high time you got that bad knee looked at by a doctor!

  • It's high time we made some changes around here.

  • It is high time for Europe to take responsibility for its own defense.

  • It is high time the Government displayed a more humanitarian approach towards victims of the recession.

  • Those boys betting on a high time began to grow ecstatic as the chair tipped back and then came forward.

2. Repeating the main verb: 

  • Of course, she looked and looked and looked, hoping it had landed on the ledge, but it just wasn’t there. 

3. Echoing phrases with “so” to express agreement: 

  • Policeman: This is the man who tried to rob you, is he? 

Girl: So he is!
