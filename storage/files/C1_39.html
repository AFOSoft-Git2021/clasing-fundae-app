The Senses

The five senses - sight, taste, touch, hearing and smell – collect information about our environment that are interpreted by the brain. 

We make sense of this information based on previous experience (and subsequent learning) and by the combination of the information from each of the senses.

Lumpy

MEANING: covered with or containing lumps; uneven and often crude in style.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The masher produces lumpy mashed potatoes, but some folks like them just fine that way.

  • The yards were constantly being churned up as new bodies were buried, and they got lumpy.

  • The skin on his back is speckled and lumpy from burns, but when I ask he laughs.

  • Sandra lay on the lumpy mattress, unable to sleep.

Lisp

MEANING: If someone speaks with a lisp, they pronounce "s" and "z" sounds like "th."

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • I was teased a lot when I was in school because I spoke with a lisp.

  • He has a slight lisp and is conscious of it.

  • He hopes the expensive makeover will cure his lisp.

  • I was born deaf in one ear, but my school never picked up on it and I developed a really bad lisp.

  • "I say!" he shouted, forgetting his lisp in his excitement.

Savor

MEANING: to enjoy food or an experience slowly, in order to enjoy it as much as possible; a smell or taste, especially a pleasant one.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • It was the first chocolate he'd tasted for over a year, so he savored every mouthful.

  • Love the fact that you are alive and savor everything that life has to offer.

  • Let us rediscover the flavors and savors of regional cooking.

  • He wanted to savor his time with Henrietta and their grown children.

Shimmer

MEANING: to shine in such a way that the light seems to shake slightly and quickly.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • He shimmers in and out of rooms and may appear or disappear suddenly and without warning.

  • The challenge is to make inanimate objects shimmer and glow with vibrancy and life.

  • Soft light begins to shimmer on the still water.

Deodorize

MEANING: to remove odors (= smells, especially unpleasant ones) from something.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • Baking soda has many uses, from cleaning tiles to deodorizing fridges.

  • Deodorize your dishwasher by sprinkling 1/2 cup soda on the bottom between loads.

  • The automatic toilet cleaner not only cleans and deodorizes, it kills germs.

  • I need to shower, deodorize, and change into fresh clothes.

  • Use lemon juice to deodorize your skin after handling foods with strong odors.

  • Olive oil soap will gently clean and deodorize your hands in the kitchen.

  • These solvents are odorless or deodorized.

  • They spend nearly an hour after work scrubbing and deodorizing.

  • I am showered, cleaned, shaved, and deodorized, wearing a pressed shirt and shiny shoes.

Tangy

MEANING: A tangy flavor is pleasantly strong and sharp.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • Chopped walnuts lend flavor and crunch, and dried cherries bring on their own tangy-sweet jazz.

  • This recipe is unabashedly cheesy, tangy with feta and snappy with fresh dill.

  • A few drops of tangy lemon oil in the bath will freshen up a hot and clammy patient.

  • I loved the smell of cork grease and slide oil, of musty woolen uniforms, and the tangy dankness of brassy horn bells.

  • The tea goes with this tangy lemon tart perfectly.

Drab

MEANING: boring, especially in appearance; having little color and excitement.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • She walked through the city center with its drab, gray buildings and felt depressed.

  • I feel so drab in this gray uniform.

  • He found London disagreeable, with its thick fog, cold drizzle, and drab food.

  • Her personality was drab, earnest and humorless and her appearance calculatedly unattractive.

  • The red stems of this bush provide a contrast to the drab brown of the rest of the winter garden.

Incense

MEANING: a substance that is burnt to produce a sweet smell, especially as part of a religious ceremony.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • Let's burn incense in the memory of our pure past.

  • The smoke of an incense coil wisped at the corner of the bedroom.

  • There was an unmistakable smell of incense in the air.

  • Blake thought the burning incense could have been a hallucinogenic drug which was altering his perception.

Dusty

MEANING: dry dirt in the form of powder that covers surfaces inside a building, or very small dry pieces of soil, sand, or other substances.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The furniture was covered in dust and cobwebs.

  • A cloud of dust rose in the air as the car roared past.

  • These china ornaments just collect dust.

  • He hit the ground with his stick and a puff of dust rose up into the air.

  • The truck went by in a swirl of dust.

  • I blew the dust off the books.

  • Erupting volcanoes discharge massive quantities of dust into the stratosphere.

Blurred

MEANING: something that you cannot see clearly; to (make something or someone) become difficult to see clearly.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • If I don't wear my glasses, everything is just a blur.

  • It all happened so long ago that it's just a blur to me now.

  • The last few days seem to have gone by in a blur.

  • As she drifted into sleep, the doctor's face began to blur and fade.

Tart

MEANING: (especially of fruit) tasting sour or acidic.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • You might need some sugar on the rhubarb - it's a little tart.

  • The blackberries were a bit too tart on their own, so we stewed them gently with some apples.

  • The cake had a tart, lemony flavor.

Clink

MEANING: a short ringing sound like pieces of glass or metal knocking lightly together.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • I could hear the clink of coins in his pocket.

  • The clink of dishes in the dining room told him that dinner would soon be ready.

  • She walked into the bathroom and Rachaela heard the clink of the pot of cold cream.

Downy

MEANING: filled with feathers; covered with soft thin hair.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The entire leaf is covered in fine, downy hair, keeping it water-repellent.

  • The downy little bird nestled comfortably to its mother.

  • My coat is filled with downy feathers.

Grainy

MEANING: (of a picture) appearing to be made up of a lot of spots.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • Walls are covered with grainy blow-ups of sleek-jawed Latin athletes.

  • The grainy sand had gone from under his feet.

  • Our product line contains platinum, grainy series.

  • Dirt encrusted on the bus window gave a grainy texture to the scenery.

Mumble

MEANING: to speak quietly and in a way that is not clear so that the words are difficult to understand.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • She mumbled something about needing to be home, then left.

  • He often mumbles, and I never know what he’s asking me.

  • Whenever the subject comes up, officials tend to mumble about uncertainties.

  • I have heard people who read badly mumble to themselves.

  • All I could do was mumble that I regretted that I hadn't taken my degree.

Palatable

MEANING: Palatable food or drink has a pleasant taste; good enough to eat or drink.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • They could make powdered eggs into palatable omelets.

  • Barley straw is soft and palatable, and is widely used as bulk feed for beef cattle.

  • Dietary fiber is provided by a wide selection of easily available and palatable foods.

Putrid

MEANING: decayed and having an unpleasant smell; very unpleasant or ugly.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The number of cattle which die or become ill as a result of drinking impure or putrid water is not known.

  • He lay stinking in the putrid morning sun, trousers pulled down around his buttocks.

  • The room was airless and putrid and stifling hot, the window lavender with either twilight or dawn.

  • When the wind was wrong, the putrid stench was wafted up across the fields.

Reek

MEANING: to have a strong unpleasant smell.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The room was filled with the reek of stale beer.

  • The warm air was scented with the heady reek of diesel oil from the bus.

  • The reek from the dead blue whale is strong enough to churn the stomach.

  • Her nose was immediately assaulted by the reek of smoke.

  • There was a smell of fish, and a bad, sour reek coming from the overcrowded houses.

Tarnished 

MEANING: If metal is tarnished it has become less bright or a different color.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The photographs were in tarnished silver frames.

  • People trade in their tarnished gold chains, old wedding bands or gold-filled teeth.

  • She had rinsed out the tub, and lowered the big and tarnished metal plug into the hole.

  • Medallions were tarnished and corroded.

Twitter

MEANING: to talk quickly and nervously in a high voice, saying very little of importance or interest; (of a bird) to make a series of short, high sounds.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • I was woken up by a bird twittering just outside my window.

  • The only sounds were the birds twittering and chirping in the trees.

  • She comes in here when I'm trying to work and just twitters on about nothing.

  • She played me the tape-recorded twitter of a warbler.

  • The twitters and chirps from the treetops grew louder.

Glitter

MEANING: to produce a lot of small, bright flashes of reflected light;

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • Her diamond necklace glittered brilliantly under the spotlights.

  • She was blinded by the glitter and the glamor of her own life.

  • The sea-wall's ribbed stones glitter like light-houses.

  • Gladys is wearing a black sleeveless shirt with something written in silver glitter script across the front.

Slippery

MEANING: If something is slippery, it is wet or smooth so that it slides easily or causes something to slide.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The sidewalks were slippery with ice.

  • He struggled to keep his footing on the slippery floor.

  • Wheels won't bite on a slippery surface.

  • It's slippery, so mind where you're walking.

  • We had to be mindful of every step we took on the slippery sidewalk.

Scent

MEANING: a pleasant natural smell.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The scent of lilacs permeated the air.

  • The dogs were onto the lost boy’s scent.

  • The spring evening was warm, and the scent of flowers filled the air.

  • Flowers without scent produce fewer seeds, although they are visited as often by pollinators as are flowers that do emit a scent.
