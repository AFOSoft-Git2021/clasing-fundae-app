Vocabulary for Restaurant

Places to eat out

PLACES MEANING SENTENCES
Restaurant (noun): a place with a kitchen and dining area where people pay to eat meals There are lots of Thai and Japanese restaurants around here.
Cafe / Coffee Shop (noun): an informal restaurant serving simple meals plus hot and cold drinks Is there an all-night café around here?
cafeteria / canteen a place that sells low-cost meals in a school or work-place, usually from a self-service counter What's the food in the school cafeteria like?
Snack Bar a small, informal restaurant where small meals can be eaten or bought to take away Before the film, I bought some popcorn and a drink at the snack bar.
Fast Food Restaurant (noun): quickly prepared food like hamburgers and French fries I'll get some fast food on the way home.
Pub a place, especially in Great Britain or Ireland, where alcoholic drinks can be bought and drunk and where food is often available Do you want to go to the pub after work?
Bistro a small informal restaurant or bar, especially one in France or one in a French style Currently, a new mural is being developed near the school bistro.

Sentences you can use when booking a Table

I'd like to reserve / book a table for four at 8pm, please.

I'd like to reserve / book a table for a party of six at 8pm, please.

I'd like to book a table for two at 8 in the name of Hand, please.

Could we have a table by the window, please?

Could we have a non-smoking table, please?

Could we have a table away from the kitchen/toilets, please?

Could we have a booth, please?

Could you make sure it's a quiet table, please

Sentences you can use when arriving at the Restaurant.

Good evening, the name is Hand. I have a table booked for six.

Do you have the menu in English/German/French ..., please?

Do you have a high chair for young children, please?

Could we have a table over there, please?

I'm sorry but I asked for a table by the window.

Could we have an extra chair, please?

Sentences you can use when placing your order.

I'd like the .............., please.

For starters I'll have the soup and for the main course I'd like the roast beef.

Could I have chips instead of new potatoes, please?

What is the house special today?

Is there anything you would recommend?

Could I see the wine menu, please?

I'll have a bottle of the South African Cabernet Sauvignon.

I'll have a glass of house red/white, please.

Which wine would you recommend?

Sentences you can use when during/after the meal.

Could we have some more bread, please?

Do you have a pepper mill?

Could I have some dressing, please?

Could you pass me the salt, please?

That was delicious. My compliments to the chef.

Sentences you can use when complaining about something.

Excuse me, but my meal is cold.

Excuse me, we've been waiting for over half an hour for our drinks.

I'm sorry but I ordered the side salad not the vegetables.

Excuse me this steak is over done, I ordered rare.

I'm afraid this wine tastes corked.

Excuse me this wine isn't chilled properly.

Sentences you can use when paying the bill.

Could I have the bill, please?

Do you take Visa?

We'd like separate bills, please.

Is service included?

No, please. This is on me. (When you wish to pay for everyone.)

Restaurant Vocabulary:

VOCABULARY MEANING SENTENCES
à la carte ordered as a separate dish; not part of a set menu Do you have à la carte dishes as well as set menus?
appetizer a dish that's eaten before the main course For an appetizer I'll have spring rolls, please.
bill (also US "check") the written amount you must pay for a meal and drinks Can we have the bill, please?
course one part of a meal French meals usually include three or four courses.
cuisine a country or region's style of cooking There's more to Indian cuisine than curries, you know.
dessert sweet food eaten at the end of a meal You love desserts, don't you?
eat out (phrasal verb): to go out to a restaurant, esp. at night We usually eat out three or four nights a week.
entrée is the main course, or sometimes a dish before the main course What are you having for your entrée?
menu the list of foods and drinks served in a restaurant, café, hotel, etc. Let's check the menu before deciding whether to eat here.
cutlery (also silverware) knives, forks, and spoons used for eating We only use our best cutlery on special occasions.

Eating and Drinking Vocabulary:

VOCABULARY MEANING SENTENCES
Nibble Eat slowly and gently with small bites. She nibbled some cheese and crackers before lunch
Graze You will find this word in eating and drinking vocabulary very common that expresses to eat small portion of the food throughout the whole day. Jam was grazing on chips all noon
Bite To hold and cut some piece of food with teeth directly to eat. You can use this word for such food items that can be hold in hand and keep enough size to put in mouth to cut it with front teeth like pizza, sandwich, cookie, cake, etc. She gulped the pizza in few bites
Munch To chew something in a noisy way She is munching snacks.
Starving suffering or dying from hunger. Our government is working to feed starving children in poor regions.
Gulp To eat and swallow some food very quickly and in a large quantity. This word is commonly used in eating and drinking vocabulary. Zaki told him not to gulp his sandwich.
Swallow To take food into stomach through mouth Always chew your food well before swallowing it.
Chew To cut food into pieces using teeth before swallowing it It looks very bad to chew food with open mouth.
Lick to take some food or liquid into mouth with the help of tongue, to touch a tongue on something to get its taste She licked her fingers after taking meal with hands.
Guzzle to drink some liquid such as bear or ale very quickly and in a large amount, to drink greedily She guzzled three glass of soda after lunch.
Choke to become unable to breath freely when something like food gets stuck in throat that cause stoppage in breathing Guzzling a glass of liquor continually can cause choking.
Vomit The liquid or food that comes out from stomach through mouth due to sickness or some other reason. Blood vomiting caused her death.
Munch To chew something in a noisy way, to chew food that causes noise She was munching cookies during class time.
Eat like a bird to eat very little, to eat a small portion of food She eats like a bird in school parties.
Eat like a horse to eat a lot He ate like a horse on my wedding party.

Food Vocabulary:

VOCABULARY MEANING SENTENCES
aroma a nice smell, especially from food, wine, coffee, etc. I love the aroma of freshly-baked bread.
bake to cook in an oven Have you ever tried baking a cake?
bland having little taste; tasteless Most people think British food is bland.
dairy product a food made from milk, like butter, cheese, yoghurt, etc Dairy products are becoming more popular in Asia.
delicious tasting very good The food in this restaurant is really delicious.
dessert sweet food eaten at the end of a meal Have you ever tried Middle-eastern desserts like baklava?
grain seeds used as food like wheat, rice, lentils, etc. rains like wheat and rye are used to make different kinds of bread.
ingredients all the foods used to make a dish or meal What ingredients do we need to make spaghetti sauce?
junk food foods and food products that are unhealthy because of all the fat, salt or sugar they contain People who love junk food soon get fat and unhealthy.
poultry Birds that people eat, like chickens, ducks, geese, etc Factory farms keep poultry in tiny cages and the birds never see the outside world.
recipe instructions for cooking a dish or a meal My mum has a great recipe for chocolate pudding.
seafood anything from the sea that can be eaten If you eat vegetarian food plus fish and seafood, but not meat or poultry, you're a pescatarian/pescetarian.
