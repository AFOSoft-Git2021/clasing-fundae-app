Will & Shall

Will

The most basic and common form used to express the future is ‘will’. The structure of ‘will’ is easy because it is the same for all subjects and you don’t need to change anything about the verb.

SINGULAR PLURAL
I will be (I'll be) we will be (we'll be)
you will be (you'll be) you will be (you'll be)
he/she/it will be (he/she/it'll be) they will be (they'll be)

To make the negative form we add ‘not’:

SINGULAR PLURAL
I will not be (I won't be) we will not be (we won't be)
you will not be (you won't be) you will not be (you won't be)
he/she/it will not be (he/she/it won't be) they will not be (they won't be)

To make the question form we invert the subject and ‘will’:

SINGULAR PLURAL
Will I be? Will we be?
Will you be? Will you be?
Will he/she/it be? Will they be?

We use ‘will’ for the following situations:

a) to describe the future

  • We’ll be at the hotel until 8pm.
  • The trains will be late today because of the bad weather.
  • How long will you stay in Hong Kong?
  • He’ll send us the report when he has all the data.

b) to make a prediction

  • The world population will grow a lot in the next 50 years.
  • I think I’ll stay in this company until I retire.
  • She doesn’t think she’ll pass the exam.
  • Who do you think will win the tournament?

c) to express a decision made at the moment of speaking

  • I’ll have the vegetable soup please.
  • (The phone rings) I’ll answer it.
  • So that’s the plan – we’ll check the sales figures and you check the expenses.
  • Jean’s over there. I’ll just go and say ‘hello’ before we leave.

d) to make a request

  • Will you bring some more water please?
  • Will you come to the appointment with me?
  • Will you be quiet please? I’m trying to make an important phone call.
  • You’ll give me a hand with dinner, won’t you?

e) to make promises and offers

  • I’ll call you when I get home.
  • She’ll help you finish the work, don’t worry.
  • We’ll send you the goods as soon as possible.
  • Lee and Kim will show you around the city.

f) to describe the consequence of a conditional phrase:

  • If it rains, I’ll take my umbrella,
  • If they win the next match, they’ll go through to the final.
  • Will she come to dinner if I invite her?
  • We won’t mind if you arrive late.

Shall

In the past, ‘shall’ was often used as an alternative to ‘will’, and you can find many examples of ‘shall’ in famous literary works. But in modern English we usually prefer ‘will’ for affirmative and negative sentences.

We form sentences with ‘shall’ in the same way as we do with ‘will’.

SINGULAR PLURAL
I shall help We shall help
You shall help You shall help
he/she/it shall help They shall help

The negative form is created by adding ‘not’. The contraction form, ‘shan’t’, is something we generally use in spoken English but rarely in its written form.

SINGULAR PLURAL
I shall not help (I shan’t help) We shall not help (we shan’t help)
You shall not help (you shan’t help) You shall not help (you shan’t help)
he/she/it shall not help (he/she/it shan’t help) They shall not help (they shan’t help)

We make the question by inverting the subject and ‘shall’. Here is the question form for all the subjects, though we only normally use ‘shall’ in questions with I and we:

SINGULAR PLURAL
Shall I help? Shall we help?
Shalp you help? Shall we help?
Shall he/she/it help? Shall they help?

We use ‘shall’ in the following ways:

a) to make offers using I/we

  • Shall I make some lunch?
  • Shall we help you with your bags?
  • Where shall I drop you off? Outside your office?
  • Shall I water your plants while you’re away?

b) to make suggestions using I/we

  • Shall we go to the park later?
  • Shall we have something to eat?
  • Who shall we invite to the party?
  • What shall we do after we’ve watched the film?

c) to express formal obligations

  • Applicants shall provide evidence of their qualifications.
  • Guests shall not remove anything from the rooms.
  • The tenant shall pay the agreed amount on the first day of every month.
  • The accused shall appear in court again on 26th October.

d) to make a promise

  • I shall never forget the help you gave me.
  • I shan’t be late.
  • We shall do everything we can to solve the problem.
  • He shan’t be long. Do have a seat while you wait.

e) to describe the future very formally

  • We shall overcome this difficult moment.
  • The tests shall be carried out by an independent body.
  • Mr Lopez shall begin his talk at 2:30pm.
  • Access to the city center shall be limited today due to the marathon.

Sentences of Will

Affirmative

  • It is evident that she has been at fault and will be severely punished.
  • You will be called upon to work long hours.
  • You will never succeed unless you persevere.
  • Our team will put in a lot of effort.
  • I am sure that the work we produce will be of high quality and meet all deadlines.
  • I will clean the house tomorrow.
  • You will sleep during the movie.
  • We will go to Paris this year.
  • He will finish it before you come back.
  • They will come in at 9pm.
  • The kids will study after dinner.
  • Mom will stay overnight at the hotel.
  • They will get a pay rise next month.
  • She will be home soon.
  • You will write the report after lunch.
  • He will give you his car if you ask him.
  • You will be a designer one day.
  • We were there yesterday, and we will be there tomorrow too!
  • I will buy a new car next month.
  • He will get lost on the way home.
  • She will see you now, George.
  • What time is it in Japan? Japan time will be 6 hours ahead of us (6 hours west).
  • You will like it.
  • She will get an A+ on the test, for sure.
  • There will be enough money to do what I need to do next month.

Negative Sentences of Will

  • They will not come home tonight.
  • He will not be able to join us on vacation.
  • She will not take care of it.
  • My wife said that she will not give me any more money for clothes.
  • I will not use public transport.
  • You are very friendly, but I will not depend on you.
  • They will not come when they can.
  • We will not use the old table in the new apartment.
  • The car will not pass the test.
  • I will not exercise today.
  • She will not quit her job.
  • They will not send their son to art school.

Interrogative Sentences of Will

  • Will you advise me a little on that matter?
  • What will the weather be like today?
  • Will everybody be discussing it?
  • Will I know where to turn to for help?
  • Will you be ready by then?
  • Will she go to the movie?
  • Will he cook meals every night?
  • Will they be at the meeting on time?

Sentences of Shall

Affirmative

  • You shall clean my car and I will go on vacation.
  • He shall do his homework now.
  • We shall be watching TV now.
  • They shall get into trouble one day.
  • She says that she shall be there at 8 o’clock sharp.
  • She shall bring the files to work on Wednesday.
  • You shall catch up on all your work today.
  • You shall please your teachers.
  • We shall reduce costs.
  • They shall travel to Berlin.
  • David shall do the exercises.
  • You shall be a good boy and study hard.
  • Few boys shall refuse a dare from their friends.
  • We shall live in a small suburb.
  • You shall never leave!
  • They shall be punished.
  • You shall do this for me, or you shall be punished too.
  • They shall not pass!
  • We shall be looking out for it.
  • You shall make it work.
  • She shall understand the situation.
  • A day later I shall have met my deadline.
  • You shall go to the ball.
  • I shall feed the dog.
  • I shall water the flowers later.
  • I shall be eligible for the prize.
  • It shall rain in the morning.
  • You shall come to classes early.
  • I shall call at five o’clock.
  • They shall finish within a week.
  • This sauce shall be delicious.
  • We shall meet next month if we have time.
  • She shall soon solve the problem.

Negative Sentences of Shall

  • You all have to sign this petition since you shall be living in our city.
  • I shall not eat it, OK?
  • You shall not do that.
  • You shall not fail in this task.
  • You shall not force your opinion on me!
  • You shall not go to the ball
  • I shan’t be home tomorrow night.
  • We shan’t know the result of the tests until Tuesday.
  • Thou shall not give into temptation.
  • We shall not consider this topic here except to make the following point.

Interrogative Sentences of Shall

  • Shall we make it on time?
  • Shall people use this new tool?
  • Shall we see her tomorrow?
  • Shall we be rewarded for honesty?
  • Shall you meet the required standards?
