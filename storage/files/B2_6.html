Sentence Connectors to give another type of

This type of connector is used to give information against general expectations.

All the same

Meaning: In spite of this; nevertheless.

Sample Sentences: 

  • She knew they had meant it kindly, but it had hurt all the same.
  • All the same, there is some truth in what he says.
  • The new machine has all the same functions, but is only half as large.

Still 

Meaning: To an even greater degree or in an even amount.

Sample Sentences: 

  • The number of people killed in the explosion is likely to rise still .
  • The company is hoping to extend its market still further.
  • Still more snow fell overnight.
  • I'll meet you at the theater. No, better still, let's meet for a drink first.
  • I'm worried that his car has broken down, or worse still, that he's had an accident.

Even so

Meaning: Although it is true

Sample Sentences: 

  • The phone system has improved, but even so, there are still very few houses with phones.
  • There are a lot of spelling mistakes; even so, it's quite a good essay.
  • The bus was only half full. Even so, a young man asked Nina if the seat next to her was taken.

Regardless 

Meaning: Despite; not being affected by something:

Sample Sentences: 

  • The plan for a new office building went ahead regardless of local opposition.
  • She knew it was dangerous to visit him except at night, but she set out regardless of the risk.
  • This job is open to all, regardless of previous experience.

MORE SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • All the children are lumped together in one class, regardless of their ability.
  • He could see that she didn't like what he was saying, but he plowed on regardless.
  • It was pouring with rain, but we pressed on regardless.
  • Regardless of what you think, I believe she's the best person for the job.
  • Regardless of cost, the building must be completed on time.
  • The new method is not perfect; even so, it's much better than the old one.
  • I know he's only a child, but even so he should have known that what he was doing was wrong.
  • They are still present.
  • The Greeks are still powerful players in world shipping.
  • Do you still live at the same address?
  • Cynthia's still working on her thesis.
  • Despite my repeated assurances, Rob still looked very nervous.
  • I'd still like to go to a ball game.
  • Whether they win or lose is all the same to me.
  • The plots of his books are basically all the same.
