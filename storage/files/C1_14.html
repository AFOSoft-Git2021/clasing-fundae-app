PASSIVE PERFECT INFINITIVE

With modals and with infinitive forms, the passive is formed with be + past participle or have been + past participle.  

With future forms it is constructed with be + past participle or being + past participle.

The perfect infinitive is often used after the modal auxiliary verbs could, would, might, ought, should and needn't to talk about unreal situations

EXAMPLES

  • They can be given money.

  • She should be picked up at 6:00 by her father.

  • Attention must be paid in class. 

  • Books can be found on the shelves.

  • If I had known the future, I would have done it differently.

  • He could have taken the flight.

  • We may have passed the math exam, but it was in Spanish.

  • You might have sold the car., if you really need the Money.

  • You should have listened to the teacher.

  • We must have been crazy!

  • A test will be given to you on Wednesday.

  • Some flights might be delayed over the Christmas break. 

  • His lunch must have been eaten. 

  • An exam may be given by our English teacher today.

  • Many books have been written by Thomas.

  • Some information can be given about the job by Juan.

  • The rules may be forgotten quickly

  • The lessons should be studied repeatedly

  • The competition must be won by Benjamin

  • The game should be canceled

  • She can't be persuaded by the doctor

  • My car needs to be repaired

  • By whom should the damage be paid

  • My dog was supposed to have been taken to a friend’s house.

  • My shoes should have been repaired last week.

  • If I had guessed the future, I would have taken some precautions against what would happen.

  • The email has been written by the technical support.

  • The work must be finished within two hours.

  • The meeting should be canceled.

  • The changes might have been predicted.

  • My car could have been sold for five thousand pounds.
