Have & Has

Have is a verb that means to possess something or to do something.

Have is used with the pronouns: I, you, we, they and pluralized nouns.

*speaking in the first person (I, we)

*speaking in the second person (you)

*speaking in the third person plural (they)

Examples:

I have a pencil.

We have a big house.

Doctors have a rough time, dealing with illnesses all the time.

Do you have any money?

We have a new ride at the amusement park.

The children have gone to play in the park.

It is good to have friends in trouble.

You can’t eat your cake and have it.

We have waited for hours in this line.

You have spinach in your teeth.

I don’t have time for this.

You have written a beautiful book.

My parents have owned their boat since I was born.

The doctor recommended that I should have massages every week on my back.

I used to have terrible dreams when I was young.

They have moved house.

We have done a lot so far.

HAS

Is also a verb that means the subject has done something at some point in their life.

Has is used with the third person singular (he, she, it).

She has dark hair and brown eyes.

Japan has a population of over 120 million.

The boom has created job opportunities.

She has made a television documentary on poverty in our cities.

She has a car that we can borrow.

The field has been seeded with corn.

Every family has a skeleton in the cupboard.

The longest day has an end.

My sister has a new job

My mother has a new car.

The school has needed repairs ever since last year’s flood.

She has known about the accident since she saw the news last night.

The car has power brakes.

My father has driven a school bus for three years.

When to Use Has

*Use the verb has with he, she, and it in the present tense.

SENTENCE

 EXPLAINATION
James has three cats.

 This sentence is correct because has is the proper form of the verb 'to have' since being used alongside 'James' (he).

My sister has a three-car garage.

This sentence is correct because has is the proper form of the verb when used alongside 'sister' (she).

This car has a powerful A/C unit.

This sentence is correct because has is the proper form since it is used alongside 'car' (it).

When to Use Has

Have is the second form of the verb 'to have' in the present tense

SENTENCE EXPLAINATION
I have two nephews. This sentence is correct because have is the proper form of the verb 'to have' since being used alongside I.
In our house, we have two bedrooms and one bath. This sentence is correct because have is the proper form of the verb since being used alongside we.
You have an extensive collection of books in your library. This sentence is correct because have is being used alongside you.
My neighbors have a three-car garage. This sentence is correct since have is the proper form of the verb 'to have' and is being used alongside 'neighbor' (they).
