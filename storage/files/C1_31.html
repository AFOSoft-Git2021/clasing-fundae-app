Descriptive Adjectives Money Related

Rich Man

MEANING: a man who is wealthy 

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Economy the poor man’s mints; extravagance the rich man’s pitfall.

  • The rich man lives in luxurious surroundings.

  • The rich man was asked to pay a high ransom for his daughter who was taken away by criminals.

  • In ten years, he was editor and publisher of the Tribune, and a rich man.

Wealthy

MEANING: a large amount of money or valuable possessions that someone has.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Wealthy people often migrate in winter to warmer sunnier countries.

  • Early to bed and early to rise, makes a man healthy, wealthy and wise.

  • The couple are said to be fabulously wealthy.

  • We should levy a heavy tax on the wealthy men.

Rich 

MEANING: Rich people considered together as a group.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The resort is frequented by the rich and famous.

  • The island has a rich and complex history

  • All of the world's richest nations were invited to attend the summit.

  • We're not rich but we try to give to charity as often as we can.

Prosperous 

MEANING: Successful, usually by earning a lot of money.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • In a prosperous country like this, no one should go hungry.

  • Understanding the principles of economic freedom and their importance in building more prosperous societies is a key lesson for students around the world.

  • The country was on the verge of becoming prosperous and successful.

  • My husband joins me in hoping that you have a long and happy and prosperous life together.

  • Dirty streets and homelessness are no advertisement for a prosperous society.

Affluent 

MEANING: Having a lot of money or owning a lot of things.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:  

  • We live in an affluent neighborhood.

  • The store will benefit from its base of affluent customers to whom various other products will be sold in due course.

  • Some have argued that ethical food is a sign of an affluent society with a lot of time.

  • Affluent people tend to look upon illiteracy with comfortable detachment.

Well off 

MEANING: rich

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • It is a resort that clearly caters for the well-off.

  • My old customers were well-off, middle-aged people.

  • Lawren was therefore well off and able to indulge in his favorite pastime of painting.

  • Basque is so well off that it can worry about esoteric things as far as the language is concerned.

  • Finance firms are concentrating on the well-off.

  • Housing trusts help to provide houses for people who are not well off.

Filthy rich

MEANING: extremely rich

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The profession is dominated by filthy rich white men.

  • His family got filthy rich when oil prices rose.

  • Shareholders are suing the company's filthy rich chief executive.

  • Most of her clients are well-off but not filthy rich.

  • Most of us are working really hard in order to make someone else filthy rich.

  • They got filthily rich by exploiting and underpaying their workers.

Stinking rich

MEANING: To be extremely rich.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • He's stinking rich, and with no more talent than he ever had before.

  • Another example of modern capitalism securing a redistribution of wealth from the not very well off to the stinking rich.

Poor Man

MEANING: Used to refer to something that is a worse or cheaper version of something else that is mentioned.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The poor man would be better off wrestling with the complexities of Bernie Ecclestone's financial affairs.

  • I would rather be a poor man in a garret with plenty of good books to read than a king who did not love reading. 

  • The poor man is not the man without a cent, but the man without a dream.

  • It grieved me to see the poor man in such distress.

Hard-up 

MEANING: Having very little money.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • We're a bit hard up at the moment so we're not thinking about vacations.

  • It is not only pensioners in villages who are hard up.

  • Three hard-up governments are preparing to sell shares in state-owned assets in the next two weeks.

  • The owner of a large concern may well feel an obligation to find a position in his firm for a hard-up cousin.

  • Hard-up families in the stockbroker belt are begging state schools to bail them out as the recession bites deeper.

Needy 

MEANING: poor and not having enough food, clothes, etc.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:  

  • The proceeds from the sale go to help needy people in the area.

  • Let us pray for those who are not so fortunate as ourselves - the sick, the old, and the needy.

  • Filling yourself with love so that you are not needy for another's time and attention.

  • You don't want to seem needy, but you're feeling more and more neglected and sometimes ignored completely.

Skint 

MEANING: Having no money.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • I get paid each Friday, and by Tuesday I'm always skint.

  • Quite often I find myself skint at the end of the month.

  • It was just after Christmas when everyone was totally skint.

Penniless

MEANING: Having no money.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • She fell in love with a penniless artist.

  • A series of bad investments left him almost penniless.

  • The pain worsens, but their increasingly frantic appeals to penniless relatives, hospital bosses and charities fall on deaf ears.

  • It was made by a handful of arduous, dedicated friends under the guidance of a penniless, uniquely untalented visionary.

Broke

MEANING: Without money.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • I can't afford to go on vacation this year - I'm broke.

  • Some of the merchants say they are unable to cut their expenses and are on the verge of going broke.

  • He turned up at my house yesterday, flat broke and hungry.

  • I finally paid all my bills, so now I am flat broke.

Impoverished 

MEANING: Extremely poor

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Poverty rates rose near new train stations, not because the stations impoverished local residents, but because public transit was attracting the poor.

  • The idea behind putting you in an eye chart and you read letters is really impoverished.

  • One day, he woke up and realized the error of his ways, immediately joining up with the impoverished.

Poverty stricken 

MEANING: A poverty-stricken person or place is suffering from the effects of being extremely poor.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Some beggars are neither poverty-stricken nor homeless.

  • There are few jobs for the peasants who have flooded into the cities from the poverty-stricken countryside in search of work.

  • My grandparents arrived in this country as poverty-stricken immigrants.

  • The complex was intended to be a modern, vibrant community to replace the old poverty-stricken neighborhoods of the poor.

Destitute 

MEANING: Without money, food, a home, or possessions.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The floods left thousands of people destitute.

  • These groups gathered clothing, schoolbooks, and medical supplies for the destitute flood victims.

  • During the global economic crisis many people were left destitute.

  • Poverty and destitution are widespread in the country.

Generous

MEANING: Larger than usual or expected.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • He was an incredibly generous and giving man, always helping out with theater productions at his kids' school, building sets, doing whatever.

  • I have several close male friends who are wise, kind and generous.

  • We will continue to invest in a generous rewards budget, but there will no longer be a predetermined targeted distribution.

  • The library has received a generous request from a local businessman.

  • Obviously he had plenty of money and was generous in its use without being ostentatious.

Charitable 

MEANING: Giving money, food, or help free to those who are in need because they are sick, poor, or have no home.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The entire organization is funded by charitable donations.

  • Some critics said the show was good in parts - those less charitable said the whole thing was a disaster.

  • The firm's family owners decided to create a charitable foundation.

  • The organization has been granted charitable status.

Unselfish

MEANING: An unselfish person thinks about what is good for other people, not just about their own advantage.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • We delude ourselves into thinking we are good, moral, unselfish people, but we are lying to ourselves for the benefit of others.

  • Unselfish and intelligent, he plays like a 10-year vet with his spate of passes, deflections and overall maturity.

  • He already understands basketball and knows what to do, and he's an unselfish player.

  • Whenever your child is unselfish, helpful or sensitive to how others feel[sentencedict.com], reward her with words of praise and encouragement.

Mean

MEANING : To have a particular result; to have an important emotional effect on someone

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • It wasn't a valuable picture but it meant a lot to me.

  • Possessions mean nothing to him.

  • Nothing means more to me than my children's happiness.

  • He's always making flattering remarks, but he doesn't really mean them.

Miserly

MEANING:   Of or characteristic of a miser.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • His miserly great-uncle proved to be worth nearly $1 million.

  • Being a student today with miserly grants and limited career prospects is difficult.

  • Too many customers are alienated by our miserly attitude to compensation for mistakes that we made.

  • Lying on his deathbed the rich, miserly old man calls to his long suffering wife.

  • Old Crumwallis won't notice I've had more than his miserly allowance.

Selfish

MEANING: Caring only about what you want or need without any thought for the needs or wishes of other people.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • It would be a bit selfish to keep them all for myself.

  • Susan and Deborah share an intensely selfish, egotistic streak.

  • Money is not the root of all evils as is usually claimed, what is the root of all evils is the lust for money, that is the excessive, selfish and greedy pursuit of money.

  • She thinks that young people today are becoming increasingly selfish, and worship at the shrine of individual happiness and gratification.

Stingy

MEANING: Unwilling to spend money; small in amount.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • He's really stingy and never buys anyone a drink when we go out.

  • The landlords are so stingy - they refused to pay for new carpets.

  • The stingy funding meant that some middle-class people wouldn't get much financial help, despite high premiums.

  • The company is famously stingy about giving out sales and financial figures.

  • Stingy people are generally associated with misery and anger.

Tight

MEANING: If time or money is tight, there is only just enough of it; Tight controls or rules severely limit what can happen; Clothes or shoes that are tight fit the body too closely and are uncomfortable.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • That jacket is too tight - you need a bigger size.

  • Some people experienced tight chests after exercising in areas of high pollution.

  • If you have any symptoms such as itchy mouth or tight throat after eating nuts, stop.

  • Rules on what can be taken on board an aircraft have become tighter.

  • I'm sorry I can't stay for a chat, time's really tight.

  • They're raising three kids on one small salary so money is very tight.

Uncharitable

MEANING: unkind and unfair

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The uncharitable explanation is that she's too afraid to ask.

  • She would take back many of the unkind and uncharitable things she had said about the country since she had lost the trail.

  • Uncharitable persons said that he dealt more poker.

  • He was merciful to everyone but the unmerciful, and charitable to every one but the uncharitable, and kind to everyone but the unkind.

Ungenerous

MEANING: Not willing to give money, help, kindness, praise, etc. to other people, or only willing to give a little.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • It is not being ungenerous to the team to suggest that it, too, has many weak points.

  • The account pays an ungenerous 0.5% rate of interest.

  • He clearly had a passion for music, so why was he such an ungenerous critic?

  • There is a saying that a rich man who is ungenerous is like a tree without fruit.

  • The article attempts to explain why we have in recent years become so ungenerous to migrants and refugees.
