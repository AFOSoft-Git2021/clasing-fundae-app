Prepositions of Place: In, On, At

Generally, we use at, in and on when we talk about the location of things.

at - for a POINT

EXAMPLES:

She's waiting at the entrance.

He's sitting on his chair at his desk.

I work at a bank.

in - for an ENCLOSED SPACE. Basically, when something is inside something.

EXAMPLES:

I’m growing tomatoes in my garden.

I was so tired that I took a nap in the car.

Marie was born in Paris, France.

on - for a SURFACE. It also means that something is in a position that is physically touching, covering or attached to something.

EXAMPLES:

Please place the bouquet on the table.

What’s on the menu this evening?

You are standing on my foot.

at (point) in (enclosed space) on (surface)
at the corner in the garden on the wall
at the bus stop in London on the ceiling
at the door in France on the door
at the top of the page in a box on the cover

at the entrance in my wallet on the carpet
at the crossroads in a building on the menu
at the front desk in a car on a page
