Work & Jobs interviews

During the job interviewing process it's important to use verbs that precisely describe your duties and responsibilities at your present and past positions. The following list provides verbs that are both precise and commonly used in an English-speaking workplace. These verbs are used to express responsibilities and tasks performed throughout an applicant's professional career.

Verb Meaning Example Sentence
accomplished highly trained or skilled. I've accomplished a lot in my current position.
administered manage and be responsible for the running of (a business, organization, etc.). I administered four committees.
advised behaving as someone, especially the speaker, would recommend; sensible; wise. I advised management on purchasing decisions.
allocated distribute (resources or duties) for a particular purpose. I allocated resources on a weekly basis.
analyzed examine methodically and in detail the constitution or structure of (something, especially information), typically for purposes of explanation and interpretation. I analyzed financial data.
applied (of a subject or type of study) put to practical use as opposed to being theoretical. I applied my knowledge to workflow.
arranged organize or make plans for (a future event). I arranged meetings.
assisted help (someone), typically by doing a share of the work. I assisted the CEO.
completed finish making or doing. I completed the highest level of the training.
conceived form or devise (a plan or idea) in the mind. I've conceived of numerous products.
collaborated work jointly on an activity, especially to produce or create something. I've collaborated with more than fifty clients.
conducted led by a guide; managed. I conducted telephone surveys.
consulted seek information or advice from (someone with expertise in a particular area). I've consulted on a wide range of issues.
contracted enter into a formal and legally binding agreement. I've contracted with large and small businesses.
coordinated bring the different elements of (a complex activity or organization) into a relationship that will ensure efficiency or harmony. I coordinated between sales and marketing departments.
created bring (something) into existence. I created more than twenty advertising campaigns.
dealt take part in commercial trading of a particular commodity. I've dealt with a wide variety of issues.
decided (of a person) having clear opinions; resolute. I've decided I need to further my career.
delegated send or authorize (someone) to do something as a representative. I've delegated tasks on a number of projects.
detected discover or identify the presence or existence of. I detected a number of mistakes.
developed advanced or elaborated to a specified degree. I developed an invention.
edited (of written material) corrected, condensed, or otherwise modified in preparation for publication. I edited company communications.
estimated (of a value or number) roughly calculated; approximate. I estimated future costs.
examined inspect (someone or something) in detail to determine their nature or condition; investigate thoroughly. I examined sites for pollution.
finalized produce or agree on a finished and definitive version of. I finalized projections for the year.
founded establish or originate (an institution or organization), especially by providing an endowment. I've founded two companies.
guided conducted by a guide. I guided operations through the process.
maintained cause or enable (a condition or state of affairs) to continue. I maintained the company database.
managed be in charge of (a company, establishment, or undertaking); administer; run. I've managed more than five hundred employees.
negotiated obtain or bring about by discussion. I negotiated a better deal for the company.
planned decide on and arrange in advance. I planned company retreats.
prepared made ready for use. I prepared documents for management.
presented formally introduce (someone) to someone else. I presented at many conferences.
promoted advance or raise (someone) to a higher position or rank. I promoted employees in human resources.
job interview an interview to determine whether an applicant is suitable for a position of employment She was dressed up for a job interview
Team Player A person who actively contributes to his team to complete tasks, meet goals or manage projects She is a great team player and an exceptional role model as a psychologist.
Responsible having an obligation to do something, or having control over or care for someone, as part of one's job or role. In my previous job, I was responsible for handling salary adjustments and preparing checks before pay days.
Achieve to get or reach (something) by working hard Our company wants to achieve the goal of doubling its profit before the end of the year.
Productive working hard and getting good results Companies want their employees to be very productive.
Success to achieve something that was wanted or hoped for. The interview was a success. I got the job!
Obstacle a thing that makes it difficult to get something done. Bad communication was a huge obstacle for the team.
Exhibit to show or reveal something. Terry exhibited that he can work well with other people while working on the project.
Leadership the power or ability to lead other people. The company needs someone with strong leadership skills.
Difficult not easy; requiring a lot of work. Speaking English during an interview can be difficult if you don’t practice.
Experience the skill or knowledge that you get by doing something. Jennifer has 3 years of software development experience.
Strength a quality that someone or something has that helps them succeed. Many people like to say that working hard is their greatest strength.
Weakness a quality someone or something has that is not good. Jane’s biggest weakness is that she doesn’t ask for help when she needs it.
Candidate a person who is competing to get a job. Our manager will interview six candidates for the job.
Hire to give work or a job to someone. Ed did a great job during the interview and was hired by the company.
Salary an amount of money that an employee is paid each year My new salary will be $45,000.
Negotiate a formal discussion between people who are trying to reach an agreement. I want to negotiate next year’s salary.
Work Environment the conditions that you work in and the effect they have on your ability to work. The work environment at my last job was relaxed.
Performance how well a person, machine, etc. does a piece of work or an activity The best candidate will have a high-performance level.
Goal something that a person wants to achieve. Our goal is to hire someone as soon as possible.
Supervised to be in charge of The UN is supervising the distribution of aid by local agencies in the disaster area.
