Vocabulary for Global issues

Environmental

relating to the natural world, especially to its conservation.

  • Media attention has shifted recently onto environmental issues.
  • The government is facing pressure from environmental campaigners.
  • The manifesto includes tough measures to tackle road congestion and environmental pollution.

Pollution

harm done to the environment through chemicals or waste products that affects the air, soil, or water of the earth.

  • The manifesto includes tough measures to tackle road congestion and environmental pollution.
  • The company claims it is not responsible for the pollution in the river.
  • Car exhaust is the main reason for the city's pollution.
  • The once azure skies of Athens have been ruined by atmospheric pollution.

Political turmoil

a state of confusion or problems in the government of a country or region.

  • The whole region is in turmoil. 
  • The country is in a state of political turmoil.

Poverty 

the state of not having enough money to take care of basic needs such as food, clothing, and housing.

  • He emigrated to Australia to escape the grinding poverty of his birthplace.
  • Helping to alleviate poverty in developing countries also helps to reduce environmental destruction.

Hunger

a shortage of food affecting a few individuals or small areas.

  • All over the world, people die of hunger every day.
  • After three days, we felt an agonizing, gnawing hunger.
  • We hadn't eaten since yesterday and the hunger pangs were getting harder to ignore.
  • It revolts me to know that the world spends so much money on arms when millions are dying of hunger.

Famine

a severe shortage of food resulting in widespread hunger.

  • Another crop failure could result in widespread famine.
  • There were reports of refugees dying of famine.
  • Thousands of people emigrated during the Irish potato famine of 1845–46.

Child labor

the full-time employment of children, especially of those who are legally too young to work.

  • The problem of child labor is still front and center in the globalized economy.
  • They called for a boycott of the garments that had been produced by child labor.

Natural disasters

an event such as an earthquake caused by natural forces rather than by human action.

  • Europe's 2003 heat wave claimed more than 30,000 lives, making it the continent's largest natural disaster in 50 years.
  • The subway began shutting down at noon, the first time the system was closed because of a natural disaster.

Ecosystem

all the organisms that live in a particular area, the way they affect each other and their environment as a whole.

  • Pollution can have disastrous effects on the delicately balanced ecosystem.
  • Marine biologists fear the predatory crabs have the potential to disrupt marine ecosystems.
  • They realized they would have to ban fishing in some areas in order to keep ecosystems healthy.

Climate change

a permanent change in global or regional climate patterns, including temperature, wind, and rainfall.

  • The President called for urgent action on climate change.
  • Technological methods to cope with climate change will become more and more important.
  • I didn't know anything about climate change at the time.

Global warming

 the increase in the average temperature of the Earth as a result of the greenhouse effect.

  • The destruction of the rainforests is contributing to global warming.
  • Do you think that these higher-than-average temperatures are attributable to global warming?
  • Several hypotheses for global warming have been suggested.

Fundraiser

a social event held with the intention of raising money for a charity or political party.

  • Thanks to your energy and commitment, the fundraiser was a great success.
  • Marlena Fischer is emerging as a top fundraiser for the charity.
  •  The fundraiser to help hurricane victims will be held tonight at the community center.

Economic crisis

a severe economic state that is marked by high rates of inflation, unemployment or depression.

  • The recent economic crisis has led to massive layoffs.
  • The country now faces an economic crisis .
  •  Financial crisis rapidly inflated to a nation-wide economic crisis.

Globalization

The integration of national economies into the international economy.

  • We must take advantage of the increased globalization of the commodity trading business.
  • The globalization of politics coincided with the use of US political consultants by Western democracies.

Multinational Corporation

Businesses that operate in many(multiple) countries.

  • When someone is wronged, they like to hear the wrongdoer express contrition, whether it's a significant other or a multinational corporation.
  • Running a multinational corporation with annual revenues north of $63 billion and a market cap of more than $153 billion is not easy.

Overpopulation

The number of people in an area exceeds the capacity of the environment to support life at a decent standard of living.

  • Overpopulation is one of the country's most pressing social problems.
  • The Irish would not allow overpopulation to occur again.

 

Urbanization

The movement of people to cities.

  • Its urbanization, progressing steadily, had finally reached the ultimate.
  • Owing to reclamation, technological improvements and urbanization, agricultural productivity and the level of production rose during the period.

> 

Green Revolution

A period in which scientists applied to technology to increase food production.

  • The Green Revolution of the '60s and '70s fed many hungry mouths in Asia.
  • Energy-saving light bulbs are just one sign of the Green Revolution.

> 

Acid Rain

Rain that falls through the air containing fossil fuels.

  • Acid rain has a devastating effect on the forest.
  • Sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxide combine to form acid rain.

 

Ozone Layer

A layer of gasses, in high atmosphere, that protects the Earth from dangerous UV rays of the sun.

  • The ozone layer surrounding the earth protects our skin from being hurt by the ultraviolet rays.
  • Most people know about the Ozone layer in the earth's stratosphere.

 

Greenhouse Effect

When warm air becomes trapped in the lower atmosphere.

  • They claim that the floods are a result of the greenhouse effect.
  • As it rots, it releases carbon dioxide and methane, contributing to the greenhouse effect.

Deforestation

The destruction of forests (esp. tropical rain forests).

  • Overgrazing by sheep and goats contributed to the deforestation.
  • Last year, the rate of deforestation in the Amazon declined by almost 30 percent.

Malnutrition

physical weakness and bad health caused by having too little food, or too little of the types of food necessary for good health.

  • Many of the refugees are suffering from severe malnutrition.
  • Infections are more likely in those suffering from malnutrition.
