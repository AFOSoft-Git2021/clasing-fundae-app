Words And Expressions of Foreign Origin

liché

MEANING: a phrase, remark, or opinion that has been said or expressed very often before and is therefore not original and not interesting.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • My wedding day - and I know it's a cliché - was just the happiest day of my life.

  • Occasionally his writing slips into cliché.

  • If you'll forgive me for using a book reviewer's cliché, I couldn't put it down.

  • He often wrote to radio announcers complaining about their use of cliché.

  • It's a cliche to say that the creations of a great artist will forever live after them.

  • The cliché was that Britain had terrible food, the worst in Europe.

  • Niagara Falls may be the ultimate tourist cliché but it remains one of the world's most breathtaking sights.

  • He has been described as looking like a cliché of a librarian.

Faux pas

MEANING: words or behavior that are a social mistake or not polite; a remark or action in a social situation that is a mistake and causes embarrassment or offense.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • I made some remarks about his wife's family, and then realized I'd made a serious faux pas.

  • She made the faux pas of referring to Wales as “part of England.”

  • Secret is arguably more tantalizing because it's pretty much faux pas-less social networking.

  • She looked around to be sure her faux pas was undetected.

  • Edna was met with a hushed silence fitting for only the most violent faux pas.

> Terra incognita

MEANING: a place, subject, or situation that you are not familiar with.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • Dealing with children is terra incognita to me.

  • No reader will find that description very enlightening, so the dependent variable remains terra incognita.

  • Stepping into terra incognita by deed seems to trigger openings into the terra incognita of metanormal experience.

  • Berlin has potential, power and something of the terra incognita.

> Bravado

MEANING: a show of courage, especially when unnecessary and dangerous, to make people admire you.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • It was an act of bravado that made him ask his boss to resign.

  • A game legendary for its macho bravado that's dominated by pasty-faced math geeks.

  • Later bravado runs high to disguise their feelings and some of them exploit the situation for gain.

  • The soldiers cloaked their fear with joking and bravado.

  • Taylor tells the story anonymously, with an aw shucks bravado.

> Venue

MEANING: the place where a public event or meeting happens; the city or county in which a trial happens.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The hotel is an ideal venue for conferences and business meetings.

  • The stadium has been specifically designed as a venue for World Cup games.

  • An alternative venue for the concert is being sought.

  • Hats off to Connie for finding such a splendid venue for a party!

  • We don't yet know what the venue will be for the ceremony.

  • The hotel is often used as a conference venue.

  • The restaurant was the ideal venue for a romantic evening.

> Pièce de résistance

MEANING: the best and most important or exciting thing, often the last in a series of things; 

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The pièce de résistance of his stage act was a brilliant Barbra Streisand impression.

  • The waiter suggested we try the restaurant's pièce de résistance: the chocolate soufflé.

> Fait accompli

MEANING: something that has already happened or been done and cannot be changed.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The policy change was presented to us as a fait accompli, without consultation or discussion.

  • China’s goal would likely be to seize Taiwan as quickly as possible, to present the U.S. with a fait accompli.

  • She married the man her parents disapproved of and presented them with a fait accompli.

  • From analog to digital technology change has become a fait accompli.

> Entourage

MEANING: the group of people who travel with and work for an important or famous person.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The star arrived in London with her usual entourage of dancers and backup singers.

  • Her usual entourage includes musicians, backup singers, and technicians.

  • She had one police officer with her, no entourage, no massive security detail.

  • She would socialize with people from her family, which was all kind of part of the entourage, and they're not rich.

  • Just present in his home listening and watching him interacting with his advisers and entourage.

> Bon vivant

MEANING: a person who enjoys good food and wines and likes going to restaurants and parties.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • He was not distinguished in college for his scholarship, but rather as a wit, a bon vivant, and a good fellow.

  • He created a skirt-chasing bon vivant persona for himself.

  • He was her sole bon vivant in the true sense of the word, whatever that may be.

  • The whole office wishes the happy landlord 'bon vivant' until we can do better by him.

> Encore

MEANING: an extra song or piece of music that is performed at the end of a show because the audience shouts for it; shouted by the audience at the end of a performance to get the performer to sing or play more.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The audience demanded encore after encore.

  • The soloist brought the house down with an encore for his impressive voice.

  • The maestro assented to the request for an encore.

  • The film charts the efforts of a dodgy theater manager trying to lure the singer back for a last encore.

  • She topped off her performance with a dazzling encore.

> Juggernaut

MEANING: a very large, heavy truck; a very large and powerful company, organization, or industry.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • Health care has turned into something of a juggernaut in the economy recently.

  • His main challenge now is to try to compete with a juggernaut.

  • Through the shimmer he saw the huge octopus eyes of the juggernaut grow dimmer.

> Hoi polloi

MEANING: ordinary people.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • Anthony will be in the VIP lounge where he doesn't have to mix with the hoi polloi.

  • They had little faith that the hoi polloi would very soon be using computers.

> Nouveau riche

MEANING: people from a low social class who have recently become very rich and like to show this by spending a lot of money.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The restaurant is popular with the city's nouveau riche.

  • The club has many members from blue-blood families who would not necessarily have much in common with the nouveau riche.

  • An "old money" elite raise a disapproving eyebrow at the nouveau riche.

  • a New England aristocrat who bridled at the nouveau riche

  • the apartments and suburban mini-mansions of the nouveau riche

  • The New Yorker-like style of the magazine was aimed at intellectuals, not the nouveau riche.

> Modus vivendi

MEANING: an arrangement allowing people or groups of people who have different opinions or beliefs to work or live together.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • Our two countries must put aside the memory of war and seek a modus vivendi.

  • Fascinated by displays of wealth, Parr cites his photographs of the nouveau riche in Moscow as his best work.

> Panache

MEANING: a stylish, original, and very confident way of doing things that makes people admire you.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The orchestra played with great panache.

  • Their work has all the usual punch, panache and flair you'd expect.

  • The cannons are played with panache yet with the heart on the sleeve.

  • The performance didn't have the panache you expect from a chart-topping rock band.

> Bona fide

MEANING: real, not false.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • Make sure you are dealing with a bona fide company.

  • She may be a bona fide member of the food media now, but this book chronicles how she ended up where she is.

  • We are happy to donate to a bona fide charitable cause.

  • Charging an expense on a company credit card does not automatically make it a bona fide business expense.

> Bulwark

MEANING: something that protects you from dangerous or unpleasant situations.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • My savings were a bulwark against unemployment.

  • Our people's support is a bulwark against the enemy.

  • This provides a necessary bulwark against the danger of an all-powerful state invading the individual's liberty.

  • The navy is our principal bulwark against invasion.

  • Law and morality are the bulwark of society.

> Malaise

MEANING: a general feeling of being sick or having no energy, or an uncomfortable feeling that something is wrong, especially with society, and that you cannot change the situation.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • They claim it is a symptom of a deeper and more general malaise in society.

  • We were discussing the roots of the current economic malaise.

  • Many think there’s a growing moral malaise in society.

  • These findings draw a picture of consumers coming to grips with their inability to navigate the long economic malaise.

> In flagrante delicto

MEANING: in the act of doing something wrong, especially having sex with someone who is not your husband, wife, or usual partner.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • She returns home early to find her husband in flagrante delicto with a neighbor.

  • In such circumstances, police or military personnel may be unofficially authorized to severely punish individuals involved in looting, rioting and other violent acts, especially if caught "in flagrante delicto".

  • One winter night her husband took an unexpected shore leave, and caught them both "in flagrante delicto".

> Cashmere

MEANING: very soft, expensive wool material that is made from the hair of goats from Kashmir.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • Walter would want a cashmere sweater, a blazer from Brooks Brothers, or a Mont Blanc pen.

  • She leaned lightly against the soft wool of his dark cashmere coat.

  • When he jumped down some twenty minutes later, I found he had chewed away a large portion of my cashmere sweater.
