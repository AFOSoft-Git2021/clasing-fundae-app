Adjectives ending in “-ED

An adjective that ends in -ED is used to describe: a feeling (or how a person feels) or an emotion. It is used to describe a temporary thing. Since only people (and some animals) have feelings, -ed adjectives cannot be used to describe an object or situation.

  • My girlfriend is bored.
  • I am confused.
  • The boy was interested in reading.

The ED adjective is formed by adding ed to the end of the verb:

interest > interested

This ED form is called the past participle. We are using it as an adjective.

Example:

I am very interested in football.

Common Adjectives ending With ED (Meaning and Sentences)

Used

1. employed in accomplishing something.

2. (of persons) taken advantage of.

  • Fodder crops are used to feed livestock.
  • They used dry twigs to start the fire.
  • He used a knife instead of chopsticks.

United

1. of or relating to two people who are married to each other.

2. involving the joint activity of two or more.

  • Factions previously at war with one another are now united against the common enemy.
  • The whole town was united in grief.
  • It was the first film festival to take place in a united Germany.

Posted

1. publicly displayed.

  • Keep me posted on anything that happens while I'm away.
  • The check was posted to your home address yesterday.
  • A guard was posted outside the building.

Reserved

1. set aside for the use of a particular person or party.

2. marked by self-restraint and reticence.

  • I have a ticket reserved for today's matinee.
  • The English have a reputation for being reserved.
  • Ellen was a shy, reserved girl.

Required

1. necessary for relief or supply.

2. required by rule.

  • We hope to be able to raise the required sum of money.
  • The company failed to maintain its required minimum net capital.
  • I bet they still required you to do your best.

Listed

1. on a list.

  • This approach is straightforward when a single diagnosis is listed, as is often the case for outpatient and emergency department visits.
  • The items are listed in order of importance.
  • Her name is listed in the telephone directory.

Limited

1. small in range or scope.

2. subject to limits or subjected to limits.

  • My culinary skills are rather limited, I'm afraid !
  • I'm afraid my grasp of economics is rather limited.
  • I only have a limited knowledge of Spanish.

Needed

1. necessary for relief or supply.

  • After six hours of yard work, we sat down for a much-needed rest.
  • Most people like to feel needed.

Wanted

1. desired or wished for or sought.

2. characterized by feeling or showing fond affection for.

  • He's a wanted man.
  • She was a much wanted baby.
  • He was one of the ten most wanted criminals in America.

Completed

1. successfully completed or brought to an end.

2. (of a marriage) completed by the first act of sexual intercourse after the ceremony.

  • I have complete confidence in her. She'll be perfect for the job.
  • All replies will be treated with complete confidentiality.
  • At school she had complete contempt for all her teachers.
  • His answer demonstrated a complete lack of understanding of the question.

Reported

1. Made known or told about; especially presented in a formal account.

  • The number of reported crimes has increased.
  • There are over 55,000 reported cases of food poisoning every year.

Approved

1. Established by authority; given authoritative approval

  • What's the approved way of dealing with this?
  • This school only offers approved language courses.
  •  The conference approved a proposal for a referendum.

Continued

1. without stop or interruption

  • Continued fighting in the city is causing great concern.
  • The team's continued involvement in the competition is uncertain.
  • I'm reckoning on your continued support.
  • Police urged continued vigilance in the fight against crime.

Certified

1. endorsed authoritatively as having met certain requirements

2. fit to be certified as insane (and treated accordingly)

  • The meat has been certified fit for human consumption.
  • The accounts were certified by an auditor.
  • He certified it was his wife's handwriting.
  • The bank certified my accounts.

Determined

1. characterized by great determination

2. devoting full strength and concentrated attention to

  • I'm determined to get this piece of work done today.
  • She will get the job she wants - she's a very determined person.
  • She's determined to make this project a success.
  • He was determined to find out the truth.

Ended

1. having come or been brought to a conclusion

  • All their plans ended in failure.
  • Attempts to negotiate peace ended in armed revolt.
  • The report ended on a positive note.
  • The army was disbanded when the war ended.

Forced

1. produced by or subjected to forcing

2. forced or compelled

  • The plane had to make a forced landing because one of the engines failed.
  • She tried hard to smile but suspected that it looked forced.
  • The company will use any proceeds from a forced sale to pay off debt.
