Compound Adjectives for Personality


Hard-working

MEANING: always doing a lot of work.

EXAMPLES:

  • Pointing out your privilege doesn't mean you aren't talented, smart, or hard-working.

  • The staff working on the unit are hard-working and have very little time to sit at a desk.

  • His previous employer describes him as honest, hard-working and conscientious.

  • The demise of the industry has caused untold misery to thousands of hard-working tradesmen.


Tight-fisted

MEANING: unwilling to spend money,

EXAMPLES:

  • Don't wait for Krista to buy you a drink - she's too tight-fisted.

  • He was known to have made a fortune on the stock market, but was nonetheless notoriously tight-fisted.

  • He was small, lame, tight-fisted, and apt to weep under pressure, a performance that could disconcert authors and employees.

  • He had the reputation of being one of the most tight-fisted and demanding of employers.

Kind-hearted

MEANING: (of a person) showing a lot of care and kindness for other people

EXAMPLES:

  • He knows what his buddies say about him: loyal friend, devoted husband and parent, kind-hearted, a good guy to drink a beer with.

  • Her tears seemed to grieve the kind-hearted Munchkins, for they immediately took out their handkerchiefs and began to weep also.

  • The world needs more people who are kind-hearted, self-loving, and compassionate.

  • It would be a great shame if the charity folded, after all the efforts of so many kind-hearted people.


Absent-minded

MEANING: Someone who is absent-minded often forgets things or does not pay attention to what is happening near them because they are thinking about other things.

EXAMPLES:

  • He leads two visitors on a brief tour, like an absent-minded sociologist relating human experiments to the past.

  • I often do absent-minded things, particularly when I'm worried.

  • The thought played over and over in her mind as she waved an absent-minded farewell and drove off into the night alone.

  • Stephen gave an absent-minded nod and hurried off to solve the next problem.


Two-faced

MEANING: Someone who is two-faced is not sincere, saying unpleasant things about you to other people while seeming to be pleasant when they are with you; hypocritical.

>

EXAMPLES: 

  • I've never met anyone so two-faced: she's sweet and charming to your face, and then goes and complains about you to the boss!

  • Whoever you blame on the scum transfer fiasco he came out of it as a two-faced hypocrite.

  • Don't tell her any secret of yours because she is known for being a two-faced person.


Pig-headed

MEANING: showing unreasonable support for an opinion or plan of action and refusing to change or listen to different opinions

EXAMPLES: 

  • Not even somebody as pig-headed as Ira could argue about it.

  • That pig-headed old man refused to cooperate with the neighbors.

  • The consulate official was being pigheaded about giving us our visas.

  • Although the seasoned mechanic knew what was wrong with the car, the pig-headed owner refused to listen to the expert’s advice.

  • My pig-headed son was too stubborn to wear a rain jacket or boots even when the forecast called for rain. 

 

Smooth-tongued

MEANING: fluent or convincing in speech

EXAMPLES:

  • From the instant she'd recognized his style, Angela equated the speaker with her father, another smooth-tongued gentleman.

  • Now what we have is the most telegenic, the most entertaining, the smooth-tongued candidates win primaries and that's it.

  • Selling was hard as teaching smooth-tongued statesmen that cleanliness and godliness are contiguous.


Stuck-up

MEANING: too proud or satisfied with yourself

EXAMPLES:

  • I never really liked her - she was always a stuck-up person.

  • Jessie's email reply has given rise to a barrage of complaints, with memories of her earlier stuck-up posture still fresh in clients' minds.

  • The new girl at school seems stuck-up, but I think she is just a little shy .


Self-centered

MEANING: only interested in yourself and your own activities

EXAMPLES:

  • Angela is a good kid who is also, at times, whiny and self-centered.

  • Surrender is hard work. In our case, it is intense warfare against our self-centered nature.

  • People, and that includes all of us, often make selfish, self-centered choices.

  • There is no doubt that in exchanging a self-centered for a selfless life we gain enormously in self-esteem. 


Good-natured

MEANING: pleasant or friendly

EXAMPLES:

  • By that time, they had become relaxed enough with each other to exchange a few good-natured barbs.

  • Behind the good-natured jokes and camaraderie that come from decades of familiarity, an air of sadness is palpable.

  • Neil was a gentle good-natured person, the type of man you instantly trust and feel comfortable around.


Quick-tempered

MEANING: likely to become angry very easily

EXAMPLES:

  • He was a nervous, quick-tempered man.

  • Looking after children requires patience. But I'm a quick-tempered mother.

  • Most people agree that tiger people are courageous but can also be quick-tempered. They may also be very egocentric and unpredictable.

  • Louis is an excitable, quick-tempered person.


Broad-minded

MEANING: willing to accept many different types of behavior, beliefs, or choices in other people

EXAMPLES:

  • He is broadminded about the different forms families can take.

  • A broad-minded approach opens more doors.

  • Today there are much more broadminded attitudes towards sexuality.

  • We are supposed to be a broad-minded society.


Narrow-minded

MEANING: not willing to accept ideas or ways of behaving that are different from your own

EXAMPLES: 

  • Nobody except a hard-hearted, narrow-minded politician would laugh.

  • She's one of those narrow-minded people who think that for a work of art to be great it can't be pleasurable.

  • He is a narrow-minded person who would crow over others' failures.

Quick-witted

MEANING: intelligent; able to react quickly and skillfully

EXAMPLES: 

  • Yingying is a quick-witted boy who represents the yellow Olympic ring.

  • Throughout a lifetime of public service, Adrian proved himself a quick-witted negotiator.

  • Roberty, a quick-witted lawyer, knows the world of antiquities dealers and runs a foundation dedicated to ancient art.
