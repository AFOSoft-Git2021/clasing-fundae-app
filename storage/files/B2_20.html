In order to ↔ So that

We use “so that” and “in order to” to talk about purpose. We often use them with modal verbs (can, would, will, etc.).  Both of them are conjunctions that we use to talk about the purpose of something. 

So that is far more common than in order that, and in order that is more formal:

  • I’ll go by car so that I can take more luggage.
  • We left a message with his neighbor so that he would know we’d called.
  • In order that you can sign the form, please print it out and mail it to this address.
  • I will do my best in order to teach you English.

So that

Used to introduce a clause of purpose. Another name for in order that. It also answers the question “Why?” We use it to begin adverb clauses of purpose. 

“So that” can also come at the beginning of a sentence but this is rarer and usually sounds stilted to Americans.

Sample sentences

  • Pass it on to her so that she can think things through and knows where to turn when she comes to her senses.
  • The point was to stretch myself far, so that it is easier in game situations.
  • But a singer's job is to interpret a story so that people feel it.
  • It takes quite a while for these emotions to settle, so that you can think more objectively.
  • I want to look good so that other people think nice things about me.
  • It would be helpful to make a few practice swings without touching the sand so that you become familiar with the feel of the shot.
  • It helps to lower blood sugar so that you feel less hungry.
  • So that you feel less hungry, it helps to lower blood sugar.

In order to

According to a particular sequence, In the correct condition for operation or use. This is used in very formal expressions or contexts. 

Keep in mind that in the sentence, it is followed by an infinitive verb.

Sample sentences

  • The government developed new insights in order to know more about the citizens.
  • In order to be more productive, they employed more people.
  •  I'm going to Ireland in order to visit my family.
  • I meditate in order to relax.
  •  Teamwork is required in order to achieve these aims.
  •  The minister held a press conference in order to pre-empt criticism in the newspapers.
  • In order to maximize profit the firm would seek to maximize output.
