Adjectives for Quantity

We can use number + noun as a compound adjective before another noun. This construction is often used with nouns of measurement, such as foot, meter, mile, pound, kilogram, second, minute, hour, pound, dollar, etc.. Here are things that you should remember about number-noun compound adjectives:

  1. Remember adjectives have no plural form in English:

    1. A two-days journey - incorrect

    2. A two-day journey - correct

  1. Use a hyphen to connect the number to the noun that follows it. This allows the reader to know that both words function as a unit that modifies the noun after it:

    1. A 30-minute show

    2. A two-day journey

    3. A 50-meter pool

    4. A eight-core processor

    5. A two-bedroom apartment

    6. six-sided figure

  1. Sometimes, a compound adjective is made up of more than one word:

    1. A 10-million-dollar house

    2. A seven-year-old child

  1. When you want to make a compound adjective out of a number and a noun, use the singular form of the noun and connect the words with a hyphen (-). If the number is less than 10, write out the number. If the number is 10 or greater, use the numeral

    1. five-star hotel

    2. 12-hour shift

  1. If the unit is represented by an abbreviation, use numerals.

    1. a 5-lb. bag of flour

    2. two 6-ft. lengths of timber

  1. Do not use a hyphen between a numeral and a non-letter symbol or between a numeral and a metric unit symbol:

    1. a 90° angle

    2. four 100 °C thermometers

    3. a 2.36 m high jump

    4. three 5 L containers


Sample sentences:

  • For this prank, you will need a five-dollar bill and a piece of cloth.

  • In this room, we see a six-sided figure with three bright lines through it.

  • I live in a one-bedroom apartment.

  • The student wrote a 10-page report last night.

  • She has a five-dollar bill.

  • Angela is a 20-year-old girl who has hemiplegia.

  • The event was a three-hour conference.

  • A four-day journey would be hard; there would be no amenities; they would all be pushed to their limits.

  • We had to hike a five-kilometer tunnel  before we were able to see the waterfalls. 

  • I felt so drained during the exam. It was a 30-question test.

  • The Queen is making a two-week tour of Australia.

  • I had to pull over to take a 10-minute call from my boss. 

  • We had to drive for a few days to reach a 20-mile border.

  • Chained to its banister are a ten-speed bicycle and another, much smaller, with training wheels.

  • We had to use the stairs to climb down from a 40-story building when we had an earthquake drill.
