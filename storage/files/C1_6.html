Simple Present – special usage of “do”

To emphasize the meaning of the verb in the Present Simple Tense, it is possible to use the auxiliary verb do/does also in the affirmative sentence. The sentence then does not change its meaning, it only strengthens it.


The emphatic form is constructed by combining some form of the verb "do" with the root form of some other verb. This has the effect of emphasizing the verb in question calling a greater degree of attention to it. 

You've probably used the emphatic form when someone has accused you of not doing something. You've probably said for example, that no, you "did take" the trash out. 


EXAMPLE:

I love her. 

I do love her.


In oral expression, the main emphasis in the sentence is on the auxiliary verb do/does.

EXAMPLE:

You do look nice today.

He does believe you!



Similar strengthening can be given to imperatives by adding the same auxiliary verb:

EXAMPLE:

Come with us.

Do come with us!

Do stop talking, Harry! You’re boring everybody!


The structure is:

  • present emphatic: we start with the subject followed by the auxiliary do followed by a verb in its base form. In the third person singular, we use does;

  • past emphatic: we start with the subject followed by the auxiliary did followed by a verb in its base form.
