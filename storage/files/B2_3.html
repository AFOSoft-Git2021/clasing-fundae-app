Sentence Connectors to talk about the Present

This is used to state a fact or idea that is occurring in the current time or moment.

Nowadays

Meaning:  At the present  time, in comparison to the past:

Sample Sentences: 

  • Who remembers those movies nowadays?
  • Nowadays, I bake my own bread rather than buy it.

Currently

Meaning: At the present  time; now.

Sample Sentences: 

  • He currently is directing TV sitcoms.
  • The interest rate on my credit card is currently 25.5% APR.
  • He currently holds the position of technical manager.

At present

Meaning: The period of time that is happening now, not the past or the future

Sample Sentences: 

  • At present we're gleaning information from all sources.
  • We are at present in correspondence with the company on the matter of our estimate for the repair of the bridge.
  • At present we have no evidence of life on other planets.

At the present time

Meaning: at this time: now

Sample Sentences: 

  • No further changes are planned at the present time.
  • At the present time some of the old Airey houses are being demolished and rebuilt.
  • One of the chief causes at the present time was the increase in interest rates, especially for the self-employed.

Now

Meaning: Used to express how long something has been happening, from when it began to the present  time:

Sample Sentences: 

  • I shall now go to sleep. Good night.
  • All we can do now is wait and hope.
  • The national railways have now been electrified.

These days

Meaning: Used to talk about the present  time, in comparison with the past:

Sample Sentences: 

  • Vegetarianism is very popular these days.
  • These days you have to be careful who you trust.
  • Pretty much all restaurants have gluten-free options these days.
  • You can't be too careful these days.


In this day and age

Meaning: Up to now : even ow : continuing until today 

Sample Sentences: 


  • That old man over there had singled out a black child in this day and age!
  • He says there shouldn't be toll bridges in this day and age.
  • You can'taffordrun businesses inefficiently in thisday and age.
