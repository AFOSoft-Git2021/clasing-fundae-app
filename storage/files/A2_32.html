Planets & Universe

The planet Earth on which we live is part of the solar system. The Sun is a star at the centre of the solar system, and eight* planets and other bodies revolve around it.

The eight planets, in order from the Sun, are:

a. the inner planets

- terrestrial planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars

b. the outer planets

-gas giants: Jupiter, Saturn

-ice giants: Uranus, Neptune

SOLAR SYSTEM DESCRIPTION
Sun The star that is the source of light and heat for the planets in the solar system.
Moon The natural satellite of the Earth.
Earth The third planet from the sun. Earth is the only place in the known universe confirmed to host life. The planet Earth has 1 moon. The name Earth means soil.
Mercury The smallest planet in our solar system and nearest to the Sun,
Mars A small reddish planet that is the 4th from the sun and is periodically visible to the naked eye; minerals rich in iron cover its surface and are responsible for its characteristic color. The planet Mars has 2 moons. Mars is also known as the "Red Planet"
Venus The second nearest planet to the sun. It’s one of the four inner, terrestrial (or rocky) planets, and it’s often called Earth’s twin because it’s similar in size and density.
Saturn A giant planet that is surrounded by three planar concentric rings of ice particles; the 6th planet from the sun. Saturn is also known as one of the "gas giants". The planet Saturn has 62 moons.
Jupiter The largest planet and the 5th from the sun; has many satellites and is one of the brightest objects in the night sky
Uranus A giant planet with a ring of ice particles; the 7th planet from the sun has a blue-green color and many satellites. Uranus is the smallest of the four “gas giants". Uranus is the coldest planet in our Solar System. The planet Uranus was named after the Greek god of the sky. The planet Uranus has 27 moons.
Neptune A giant planet with a ring of ice particles; the 8th planet from the sun is the most remote of the gas giants. The planet Neptune has 13 moons. Neptune was named after the Roman god of the sea. Neptune is the coldest planet in the Solar system.
Pluto A small planet and the farthest known planet from the sun; it has the most elliptical orbit of all the planets.

Vocabulary list

WORD DEFINITION
Satellites An artificial body placed in orbit around the earth or moon or another planet in order to collect information or for communication.
Planet A celestial body distinguished from the fixed stars by having an apparent motion of its own (including the moon and sun), especially with reference to its supposed influence on people and events.
Asteroids A small rocky body orbiting the sun.
Meteors A small body of matter from outer space that enters the earth's atmosphere, becoming incandescent as a result of friction and appearing as a streak of light.
Comets A celestial object consisting of a nucleus of ice and dust and, when near the sun, a “tail” of gas and dust particles pointing away from the sun.
Gravity The force that attracts a body toward the center of the earth, or toward any other physical body having mass.
Orbit The curved path of a celestial object or spacecraft around a star, planet, or moon, especially a periodic elliptical revolution.
