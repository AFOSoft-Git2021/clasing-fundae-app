PERFECT PASSIVE INFINITIVE 

The present perfect is a tense that usually describes something about the past that has a connection with the present. It can describe a past experience that has importance to now, or it can refer to an action that began in the past and continues in the present, or it can express a recent action that creates consequences in this moment.

When we use the passive form, we focus attention on what or who receives an action (the object). We use the passive in almost all the tenses in English and create it by using the verb ‘to be’. 

We use the present perfect in the passive form for all the same reasons we use it in the active form – to talk about recent actions, experiences, and ongoing actions/situations.

In the present perfect form with the passive, we always use:

 ‘has/have been’ + the past participle form

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • The staff have been trained.

  • The reports have been written.

  • Have the candidates been interviewed?

  • Have you been introduced to the new manager?

  • He’s been taken to see the President.

  • Sara has been promoted three times in her career.

  • The waste products have been left here since last February.

  • This sonnet seems to have been written by Shakespeare.

  • Luis is driving around in a new car. He appears to have been given a raise.

  • Luis is lucky to have been given a raise. I don’t know how he still has a job.

  • Mia is considered to have been the best manager of the company.

  • She appears to have been influenced by his hefty package.

  • The thief seems to have been beaten by the mob.

  • The driver appeared to have been killed in the accident.

  • The soldiers are likely to have been rewarded for their bravery.

  • The Government seemed to have been controlled by a few powerful persons.

  • The frog seems to have been scissored by medical students.

  • The girl appeared to have been kidnapped from her house.

  • Her house seemed to have been demolished a few days ago.

  • Her father is likely to have been killed in the riot.

  • Rajat seemed to have been deceived by someone.
