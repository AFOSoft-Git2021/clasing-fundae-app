PERFECT CONTINUOUS INFINITIVE 

The perfect continuous infinitive is a combination of the perfect and continuous forms and is used to express a continuing but now completed action after a verb which must be followed by the infinitive.

The perfect continuous infinitive is used to express a continuing but now completed action after a verb which must be followed by the infinitive. The perfect infinitive is formed:

to have been + present participle

  • I'm glad to have been living in Barcelona for the last ten years.

  • He must have been waiting for ages.

  • Soon, he'll have been running for four hours.

  • The organizers were thought to have been preparing for days.

As with the present infinitive, there are situations where the zero form of the perfect continuous infinitive is required, so the word to is omitted. This happens after most modal auxiliaries, and in other places where zero infinitives are used:

  • You should have been studying for your test.

Negative Sentences

to have + not + been + present participle

  • They haven’t been talking for more than a few minutes.

  • She hasn’t been studying English for very long.

  • Don’t worry, I haven’t been waiting long

Interrogative Sentences:  

to have + subject+ been + present participle

  • Have they been talking for a long time?

  • Have you been waiting long?
