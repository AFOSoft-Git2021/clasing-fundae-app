Sound and Noise: 

Snigger

MEANING: the act of laughing at someone or something in a silly and often unkind way.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Schoolchildren snigger at the mention of some words.

  • Stop sniggering at the back of the class please!

  • We were having a snigger at the bride who was rather large and dressed in a tight, pale pink dress.

  • There aren't many words which raise a snigger every time they're mentioned.

  • Few other men in public life could say those words without provoking sniggers of disbelief in his audience.

  • After half-an-hour of suppressing my sniggers at this drivel, I couldn't understand why nobody else was laughing.

Roar

MEANING: to make a long, loud, deep sound.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The lion let out a loud roar.

  • His apartment was on a main road and there was a constant roar of traffic.

  • The plane roared overhead and went into a nosedive.

  • A cloud of dust rose in the air as the car roared past.

  • The roar of city traffic is a steady assault on one's nerves.

  • His voice was barely audible above the roar of the crowd.

  • A mighty roar went up from the crowd as the home team scored.

>

Whimper

MEANING: a series of small, weak sounds expressing pain or unhappiness, especially of an animal.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • She gave a little whimper as the vet inspected her paw.

  • The tense silence was broken by a frightened whimper from Louise.

  • I was listening for a cry or a whimper from upstairs.

  • The old man looked at her in terror and began to whimper.

  • The old dog could barely whimper and seemed close to death.

Bawl

MEANING: to shout in a very loud voice.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • He was afraid Vic would bawl him out for being late.

  • Your father will bawl you out when he sees this mess.

  • You don't have to bawl out like that. Everybody can hear you.

  • You know how I hate people who bawl all the time.

Wail

MEANING: to make a long, high cry, usually because of pain or sadness.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The women gathered around the coffin and began to wail, as was the custom in the region.

  • One of the small children began to wail with terror.

  • We were woken in the night by the wail of ambulance sirens.

  • The wail of the bagpipe could be heard in the distance.

  • We could just hear the distant wail of a siren.

>

Guffaw

MEANING: to laugh loudly, especially at something stupid that someone has said or done.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • It was guffaws and jokes all round, showing how indifferent and uncaring they were.

  • One of the court officers guffawed but turned his head before Kovitsky could spot him.

  • All the boys burst out into a guffaw at the joke.

  • The announcement was greeted with loud guffaws.

Cackle

MEANING: to make the loud sound of a chicken, or (of a person) to laugh or talk in a loud, high voice.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The chairman cut the cackle and brought his idea up at the meeting.

  • My tension level was so high that I was bound to sneeze or cough or groan or cackle maniacally.

  • The old woman gave a loud cackle.

  • Spider let out a weird high-pitched cackle that scared Miguel.

Clamor

MEANING: a loud complaint about something or a demand for something; loud noise, especially made by people's voices.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • After the bombing, there was a public clamor for vengeance.

  • The clamor for freedom in recent years has been strong.

  • The clamor of the witch hunt rose up from the people a good deal louder than it came down from the pulpits.

  • As the clamor escalated, it became clear that the fight for public opinion had become the main battleground.

  • The debate was interrupted by a clamor of opposition.

Snivel

MEANING: to cry slightly in a way that is weak and does not make other people feel sympathy for you.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Boring physics knowledge by perceptual out, a lot of girls immediately moved to snivel tears.

  • I was a raggedy scamp with a snivel nose and a hole in my cheeks.

  • He's sitting in his bedroom sniveling because he was told off for not doing his homework.

Chortle

MEANING: to laugh, showing pleasure and satisfaction.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • I thought I heard a chortle at the back of the room.

  • She suppressed an embarrassed chortle.

  • Vincent gave a jubilant chortle and laid his hand on her head.

  • A good chortle eases pain and boosts immunity.

  • He has a dry wit and merry chortle.

  • She suppressed an embarrassed chortle as the men began to chat about the paper.

  • Father used to chortle over the funny funny papers every Sunday. 

Holler

MEANING: to shout loudly.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • I heard him holler out, "Somebody bombed the Church."

  • We didn't expect anybody to stand up and holler that they had an epiphany.

  • The Republicans made a great whoop and a holler about the honesty of federal employees.

  • The rain doesn't holler wind and it moves hard the body, entering the world.

  • We did not expect anybody to stand up and holler that they had an epiphany.

Sob

MEANING: to cry noisily, taking in deep breaths.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • I could hear her sobs from the next room.

  • I do not want to tell a great sob story.

  • We can all tell sob stories, but adding them all together creates a false impression.

  • Some people may say that is sob stuff and sentimental talk.

  • When we came here 16 years ago the language used to us was that it was sob stuff, and now they use a new term.

Bellow

MEANING: to shout in a loud voice, or (of a cow or large animal) to make a loud, deep sound.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • His meek whisper became a powerful bellow.

  • As she walked uncertainty towards him, he gave a great bellow.

  • He saw blood around the cow's mouth and heard the rasp of its breath between each anguished bellow.

  • She gave a graceless snort, followed by a bellow of laughter.

  • The music was so loud we had to bellow at each other to be heard.

>

Whine

MEANING: to make a long, high, sad sound; to make a high, complaining sound, or to complain continually.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • She delivered the speech in a high-pitched nasal whine.

  • She delivered the speech in a high-pitched nasal whine.

  • I have to listen to her whine all day about her boyfriend.

  • He could hear the sound of hammering, then the whine of a circular saw.

  • A frigid wind surged through the arches, producing a ghoulish whine.

Giggle

MEANING: to laugh repeatedly in a quiet but uncontrolled way, often at something silly or rude or when you are nervous.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • We giggled at the back of the room like naughty school children.

  • When she suddenly started to sing, we all looked at each other and started to giggle.

  • Just for a giggle, we hid his trousers while he was in the water.

  • Seven out of the 10 giggled at the switchboard and the other three cut off the inquirer.

  • I desperately fought the urge to giggle.

  • She started to giggle, but Bob quelled her with a look.

Yell

MEANING: to shout something or make a loud noise, usually when you are angry, in pain, or excited.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Don't yell at me, I heard you the first time!

  • I usually just let her yell until she runs out of steam.

  • Steve could no longer prevent a yell of frustration.

  • James accelerated when he heard them yell.

  • When his schoolmate made the last goal, the boy gave out with an untrammeled yell.

  • Something brushed past Bob's face and he let out a yell.

Weep

MEANING: to cry tears.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Claire shut herself up in her own room to weep.

  • It might help you to have a good weep.

  • There's nothing like a bit of a weep to make you feel better.

  • They weep crocodile tears for the poor and disadvantaged but are basically happy with things as they are.

  • Many of the delegation members had begun to weep.

  • I would rather have my people laugh at my economies than weep for my extravagance. 

Rumble

MEANING: to make a continuous low sound.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • We could hear the rumble of distant guns/thunder.

  • They heard a low rumble and watched the sky darken.

  • He felt the floor shake under him and heard an ominous rumble.

  • The first sign of a night-time snowfall was that the incessant traffic rumble softened.

  • She heard the rumble of an American tank approaching.

Twang

MEANING: to make a noise like that of a tight string being quickly pulled and released.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • He twanged the guitar string.

  • He picks solid songs with themes just about anyone can relate to, and he sings them with a hint of twang in his warm voice.

  • There is no doubt that this music is more country than rock, even though the vocalists have less twang and far more range than most country singers.

Sizzle

MEANING: to make a sound like food cooking in hot fat.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Arizona sizzles in the summer.

  • The sizzle and smell of pork fat greeted us as we sat back down at the garden table.

  • Steam rises from the kettle and the pork chops sizzle, licked by flames from the dripping, igniting fat.

  • The vegetables dropped into the boiling oil with a sizzle.

Creak

MEANING: When a door, floorboard, etc. creaks, it makes a long low sound when it moves or is moved.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • I heard the floorboards creak as he crept closer.

  • The old floorboards creaked when I walked across the floor.

  • He heard a prison cell door creak open and slam shut.

  • The kitchen door has an annoying creak when you close it.

Patter

MEANING: continuous and sometimes funny speech or talk, especially used by someone trying to sell things or by an entertainer.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • He should succeed - he dresses well and his sales patter is slick and convincing.

  • I find the patter of rain on the roof soothing.

  • Phinizy’s clever patter kept his audience in a good mood.

Crackle

MEANING: To make a lot of short, dry, sharp sounds.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The radio started to crackle.

  • The logs crackled and popped in the fireplace.

  • The whistles and crackles are all-pervading.

  • There was a crackle of static, like a public address system at a church social, then another record started.

  • A muffled crackle sputters out from a walkie-talkie inside his jacket, and he pulls it out to log in.

  • The air was filled with sublime music and the sound of burning wood was like the soft crackle of melting ice.

Jingle

MEANING: to make a repeated gentle ringing sound, or to make things do this; a short tune or song used to advertise a product on television or the radio.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The coins jingled in her pocket as she walked along.

  • She is one of America's most successful commercial jingle writers.

  • Traditional and innovative methods operated in tandem and reinforced each other with the use of similar slogans and jingles.

Howl

MEANING: If a dog or wolf howls, it makes a long, sad sound; to make a loud sound, usually to express pain, sadness, or another strong emotion.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • He leaves his dog shut up in the house all day, and we can hear its howls.

  • She let out a howl of pain.

  • African hyena noted for its distinctive howl.

  • He makes audiences howl with laughter.

  • With a terrible howl, he leapt forward and threw himself into the water.

  • She heard the wind whistling through the trees and the howl of a distant wolf.

Snap

MEANING: to cause something that is thin to break suddenly and quickly with a cracking sound.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • You'll snap that ruler if you bend it too far.

  • Some vandal's snapped off my car aerial again.

  • You should snap out of your sadness.

  • The silence of the forest was made evident by the occasional snap of a twig.
