Must, Could, Should and Would have

Must have

When something certainly happened  (certainty)

When we expressed certainty about an event or state, in the past we use  “must have”:

MUST + HAVE + PAST PARTICIPLE

EXAMPLES:

  • You must have been tired.
  • He must have gone home.  
  • There’s no milk in the refrigerator.  We must have drunk it all.
  • Many more cars than usual were parked on our street last night.  Someone must have had a party.

Could have

Use could have to talk about possibilities if something had been different in the past.

COULD + HAVE + PAST PARTICIPLE

EXAMPLES:

  • If I had gone to college, I could have gotten a better job.
  • She could have won the gold medal if she hadn’t fallen three times.
  • I could have lost the keys in the pub.    
  • He could have got stuck in traffic.

Should have

Use should have to say that a different action was recommended in the past.

SHOULD + HAVE + PAST PARTICIPLE

EXAMPLES:

  • You should have gone there straight away.
  • I was so worried about you. You should have called!
  • I’m sorry that I’m late for work. I should have woken up earlier.

Would have

Use would have to imagine a result (if something had been different in the past)

WOULD + HAVE + PAST PARTICIPLE

Would have is a bit more difficult because it has two common structures. The first is with but.

1. To talk about something you wanted to do but didn’t

  • I would have gone to the party, but I was really busy.
  • I would have called you, but I didn’t know your number.
  • I would have loaned you the money, but I didn’t have any.

2. Part of the third conditional

  • If I had had enough money, I would have bought a car
  • If I had known they were vegetarians, I would have made a salad.

MORE SAMPLE SENTENCES

  • You could have broken your leg!
  • I could have gone to the cinema, but I decided to stay home.
  • She couldn’t have met him before. He used to live in Canada.
  • He couldn’t have gone to the shopping center. It’s closed on Sundays.
  • If we had arrived earlier, we would have caught our flight.
  • If I had brought my umbrella, I wouldn’t have gotten wet in the rain.
  • You’re an hour late! You could have let me know!
  • You could have sent a message at least!
  • She didn’t warn me about the visit, she could have mentioned that they were coming!
  • Even if I had studied for 1000 hours I could not have passed that exam! It was too difficult
  • I could have been a professional dancer, but I broke my leg when I was 17.
  • You could have passed your exams if you had studied as I told you!
  • he could have asked me to help him build the wardrobe, instead of spending all day doing it by himself.
  • Karen is late, she must have missed her train.
  • I don’t have my keys,  I must have left them in the kitchen.
  • The bicycle has disappeared. it must have been stolen.
  • He should have gone to the doctor immediately instead of waiting.
  • She should have added more salt to the food, it tastes bland.
  • We shouldn’t have turned left before, this is the wrong direction.
  • You shouldn’t have said that to her, now she’s upset.
  • He shouldn’t have gone to work, he was very ill.
  • He wouldn’t have crashed the car if he hadn’t drunk so much.
  • She wouldn’t have left, if they hadn’t been so rude to her.
  • We wouldn’t have chosen this hotel, if we had read the reviews.
  • If he had passed his exams, he would have become a doctor.
  • She would have been here earlier but her train was delayed.
  • I would have called you but my phone had no battery.
