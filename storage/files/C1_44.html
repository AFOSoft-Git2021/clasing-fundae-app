Acronyms for Business

TBH (To Be Honest)

MEANING:  telling the truth

SENTENCES: 

  • To be honest, the house is not quite our style. 

  • To be honest, I don't like him very much.

  • TBH, I hate video games.

  • I'd rather get it out the way, TBH. 

NBD (No Big Deal)

MEANING: used to indicate that something is of little consequence.

SENTENCES:

  • Still, it was no big deal, she tried to soothe her conscience.

  • It's no big deal . Everybody forgets things sometimes.

  • I’d like to work out today, but if I can’t it’s no big deal.

  • We can talk about it later, NBD.

  • The internet is freaking out about this wardrobe "malfunction", but in our humble opinion it's really NBD.

BC (Because)

MEANING: for the reason that; since.

SENTENCES:

  • We can't go to Julia's party because we're going away that weekend.

  • Just because I'm lending you my dress for tonight doesn't mean you can borrow it whenever you want to.

  • He hated being in the army because he had to obey commands.

  • The trip was quite quick because the road was clear.

  • I didn't tell her that he was late because I didn't want to cause her any alarm.

  • Patients were discharged from the hospital because the beds were needed by other people.

  • I almost missed my flight because there was a long line in the duty-free shop.

  • I wasn't in school BC I was sick.

  • I succeeded BC I willed it; I never hesitated. 

SMH (Shaking My Head)

MEANING:  a type of chat and Internet slang for user expression. It often effectively signals someone's disappointment or disgust for something, or other similar negative emotional reactions.

SENTENCES:

  • I quickly declined his offer by shaking my head and putting my hands above my head in mock surrender.

  • I could not bear shaking my head and smiling a little at his ignorance.

  • I held up the sweaters, turning from side to side, shaking my head.

  • This week has been too damn much. SMH.

  • I lost my phone last night and now I’m just SMH.

OMW (On My Way)

MEANING: Common way of telling someone that you are going to the person.

SENTENCES:

  • Unfortunately, the tire blew out on my way to the office.

  • I'll stop off at the shops on my way home and get some wine.

  • I was already on my way when it suddenly came to me that I hadn't hung out my washing.

  • I'll stop at the fishmonger's on my way home from work.

  • I'm OMW. Just left the office. See you in a bit.

LMK (Let Me Know)

MEANING: Keep me informed, tell me.

SENTENCES:

  • Give me a call to let me know you've arrived safely.

  • Let me know if you run into any snags.

  • He didn't even bother to let me know he was coming.

  • If you accept, please let me know.

  • Lmk if you want to go to the movies tomorrow.

  • Hey, can you lmk if we have milk in the fridge?

BRB (Be Right Back)

MEANING: Used to indicate the speaker will return in a moment.

SENTENCES:

  • I'll duck out to get something to eat and be right back.

  • I'm going to run downstairs and grab some books and stuff - I'll be right back.

  • Angelita, announcing that she will be right back, walks out of the room.

  • I have to take this call. BRB.

  • Oh my mom needs my attention, BRB

B2W (Back To Work)

MEANING: to return to work, to recommence working, to carry on working, to begin working again. 

SENTENCES:

  • I was eager to get back to work as soon as possible.

  • She went back to work full-time when her youngest child went to school.      

  • I muttered something about needing to get back to work.

  • Louise went back to work feeling rather out of sorts.

  • A low ting salary staracts as a strong disincentive to getting back to work for the unemployed.

  • I wish I could chat longer, but I gotta get B2W.

FYI (For Your Information)

MEANING: It is often used in both personal and business correspondence to show that information is simply being shared and that no immediate action is required or expected.

SENTENCES:

  • FYI, I don't even have a car.

  • For your information, I've worked as a journalist for six years.

  • I'm perfectly able to look after myself, FYI.

  • For your information, we are located in a beautiful seaside city.

IMO (In My Opinion)

MEANING: a statement in order to indicate that it is what you or someone else thinks, and is not necessarily a fact.

SENTENCES:

  • IMO, the decision was wholly justified.

  • In my opinion, this is Rembrandt's greatest work.

  • It's a terrible shame, in my opinion , that the building was knocked down.

  • He seemed a bit biased against women in my opinion.

  • Pineapple doesn’t belong on pizza IMO.

  • The red car is a better choice IMO.

ICYMI (In Case You Missed It)

MEANING: when they want to point you in the direction of something interesting or enlightening that's available for your perusal online.

SENTENCES:

  • In case you missed it, here's another post about coffee and poverty, focused on Haiti.

  • In case you missed it, these are the five biggest fashion trends for summer.

  • ICYMI, here's a link to her column in last Saturday's newspaper.

ATM (At The Moment)

MEANING: at the present time; now. 

SENTENCES:

  • The health-care system is in transition at the moment.

  • We are working under extreme pressure at the moment.

  • The Prime Minister is visiting Japan at the moment.

  • Cauliflowers are very cheap at the moment.

  • Sorry, I can't talk right now. I'm busy ATM. 

BAE (Before Anyone Else)

MEANING: it means that he or she is your top priority, that you care about that person more than everyone else.

SENTENCES:

  • I put my wife and kids before anyone else.

  • Estrada continued his hot streak by boating two fish before anyone else.

  • I advise you to stand in the ceremony, BAE.

OOO (Out Of Office)

MEANING: an auto-reply email that notifies people you're not at work for an extended period of time.

SENTENCES:

  • Remember that if a perfect stranger emails you, they will also receive your OOO.

  • Many people said that setting their OOO feels as if vacation is officially starting.

  • 66% of respondents said they like seeing creative OOOs from other people.

  • The minister was drummed out of office when it was discovered that he had been taking bribes.

  • The party has been out of office for many years.

  • After being voted out of office, Rios Montt seemed consigned to political obscurity.

PTO (Paid Time Off)

MEANING: Generally used to describe any period of time that an employee is paid while taking leave from work.

SENTENCES:

  • I may not be able to reply to you immediately because I am on PTO.

  • Vacation, holiday and personal time hours are accrued as PTO hours. 

  • The employee requesting such extended bereavement leave shall be allowed to utilize any Paid Time Off that she has accrued and earned.

ETA (Estimated Time Of Arrival)

MEANING: the time you expect to arrive.

SENTENCES:

  • Our ETA is 10 minutes from now.

  • The flight's ETA is 6:00 p.m.

  • The announcement contains estimated time of arrival, local time and weather conditions.

SOW (Scope Of Work)

MEANING: 

SENTENCES:

  • However, as this is beyond their scope of work they are seeking public-private partnerships to develop the technology.

  • Transportation officials say that will be almost impossible this time because the scope of work is greater.

  • I'm in the process of modifying the SOW, Mr. Fussel said.

  • Mr. Lovallo declined to put a price tag on the project, in part because the full SOW has yet to be determined.

EOD (End Of Day)

MEANING: used especially in business, to mean the end of the working day.

SENTENCES:

  • There is an escalation on that. I need results by EOD.

  • EOD is used to set a deadline for a task that should be completed by the end of the business day, typically 5:00 PM.

  • Of course I'll listen to what she has to say but at the end of the day, it's my decision.

ASAP (As soon as possible)

MEANING: without delay; promptly

SENTENCES:

  • This outstanding issue needs to be dealt with ASAP.

  • If there are any needs, please contact me ASAP, thanks!

  • Please bear with me and I will get around to it as soon as possible.

  • The colonel ordered, I want two good engines down here as soon as possible..

RSVP (répondez s'il vous plaît)

MEANING: used at the end of a written invitation to mean that you should let the people who invited you know whether or not you are coming.

SENTENCES:

  • I got a party invitation in the mail, and I called to RSVP.

  • Be sure to include your name, phone number or email address for the RSVP, date and location of the BBQ as well as a rain date.

B2B (Business To Business)

MEANING: describing or involving business arrangements or trade between different businesses, rather than between businesses and the general public.

SENTENCES:

  • Mr. Pirouz is confident about the potential for B2B e-commerce.

  • B2B helps exporters search for and contact possible importers of their products.

  • E-commerce is the wave of the future, whether in the form of business-to-business or business-to-consumer.

  • We are developing business-to-business connections worldwide, and international passenger traffic is up 20 percent over last year, another record-breaker.

B2C (Business To Consumer)

MEANING: as in a transaction that takes place between a business and an individual as the end customer.

SENTENCES:

  • We would not rule out the possibility of reentering the B2C market if conditions change.

  • The Web was designed to help people get information out of computers, so it's perfect for B2C.

  • Both amounts exist entirely as trade revenue that arose from business-to-business transactions rather than business-to-consumer transactions of subscription services.

  • A number of people are familiar with the concept of business-to-consumer direct mail advertising campaigns.

  • Bankruptcy mailing lists may be used for business to business or business to consumer marketing.

IDK (I Don't Know)

MEANING: used to say that one does not have the information someone is asking for.

SENTENCES:

  • I should get milk, but idk if the store is open now.

  • Idk what you mean.

  • IDK if I've ever seen someone fit in with their partner's family so perfectly.

  • I don't know what happened in the exams.I just freaked out.

  • Her first name was Mary. I don't know what her surname was.

  • They were so close together that I don't know how it was able to scrape through.

  • I don't know, however many notices I put up, people still park in my space.

IDC (I Don't Care)

MEANING: usually used in emails to ignore something.

SENTENCES:

  • If you remember me, then I don't care if everyone else forgets.

  • I pretend that I don't care about you, but still I feel the pain.

  • IDC about other questions and I just try to be myself.

  • IDC, she replied with a toss of her head.

CEO (Chief Executive Officer)

MEANING: the person with the most important position in a company.

SENTENCES:

  • Selling and Administrative units are headed-up by Senior Vice Presidents who report directly to the CEO.

  • She became a CEO at 37, making it to the top quicker than anyone imagined.

  • The CEO decided to reshuffle the company's top management.

  • He is giving up the title of president but will remain chairman and chief executive officer.

  • She retired last year after serving as chief executive officer for more than ten years.

  • In 2009 he was appointed chief executive officer of the Forest Group.

IIRC (If I Remember Correctly)

MEANING: to show that you believe something is correct.

SENTENCES:

  • IIRC, you left your keys on the couch again.

  • IIRC, I was the last one to clean out the fridge.

  • She was from Henan, if I remember correctly.

  • If I remember correctly, you were supposed to collect the keys on your way here.

  • If I remember correctly, time of delivery is another point on which we differ.

KPI (Key Performance Indicator)

MEANING: a way of measuring something that is crucial to the success of the business.

SENTENCES:

  • Daily KPI checklist record to ensure any errors can be tracked.

  • We have to ensure that KPI and corporate objectives are met.

  • The company has 63 key performance indicators, half of them safety-related.

  • These key performance indicators demonstrate continued strong operational performance.

  • The Board monitors several key performance indicators for the business on a monthly basis.

  • Although the group is taking steps to meet its own key performance indicators, it still has a long way to go.

YTD (Year to date)

MEANING: means the total of something from the first day of the year through the current date.

SENTENCES:

  • Year-to-date, sales are down by about 14 percent at 492 million units. 

  • The income statement account usually wants to be reflected by their monthly total or by the year-to-date.

  • Today this site advertised, you can input at any time in a year-to-date information and search for talent recruitment, recruitment and job content can be increased and modified at any time.

  • Selecting this causes the Approved and YTD Adjustments fields to be unavailable for entry.

  • U.S. bond funds over $2 billion in net new cash, putting the YTD inflows past the $17 billion mark.

TBD (To Be Determined)

MEANING: used when something has not yet been decided or is not yet known.

SENTENCES:

  • We suggest $50k per month, plus options and reasonable expenses, TBD.

  • Cost is $50, and the venue TBD.

  • The game has been postponed until next week, time TBD.

  • A date for the meeting has yet to be determined.

  • Qualification for the pension was to be determined by newly-appointed district pensions committees.

  • The biological activity of these compounds remains to be determined.

ROI (Return On Investment)

MEANING: is a metric used to understand the profitability of an investment. ROI compares how much you paid for an investment to how much you earned to evaluate its efficiency. 

SENTENCES:

  • The company insists that the debt is supportable and the return on investment is attractive. 

  • The return on investment was disappointing because the spending was not matched by far-reaching public sector reform. 

  • They are looking for a return on investment and interoperability.

  • Boost your ROI on your marketing, sales and e - commerce activities.

  • Finally, Google uses conversion data in aggregate for the overall benefit of advertisers and their ROI.
