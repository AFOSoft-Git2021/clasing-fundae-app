Crime List Vocabulary: 

Abduction

MEANING: The act of making a person go somewhere with you, especially using threats or violence.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • There has been a series of abductions of young children from schools in the area.

  • He would spend hours helping authorities search the area and would soon become a key witness in the high-profile abduction of the 11-year-old boy.

  • The foundation will use animal-assisted therapy, along with other support services to treat families recovering from abduction and the aftermath of traumatic experiences.

  • The girls' "parents" have been charged with abduction and placed in detention, awaiting trial.

Aiding and abetting

MEANING: To help or encourage someone to do something wrong or illegal.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Three tax inspectors were accused of aiding and abetting the men charged with fraud.

  • Christian’s wife was sentenced to seven years imprisonment for aiding and abetting him.

  • The appellant's wife was acquitted of aiding and abetting the appellant on four counts and discharged.

  • You'll be facing a Federal rap for aiding and abetting an escaped convict.

Arson

MEANING: The crime of intentionally starting a fire in order to damage or destroy something, especially a building.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • A movie theater was burned down in North London last night. Police suspect arson.

  • After an arson fire destroyed one of the metal houses, the fire inspector withdrew his support for the project.

  • Party workers have been rallying around since last Thursday night's arson attack which caused tens of thousands worth of damage.

  • Police are appealing for witnesses to an arson attack on an apartment block.

Assassination

MEANING: to murder a famous or important person, esp. for political reasons or in exchange for money.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • President Kennedy's assassination had far-reaching repercussions.

  • The assassination could do serious damage to the fragile peace agreement that was signed last month.

  • The assassination of Martin Luther King in 1968 was followed by that of Robert Kennedy.

  • It is believed that they plotted the assassination of the king.

  • The president survived a number of assassination attempts.

Assault

MEANING: A violent attack; a threat to injure someone followed by a violent attack on them

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The number of indecent assaults has increased alarmingly over the past year.

  • She died heroically during an assault on the world's second-highest mountain.

  • Police are hunting a student attacker after an unprovoked assault in a nightclub.

  • They launched an assault on the capital yesterday.

  • Assaults on hospital staff have increased over the last year.

  • A woman and a man have been convicted of assaulting a police officer.

  • University officials are concerned about a series of assaults on teachers and students near the campus.

Bigamy

MEANING: The crime of marrying a person while already legally married to someone else.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • In court, he admitted that he had committed bigamy.

  • Adultery or bigamy as grounds means that fidelity is a part of the public vision of marriage.

  • It is clear that the deceiver is not guilty of bigamy on these grounds; he might not be married to anyone himself, much less two people.

  • Gil was then arrested and faced numerous charges including bigamy, assault causing bodily injury, interference with child custody and continuous trafficking of persons, jail records show.

Blackmail

MEANING: The act of getting money from people or forcing them to do something by threatening to tell a secret of theirs or to harm them.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • They were villains who resorted to threats and blackmail to get what they wanted.

  • They used the photographs to blackmail her into spying for them.

  • Large corporations can be vulnerable to blackmail demands by computer hackers.

  • I thought he was trying to blackmail me into saying whatever he wanted.

  • The foreign minister declared in a statement to the press that he would never submit to blackmail.

Bombing

MEANING: An attack or attacks on a place or area using bombs, or the activity of attacking in this way.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • There was a wave of bombings in the south of the country.

  • Police have not so far been able to extort a confession from the people accused of the bombing.

  • The south-west of the country suffered an intensive bombing campaign.

  • The whole community was astir when the news came that the enemy bombing would be restored.

  • Whole villages were wiped out in the bombing raids.

Bribery

MEANING: An attempt to make someone do something for you by giving the person money, presents, or something else that they want.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Charges against the five men include bribery, extortion, and conspiracy.

  • Some cultures consider tipping waiters bribery, while in others it’s considered an insult if you don’t leave a tip.

  • The court has dismissed all remaining charges in the bribery scandal.

  • Large profits from organized crime provide ample funds for bribery of public officials.

  • The largest store in the country was caught up in bribery allegations.

Burglary

MEANING: The crime of illegally entering a building and stealing things.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Several men were questioned by police yesterday about the burglary.

  • Police are investigating a spate of burglaries in the Kingsland Road area.

  • Many people in the neighborhood have been victims of burglary at least once.

  • Many victims of burglary feel their homes have been defiled.

  • The police officer took down all the particulars of the burglary.

Child abuse

MEANING : Child abuse is not just physical violence directed at a child. It is any form of maltreatment by an adult, which is violent or threatening for the child. This includes neglect.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Cases of child abuse often go unreported or ignored by uninformed citizens.

  • Each state statute that mandates reporting of child abuse or neglect specifies the procedures reporters are required to follow.

  • Media reports of child abuse cases often express this sense of moral outrage.

  • The circular and mutually reinforcing nature of the social production of facts about child abuse can be illustrated diagrammatically.

Corruption

MEANING: illegal, bad, or dishonest behavior, especially by people in positions of power:

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The film is about a young police officer and his struggle to expose corruption in the force.

  • Political corruption is widespread throughout the country.

  • The investigation revealed political chicanery and corruption at the highest levels.

  • Recent discoveries about corruption have done serious damage to the company's reputation.

  • He resigned from the committee in disgust at the corruption.

Cybercrime

MEANING: crime or illegal activity that is done using the internet

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • With government's pushing for backdoors and trying to weaken our security in other ways, ransomware and other cybercrime is only going to increase.

  • Cybercrime is a new type of crime, having distinguishable and unique characteristics.

  • The first international legal instrument on the subject was the Council of Europe's Convention on Cybercrime.

  • We express our commitment to cooperate for strengthening international information security. We will pay special attention to combat cybercrime.

Desertion

MEANING: the act of leaving the armed forces without permission

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • During the war, desertion was punishable by death.

  • There were thousands of desertions in the last weeks of the war.

  • The loss of troops from disease and desertion had reduced his effective fighting strength to about half.

  • If there was no threat of repercussions then a great number of individuals would choose desertion if called upon to deploy into a combat zone.

  • Divorce, desertion, and illegitimacy were considered almost unmentionable scandals.

  • Apart from desertion the other force which continually sapped and depleted the strength of every navy was disease.

Domestic violence

MEANING: The situation in which someone you live with attacks you and tries to hurt you.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • There were about 150 articles in major newspapers about the issue of domestic violence.

  • The women had all experienced some form of domestic violence.

  • The report also examined criminal statutes governing domestic violence in all 50 states.

  • We have helped raise millions of dollars for education and awareness of all sorts of issues, including domestic violence.

  • The offer included a $ 250 fine, community service and domestic violence counseling.

Drug dealing

MEANING: The activity of selling illegal drugs.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Neighbors say drug dealing has become more common in the area in recent months.

  • Police often concentrate patrols in poor neighborhoods with high crime rates and street-corner drug dealing.

  • Some school systems that once associated cellphones with drug dealing and banned them from campuses are reviewing their policies.

  • The law was amended so that profits from drug dealing could be seized by the government.

  • The police say they acted after hearing reports of drug dealing in the run up to a warehouse party.

Drunk driving 

MEANING: The act of driving a vehicle after drinking too much alcohol.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • I thought everyone knew that talking on the phone is as bad, maybe worse, than drunk driving.

  • Drunk driving arrests are on the rise among women as are emergency room visits for alcohol-related accidents, according to traffic surveys.

  • The courts often let those convicted of drunk driving serve their time on weekends.

  • The council wants to stiffen the penalty for drunk driving.

  • A government official's bratty son ran over a student while drunk driving near Hebei University.

  • Drunk driving fatalities have declined more than 10 percent over the past 10 years.

Embezzlement

MEANING: The crime of secretly taking money that is in your care or that belongs to an organization or business you work for.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • They were arrested for embezzlement of company funds.

  • He's accused of tax evasion, embezzlement, and fraud.

  • Her lawyer declined to comment about the embezzlement charge she faces.

  • In those early years, pillage and embezzlement had already led to fearful disasters.

  • We have never had a problem with embezzlement at this bank.

  • She was charged with being an accessory to the embezzlement of funds.

Espionage

MEANING: The discovery of secrets, especially political or military information of another country or the industrial information of a business.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Industrial espionage was high on the list of Soviet priorities.

  • The country needs a strong intelligence service to counter espionage and foreign extremists.

  • Three months later, the Soviets convicted him of espionage.

  • The authorities have arrested several people suspected of espionage.

  • The country needs a strong intelligence service to counter espionage and foreign extremists.

Forgery

MEANING: An illegal copy of a document, painting, etc. or the crime of making such illegal copies.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • As recommended, he called the bank to confirm that the bankers' draft was not a forgery.

  • She was indicted on 12 counts of forgery, one count of theft and one count of tampering with evidence.

  • She wanted them to have unique serial numbers and some way to protect against forgeries.

  • They knew the painting was a forgery. Moreover, they knew who had painted it.

  • Refusal in payment, forgery or any other misuse thereby became treason or sacrilege and attracted savage penalties.

Fraud

MEANING: The crime of getting money by deceiving people.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • He is fighting extradition to Hong Kong to face trial on fraud charges.

  • The fraud depended on hundreds of bank accounts being opened on behalf of straw men.

  • His accountant had aided and abetted him in the fraud.

  • She was a psychic who was later revealed to be a fraud.

  • Convicted of tax fraud, he was sentenced to two years in prison.

  • The intervention has drawn criticism from a prominent conservative-leaning group combating election fraud.

Gangsterism

MEANING: The use of tactics associated with gangsters, as intimidation or violence, in order to achieve something

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Gangsterism accumulated wealth by means of violence terror and bribery; permeated the power departments of the state in the meantime.

  • I just repeated the story about my speech on the scourge of gangsterism.

  • This book blew the lid off the secret corruption and gangsterism in these unions.

  • Blood feuds and general gangsterism added to the local crime rate.

Genocide

MEANING: The crime of intentionally destroying part or all of a national, ethnic, racial, or religious group, by killing people or by other methods.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The country is still recovering from one of the worst genocides in modern times.

  • Genocide was first introduced as a legal concept at the Nuremberg Trials of 1945-46.

  • They have alleged that acts of genocide and torture were carried out.

  • He is charged with 20 war crimes, including genocide and crimes against humanity.

  • The fugitives, two of whom have been recaptured, are accused of genocide, mass murder and other crimes.

Hijacking

MEANING: To take control of an aircraft or other vehicle during a trip, especially using violence.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Two men hijacked a jet traveling to Paris and demanded $125,000.

  • The hijack ended with the release of all the plane's passengers unharmed.

  • The association is annoyed that its campaign has been hijacked by pin-striped PR men.

  • Hundreds of the department's powerful computers were hijacked by hackers, who used them to send spam emails.

Hit and run

MEANING: A road accident in which the driver who caused the accident drives away without helping the other people involved and without telling the police.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The 24-year-old was the victim of a hit and run, killed by a speeding truck.

  • She was charged in relation to a hit and run after she allegedly left the scene of an accident.

  • A taxi ran over his foot and seriously damaged his leg and knee. It was a hit and run.

  • I would expect my employer to fire me if I was in a hit and run and fled the scene.

  • This wasn't a hit and run and we have been speaking with the driver of the vehicle.

Homicide

MEANING: (an act of) murder.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The number of homicides in the city has risen sharply.

  • We hung out with a homicide detective, and we tried to make it realistic.

  •   Prosecutors have said that possible charges include negligent homicide, involuntary manslaughter and second-degree murder.

  • The homicide rate continued to rise until it was triple even that of the United States.

  • The woman, who gave police a description of the killer, is the only known eyewitness to the unsolved homicide.

Hooliganism

MEANING : The behavior of a violent person who fights or causes damage in public places

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Hooliganism has been a problem at past tournaments.

  • Violence is not at the level of hooliganism at European and South American soccer games.

  • The government has threatened to crack down on hooliganism during the World Cup finals.

  • In the future people can go to a football match knowing that they are safe from hooliganism.

  • I would have thought it is very dangerous to get football hooliganism mixed up with mugging.

  • He committed hooliganism even under the watchful eyes of the people.

Identity theft

MEANING: The crime of using someone's personal information in order to pretend to be them and to get money or goods in their name.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Statistics show that millions of people are victims of identity theft each year.

  • The consequences of identity theft can be devastating.

  • Our first defense in combating the costly crime of identity theft is teaching consumers to safeguard their personal information.

  • He said that more than $49 billion a year is lost because of identity theft.

  • In cases of identity theft and illegal credit card use, it's especially important to report it and get a police report.

Kidnapping

MEANING: An occasion when someone is kidnapped.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Two youngsters have been arrested and charged with kidnapping.

  • The kidnapping charge was dropped because of lack of evidence.

  • The attack was in reprisal for the kidnapping of their leaders.

  • She appeared in court on charges of kidnapping and assault.
