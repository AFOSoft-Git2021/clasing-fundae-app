PASSIVE INFINITIVE with -ING: 

The passive -ing form is used to express a continuous action.

Passive voice in the present progressive tense is created with the present tense form of the verb to be (am, is, are)  + being + the 3rd form of the verb (eaten, done, begun, etc.) . 

The progressive infinitive is used to suggest that actions and events are/were/will be continuing around the time we are talking about.

EXAMPLES

  • I don't like being cheated.

  • He remembers being given the book.

  • The music is being played in my room.

  • They are being taught.

  • My computer is being fixed.

  • His book is being published soon.

  • The reports are being given to me by my boss tomorrow. 

  • A vacation is not being taken by us this year.

  • I’d really like to be swimming in a nice cool pool right now.

  • You must be joking!

  • I happened to be waiting for the bus when the accident happened.

  • Tom is believed to be leaving the company.

  • The prices are said to be rising.

  • They are rumored to be planning a new strike this week. 

  • He is believed to be staying in Paris for five days.

  • She is believed to have been writing songs for years.

  • The organizers are thought to have been preparing the exhibition for days.

  • She had been searching for clues for hours before bedtime.

  • A new supermarket is being built in the city center.

  • My articles are being typed at the moment.

  • Your suggestions are being followed up by our committee.

  • Computers are being used to sift the information.

  • The children are being cared for by a relative.

  • A dropped cigarette is being blamed for the fire.

  • The provision of specialist teachers is being increased.

  • A hotel room is being prepared for them.

  • The story is being whispered about.

  • Nell is being touted as the next big thing in Hollywood.

  • A peer review system is being introduced to help teachers who are experiencing difficulty.

  • An attempt is being made to entice others back to the river.
