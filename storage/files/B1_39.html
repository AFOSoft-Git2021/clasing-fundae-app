Education

VOCABULARY MEANING SENTENCES
education the process that involves teaching and learning, particularly at a school, university, or college a day with those kids was an education in patience and forbearance
to educate to teach someone, particularly a child, especially at a school or university Parents trust schools to educate their children.
educated

 having received a good education

 She was educated in the US.
academic

 related to education, particularly higher education

 The school strives for academic excellence.
assignment

 a task given to a student to do

 I've been working on my assignment all day.
classwork

 tasks that are given to students to do in class, and not at home

Will there be classwork and homework on the first day?
campus

 an area of land in which a university, college, or school, along with all their buildings, are situated

 The campus of Harvard University is very beautiful.
diploma

 a certificate given to someone who has completed a course of study

Leticia is taking a diploma in management studies.
higher education

 education at a university or similar educational institution that grants one an academic degree at the end

 The university awards diplomas in higher education.
adult education

 classes for adults to finish their education, held in the evening or over the Internet

 Adult education helps people achieve more fulfilled lives.
private school

 a school that is funded by the parents of the students instead of the government

 I can't afford to send my kid to private school.
kindergarten

 a class or school that prepares four-year-old to six-year-old children for elementary school

 The kindergarten children were captivated by the variety of animals in the zoo.
to graduate

 to finish a university or college degree successfully, particularly the first degree

 I plan to graduate in July.
undergraduate

 a student who is trying to complete their first degree in college or university

 She had an undergraduate degree in psychology.
graduation

 the action of successfully finishing studies at a US high school or a university degree

 After graduation Neil returned to Ohio.
degree

 the certificate that is given to university or college students upon successful completion of their course

 She graduated from Cambridge with a degree in law.
lecturer

 a university or college teacher

 The lecturer tried to settle the audience down.
pupil

 someone who is receiving education, particularly a schoolchild

 The pupil leaned to the ground to pick up a wallet.
semester

 each of the two periods into which a year at schools or universities is divided

 Tony flunked chemistry last semester.
period

 each part into which a day is divided at a school, university, etc.

 This offer is available for a limited period only.
to review

 to study or practice taught lessons again, particularly to prepare for an examination

 The terms of the contract are subject to review.
to attend

 to go to school, university, church, etc. periodically

 Your job is to attend the meeting and report back to the board of directors.
to drop out

 to stop going to school, university, or college before finishing one's studies

 My father accepted my decision to drop out of school.
to examine

 to test a person's knowledge or skills in a certain subject by asking them questions or asking them to do a specific task

 I need to examine all possible contingencies.
exercise

 a series of questions in a book set to test one's knowledge or skill

 The exercises that the teacher gave were so challenging and difficult.
examination

 a written, practical, or spoken test of knowledge or skill in a subject or field, particularly a formal one

 The examination results will be announced in September.
quiz

 a short test given to students

 The teacher has a quiz on last week's work.
tuition

 an amount of money that one pays to receive an education, particularly in a university or college

 University students rioted in protest at tuition fees.
genius

 someone who is very smart or is very skilled in a specific activity

 Nerea is a genius teacher.
home study

 a course of study carried out at home rather than in a classroom

The course that she enrolled for is only suitable for home study only.

Scholarship

 a grant or payment made to support a student's education, awarded on the basis of academic or other achievement

 Tim held a Humboldt scholarship.
Law school

 A University where people study law.

 She finished law school last year.
Medical school

 A university where people study medicine.

 Kate graduated from medical school last year.
Online course

 An online course is a course that is focused on the use of information and communications technology for learning.

 Our online courses are open to the public for immediate enrolment.
Vocational course

 A course which teaches you the skills required for a specific job. For example: welding, tailoring etc.

 The vocational course in architecture seeks to equip students with the knowledge and skills needed to enter the profession.
Non-vocational course

 A course that is not related to any particular job, but to a general subject like Geography or Biology.

 We do not offer Non-vocational courses at this time.

Tutorials

 A period of study with a tutor involving one student or a small group

 He gives one lecture and two tutorials a week.
Thesis

 A piece of writing that involves an original idea on the study of a subject for a college or university degree.

 He wrote his doctoral thesis on contemporary French literature.
Research

 A careful study of a subject, especially in order to discover new facts or information about it.

 The research project was only a partial success.
Student loan

 An amount of money loaned by students, that they must pay back after graduating.

 She had to take out a student loan to help her through college.
