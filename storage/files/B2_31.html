Vocabulary for New Technologies


Access to the Internet

Able to connect to the Internet

  • Access to the Internet did not much excite these older buyers either.
  • Around the world, access to the Internet is fast becoming a necessity for individuals and organizations.


Advances in technology 

Progressive forward movement in technology

  • Future advances in technology may disclose other, more sensitive markers of cell proliferation whose predictive accuracy is greater.
  •  The advances in technology have made much information easily and instantaneously available.


Backup your files 

To save your data to another device

  • Make sure to always backup your files. 


Computer literate 

Adequate knowledge of a computer

  • It is important that all children become computer literate while they are in school.
  • You can demonstrate a program for a corporate executive but you can make him computer literate .


Control remotely 

To control technology from a distance

  • In addition, the transmission circuit of small signal is designed to suit the signal to transmit and control remotely.


Emerging technology 

Brand new machines and software

  •  One beneficiary of this emerging technology is the leatherback sea turtle, which marine biologists usually identify by conventional plastic tags.
  • There is one other emerging technology that may go the whole way.


Internet access 

The ability to enter the Internet

  • Mitsubishi and other companies offer Internet access from cellular phones.
  • The technology of high-speed Internet access on cable systems remains highly promising too.


Shop online 

Shop via the Internet

  •  Sales are now being led by a younger generation that is willing to buy on credit and shop online.


To crash 

To stop working suddenly

  • Our communications software tends to crash the system.


Wireless hotspots 

A location where the Internet is available

  • I am using the same computer as before, except now at a public wireless hotspot.
  • It is a wireless hotspot, provides access to over 100 full productivity computer workstations, a variety of student work spaces and group rooms, and the Writing Centre.


Digitize

The process of making something digital.

  • Digitizing books into an eBook device was a brilliant idea.
  •  To create special effects, engineers digitize the film and then work with it on the computer.
  • The digitized images can be stored on a computer hard disk, or printed out on special photographic paper.


Buffer

Temporary, short term storage of data in a memory bank while transferring a large amount of data.

  • I don’t even remember what was on that test. I studied for it using the buffer method.
  • The computer stores data in a buffer until the printer can accept it.


Hi-res

Refers to high quality that shows a lot of detail.

  • His stories are so hi-res, it feels like I’m there.


Web address

The web address contains information about the location of the webpage. It is also known as the URL (uniform resource locator).

  • When you submit your web address , it will be added to the job queue.
  • The secret lies in those cryptic symbols that begin every web address.


Smartphone

A mobile phone that performs many of the functions of a computer, typically having a touchscreen interface, internet access, and an operating system capable of running downloaded applications.

  • There are plans in place to accommodate those who do not have a smartphone and cannot use the app.
  • Enhance your SmartPhone with this scientific calculator.


A laptop

A computer that is small enough to be carried around easily and is flat when closed

  • A laptop would be really useful for when I'm working on the train.
  • If you have a laptop, just connect to the hotel internet service.
  • Come close and a laptop PC is thrust under your nose.


A mouse pad

The special flat piece of material on which you move the mouse of a computer.

  • Make a digital scrapbook layout featuring a favorite of the two of you into a mouse pad or coffee mug by ordering from a site such as Shutterfly or Snapfish.
  • Its walls remain bare and her desk is mostly empty except for a silver laptop with a leopard-patterned mouse pad.


A printer

A machine that is connected to a computer and prints onto paper using ink.

  • Unfortunately he bought a printer that was not compatible with his computer.
  • A printer has a carriage which holds and moves the paper.
  • A hard copy of the screen display can also be obtained from a printer.


A scanner

A device for making images of the inside of the body or for reading information into a computer system.

  • The picture is digitized by a scanner.
  • The scan failed because of a scanner hardware error.
  •  You hand your smart card to the cashier at a restaurant and she runs it through a scanner.


A window

A separate area on a computer screen that shows information and can be moved around:

  • The information appeared in a new window.


A password

A secret word or combination of letters or numbers, used for communicating with another person or with a computer to prove who you are.

  • You need a password to log on.
  • You have to enter a password to access the database.
  • A smart card computes a password or encryption key and furnishes it directly to the computer for the logon procedure.


Social network

A website or computer program that allows people to communicate and share information on the internet using a computer or cell phone

  • We teach children how to deal with cyberbullying and the safe use of chat rooms and social networks.
  • Students graduating from high school have social networks they've been building for twelve years.


To upload

To copy or move programs or information to a larger computer system or to the internet

  • I want to upload data to the computer network storage from my office computer.
  • You hope to upload the new photos as soon as possible.
  • You attempted to upload an empty file. Please check your typing.


Link

 connection between documents on the internet

  • I'll send you the link to our online forum.
  • Please click on the compatible browsers link below for more information.
  • I also added a hidden link to each page that will allow search bots to check each page.


To log in

To connect to a computer system by putting in a particular set of letters or numbers

  • What is the username and password that you use to log in?
  • In order to log in you have to type in a special password.
  •  The first user to log in becomes the administrator automatically; all subsequent users that log in are non-privileged users.


Toolbar

On a computer screen, a row of icons ( small pictures that you choose in order to make the computer do something)

  • A toolbar across the top of the page includes buttons with one-click access to other sites.
    • If you click on a unit on the toolbar and you have enough resources, the unit will respawn.
    • No one would ever install such a terrible toolbar by choice.


    Drag and drop

    If you drag and drop something on a computer screen, you move it from one area to another using the mouse

    • Drag and drop files as required.
    • I usually just drag and drop files to attach them to messages.
    • You can open a folder and choose files for upload, or you can drag and drop them directly into the upload area.

    To surf

    To spend time looking at websites

    • Persuading more subscribers to surf the internet is at the heart of a strategy intended to boost sales.
    • Even new-media producers concede there is considerable expense and technical expertise needed to surf the Internet.


    Underscore (_)

    The character  on a computer keyboard, used to draw a line under a letter or word, and also used in file names where a space is not allowed

    • Use an underscore, or start new words with a capital letter instead.
    • Type an underscore character to add a space in a display name.

    Slash (/)

    The symbol / used in writing to separate letters, numbers, or words

    • You often write a slash between alternatives, for example, and/or.
    • The no-parking sign had a red slash through a picture of a car.


    Touchscreen

    A computer screen that you touch to get information, buy something, etc

    • Customers use a touchscreen to buy tickets.
    • Voters in Georgia will use a touchscreen voting system.


    Inbox

    A place on a computer where emails that are sent to you are kept.

    • Just put it in my inbox and I'll look at it later.
    • If you would like more information please inbox me back.
