Past Perfect Continuous

Past Perfect Continuous Tense represents an ongoing action that started and continued for some time in the past. To indicate time reference “for” and “since” are used and it describes when the action started and how long it continued in the past.

Forming the Past Perfect Progressive Tense

The past perfect continuous is composed of two elements - the past perfect of the verb to be (=had been) + the present participle (base+ing).

Subject +had been +verb + ing
I had been walking
Affirmative
She had been trying
Negative
She hadn't been sleeping
Interrogative
Had you been eating?
Interrogative negative
Hadn't they been living?

Affirmative Statements

To form the past perfect continuous: we use "had" + the past participle of "be" (been) + the -ing form of the main verb.

Note: You can also use contractions:

I had = I'd;

you had = you'd;

he had = he'd;

she had = she'd

we had = we'd;

they had = they'd

EXAMPLES:

  • My grandfather had been feeling great until yesterday.
  • I had been living in the dorms during my first two years at university.
  • She'd been seeing him for a few years before they broke up.
  • I'd been bringing sandwiches to work for years.
  • I could see that he had been crying .
  • My sister has been buying new clothes with her credit cards every month.

Negative Statements

To form the negative, simply add "not" after "had":

  • subject + had + not + been + -ing form of main verb.

We can also use the contractions hadn't (had not = hadn't)

EXAMPLES:

  • They hadn't been speaking to each other for years.
  • They had not been taking good care of their health.
  • Thankfully, I had not been smoking for a year before I got pregnant.
  • I hadn't been drinking alcohol for a long time either.
  • My dog hadn't been sleeping well before he died.
  • Our children had not been doing their chores so we took away their iPads.

Yes / No questions

To form questions use:

  • Had + subject + been + -ing form of main verb ?

EXAMPLES:

  • Had you been dating him for very long?
  • Has that dog been barking a lot in the past few weeks?
  • Had they been sleeping late during the summer?
  • Had Mariebeen trying to lose weight when she got sick?
  • Had you been working at your job for a long time before you quit?
  • Had the children been acting strange?

Wh - questions

Notice the word order. The wh- question wordcomes before "had"and then the past participle of "be" (been) and then -ingof the main verb.

EXAMPLES:

  • How long hadyou been living in Berlin before you got married?
  • Where hadyour father been staying before he went into the nursing home?
  • Why hadthe bus driver been talking on the phone?
  • Who hadthey been telling our company secrets to?
  • What hadSophie's mother been cooking when the fire started?
