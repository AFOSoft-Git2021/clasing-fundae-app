Crime List Vocabulary (part 2): 

Libel

MEANING: a published false statement that is damaging to a person's reputation; a written defamation.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:  

  • They filed a libel lawsuit against the person responsible for the web posting.

  • The whole story was a vicious libel.

  • Angry at what the newspaper had printed, she sued for libel.

  • He was sentenced to 18 months' imprisonment after the collapse of his libel action against the newspaper.

  • The dissolution of the 1626 parliament marked the rise of alarming libels about the duke.

 

Looting

MEANING: The activity of stealing from shops during a violent event.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • There ere reports of widespread looting as hooligans stampeded through the city center

  • A lot of people got arrested for looting so they will think before they do it again.

  • Wild street celebrations turned to looting and several unpopular officials were reportedly lynched.

  • Riots and looting had destroyed most of the retail and industrial areas.

  • hile the area had been designated a National Archaeological Reserve, he expressed fear of looting from the site.

 

Lynching

MEANING: The act of killing someone without a legal trial, usually by hanging (= killing using a rope around the neck).

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • She wrote about the travesty of racially motivated lynching.

  • here was a lynching of one of the suspected terrorists.

  • The lynching of two soldiers was reported.

  • The video showed shocking images of real lynchings.

Manslaughter

MEANING: The crime of killing a person when the killer did not intend to do it or cannot be responsible for his or her actions.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • She was sentenced to five years' imprisonment for manslaughter.

  • Andrew denies murder but admits manslaughter on the grounds of diminished responsibility

  • He was found guilty of manslaughter and sentenced to two years in prison.

  • Capper mitted manslaughter on the grounds of diminished responsibility.

  • A judge accepted her plea that she was guilty of manslaughter, not murder.

ugging

MEANING: An act of attacking someone and stealing their money.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Police are concerned that mugging is on the increase.

  • After that mugging, the relievers did much better work, especially during the last two games against the Yankees.

  • His life is devoted to fights with rival gangs, stabbing, mugging, drinking and taking drugs.

  • I would have thought it is very dangerous to get football hooliganism mixed up with mugging.

Murder

MEANING: The crime of intentionally killing a person.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The newspaper article included a gruesome description of the murder.

  • He was imprisoned in 1945 for the brutal murder of a twelve-year old girl.

  • He has admitted committing several crimes, including two murders.

  • He died in mysterious circumstances, and there is still a possibility that it was murder.

  • She has been charged with conspiracy to murder.

Perjury

MEANING: The crime of telling lies in court when you have promised to tell the truth.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • It was rightly apprehended that if many witnesses were allowed to give evidence at all they would commit perjury, which in fact they do.

  • The fraud and perjury involved officers ranging in rank from detective constable to assistant chief constable.

  • I expect the discrepancy between the two affidavits shortly to become the subject of a police investigation in this country into perjury.

 

Pickpocket

MEANING: A thief who steals things out of pockets or bags, especially in a crowd.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:  

  • The pickpocket was brought to the nearby police station.

  • The policeman clapped hold of the pickpocket and took him to the Station.

  • The old gentleman just stood there glaring at the pickpocket and did not say a word.

Pilfering

MEANING: To steal things of little value or in small amounts.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Neighborhood kids were pilfering candy from the corner store.

  • They were charged with conspiracy to pilfer $600m from a major international conglomerate.

  • U.S. officials claimed the plane's electronic surveillance equipment had been thoroughly examined and pilfered by their military.

  • His pilfering of the fund landed him in prison and destroyed his political career.

 

Poaching

MEANING: The catching and killing of animals without permission on someone else's land; the act of taking and using something, usually an idea, for yourself unfairly or dishonestly.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Poaching poses a huge threat to the survival of this rare animal.

  • Wardens are seeing an alarming rise in poachings of many kinds of animals.

  • The company had to pay compensation for customer poaching from its rival.

  • One possible extension might be to study how poaching and forest degradation impact ecotourism potential along tropical forest margins.

 

Rape

MEANING: (an example of) the crime of forcefully having sex with someone against their wishes; to force someone to have sex when he or she is unwilling.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • She does a lot of counselling with women who've been raped and abused.

  • Ann says the only way to heal after rape is to talk about it.

  • Some of the most disturbed children had witnessed really traumatic things, such as rape and murder.

  • Rape is committed where sexual intercourse takes place without consent.

  • Most rape victims confess a feeling of helplessness.

 

Riot

MEANING: An occasion when a large number of people behave in a noisy, violent, and uncontrolled way in public, often as a protest.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Food protests and riots have erupted in more than 30 countries.

  • Students are rioting in the streets of the capital.

  • The streets in this district include South Central Los Angeles, where the riots broke out two years ago.

Robbery

MEANING: The crime of stealing from somewhere or someone.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The robbery had been recorded on a concealed security camera.

  • Armed robbery and abduction have been on the increase countrywide.

  • A man has been arrested in connection with the robbery.

  • Shop owners are struggling to combat the menace of armed robbery.

  • The robbers decided to whack up the profits from the robbery.

 

Shoplifting

MEANING: The illegal act of taking goods from a shop without paying for them.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • John has been jailed three times for shoplifting.

  • The grocer accused her of shoplifting and demanded to look in her bag.

  • Five months ago she was acquitted on a shoplifting charge.

  • Shoplifting has reached epidemic proportions and this gave rise to a lively discussion.

  • The clerk spotted the girl shoplifting and stopped her from leaving the store.

Slander

MEANING: A false spoken statement about someone that damaged their reputation, or the making of such a statement.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • She regarded his comment as a slander on her good reputation.

  • Many teachers saw the statement as a gross slander on their profession.

  • Johnson's arguments range from ridiculous to borderline slander.

  • Courts in some states treat slander and libel differently.

Smuggling

MEANING: The act or process of taking things or people to or from a place secretly and often illegally.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The murdered man is thought to have been involved in drug smuggling.

  • Nine people are in custody in the Netherlands charged with people smuggling.

  • The seizure represented a breakthrough in the battle against arms smuggling.

  • The UK Coastguard was formed in 1822 to combat smuggling.

  • Many traffickers have set up smuggling rings that rely heavily on young couriers.

Speeding

MEANING: Driving faster than is allowed in a particular area.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The driver had been stopped twice for speeding on the same day.

  • The police said he'd been speeding on the motorway.

  • The policeman let me off with a lecture about speeding.

  • The speeding car barely missed the pedestrian.

Spying

MEANING: A person who secretly collects and reports information about the activities of another country or organization.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Three Britons are facing the death penalty for spying.

  •   He said that someone might be spying on the station and the pilot was ordered to keep track of the strange object.

  • The Government disclosed that another diplomat has been arrested for spying.

  • A former US diplomat has confessed to spying.

  • The phrase is common diplomatic parlance for spying.

Stowing away

MEANING: To hide on a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle in order to escape from a place or to travel without paying.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  •   A shipping clerk who shipped himself from New York to Dallas to visit his parents was charged with stowing away on a cargo jet.

Terrorism

MEANING: (threats of) violent action for political purposes.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Governments must cooperate if they are to fight international terrorism.

  • The bomb explosion was one of the worst acts of terrorism that the country had experienced in recent years.

  • Current legislation provides for the detention of those suspected of terrorism.

  • Every right-minded person is against terrorism.

  • Terrorism is totally unacceptable in a civilized world.

Theft

MEANING: (the act of) dishonestly taking something that belongs to someone else and keeping it.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • She was found guilty of theft, but because of extenuating circumstances was not sent to prison.

  • There's been an increase in minor offenses, such as traffic violations and petty theft.

  • Unfortunately, the police have dealt with the problem of car theft in a reactive rather than a proactive way.

  • He was remanded on theft charges.

  • We rang the insurance company to report the theft.

Trafficking

MEANING : The act of buying or selling goods illegally.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Military personnel determined that the plane had been used for narcotics trafficking.

  • This scandal shows how arms trafficking has grown over the past decade.

  • One problem is the huge amount of trafficking in stolen oil.

  • Heroin trafficking is a major source of funding for the terrorists.

  • An investigation has been launched into possible child trafficking.

Treachery

MEANING: Behavior that deceives or is not loyal to someone who trusts you.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • From his cell on board the ship, the captain wrote about the treachery of his crew.

  • He imagines a critic accusing composers of treachery if they cross certain lines or create something the critic does not understand.

  • The inadequacy and treachery of the old leaderships of the working class have made the need all the more imperative.

  • Adam's apparent lack of concern enraged Mike, as hurt by his brother's treachery as he was by the theft.

Treason

MEANING: (the crime of) showing no loyalty to your country, especially by helping its enemies or trying to defeat its government.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • In 1807, Burr was arrested and tried for treason, but he was acquitted.

  • Richter is accused of committing treason against the state.

  • The defendant was convicted of high treason and sentenced to death.

  • The new law will put official corruption on the same legal footing as treason.

  • Refusal in payment, forgery or any other misuse thereby became treason or sacrilege and attracted savage penalties.

Trespassing

MEANING: To go onto someone's land or enter their building without permission.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • I hope this is a public footpath and we're not trespassing on someone's land.

  • A landlord cannot normally claim that a tenant is trespassing on his property.

  • Carlson was fined $1000 for trespassing on government property.

  • I hope this is a public footpath and we're not trespassing on someone's land.

  • The council sought an injunction to prevent a clergyman trespassing by holding services on the plaintiff's seashore.

Vandalism

MEANING: 

person who intentionally damages property belonging to other people.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The mayor promised to crack down on vandalism.

  • The report states that vandalism is a pestilence which must be stamped out.

  • A drunken teenager was arrested for vandalism.

  • There is some evidence, therefore, that crime and vandalism may be reduced by greater participation in sport.

  • This warehouse is not adequately protected against theft or vandalism.

Voyeurism

MEANING: The activity of getting pleasure from secretly watching other people in sexual situations or, more generally, from watching other people's private lives.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The show veers dangerously close to outright voyeurism.

  • Voyeurism and exhibitionism intertwine in these purposefully provocative scenes.

  • For a very private person, weddings feel like a lavish exercise in voyeurism.
