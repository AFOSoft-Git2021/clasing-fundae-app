Hours

A period of time equal to a twenty-fourth part of a day and night and divided into 60 minutes.

How to tell time on a clock

To tell time on an analog clock, you look at where the hands are pointing.

  • The short or small hand tells you the hour,
  • The long or big hand tells you the minute of the current hour, and<
  • The thinnest hand indicates the seconds of the current minute.<

HOW TO TELL TIME

1. Telling Time in English: Using ‘Past’ or ‘To’

  • As we know, there are 60 minutes in an hour.
  • With minutes 1 – 29, we say it’s past (or after) the hour.
  • With minutes 31 – 59, we say to (the next hour).
TIME HOW TO SAY IT
3:10 ten past three
8:20 It’s twenty after eight
10:40 It's "twenty to eleven" (Eleven minus twenty minutes)
3:50 It’s "ten to four” (Four minus ten minutes)

2. When to Use ‘a Quarter’ and ‘a Half’

We divide our time in quarters and halves. It’s confusing at first, but it does make expressing time easier.

  • At minute 15, we say it’s “quarter past”. Or we say, it’s “quarter after”. Both “quarter past” and “quarter after” are equally correct.<
  • At minute 45, we say it’s “quarter to” the next hour.<
  • At minute 30, we say it’s “half past”.
TIME HOW TO SAY IT
7:15 it’s “quarter past seven”
1:15 it’s “quarter after one”
5:45 it’s “quarter to six” (or 15 minutes before 6:00)
9:30 it’s “half past nine” (or half an hour after 9:00)

3. A.M or P.M: What Time is it?

As we all know, there are 24 hours in a day. Hours 1 through 11 are pretty simple. In the 9th hour, we just say “It’s 9:00”. But hours 12 to 24 might be a little different than what other cultures are accustomed to. “A.M.” stands for “Ante Meridiem” (Latin). And “P.M.” stands for “Post Meridiem” (also Latin).

  • For hours 0 through 12, we call this “A.M.”<
  • For hours 12 to 24, we call this “P.M.”<
TIME HOW TO SAY IT
For hour 7 (in the morning) “It’s seven A.M.” (7:00 A.M.)
At hour 14 “It’s two P.M.” (2:00 P.M.)
At the 17th hour “It’s five P.M.” (5:00 P.M.)

4. Noon. Midday. Midnight

At hour 12, we say it’s “noon”. You also sometimes hear people say “midday”. At hour 24, we say it’s “midnight”.

TIME HOW TO SAY IT
12:00 P.M. 12:00 NOON
12:00 A.M. 12:00 MIDNIGHT (The start of a new day or 24 hour cycle.)

5. When to Use O’clock

At the beginning of each hour, we use the term “o’clock”.

TIME HOW TO SAY IT
1:00 “It’s one o’clock”
4:00 “It’s four o’clock”
8:00 “It’s eight o’clock”

A common mistake that I have noticed with English learners is that they will use the term “o’clock” at the wrong time. We only say “o’clock” at the exact hour.
