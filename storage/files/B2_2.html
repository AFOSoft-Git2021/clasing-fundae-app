Connectors to talk about the Past

Sentence Connector

A word or phrase that introduces a clause or sentence and serves as a transition between it and a previous clause or sentence, 

Example 

I'm buying eggs

I'm looking for a dessert for tonight.

It may be preceded by a coordinating conjunction such as and in the above example

This is used to talk about the past tense, or to refer to something that already happened.


A long time ago 

Meaning: Far in the past : not at all recently

Sample Sentences: 

  • It happened a long time ago.
  • It was all a long time ago and now Ellen found she could forgive and forget.
  • I met your father once, a long time ago.


In ancient times

Meaning: Having had an existence of many years

Sample Sentences:  

  • In ancient times, astronomers named celestial objects after gods and mythical creatures.
  • In ancient times the messages were relayed from one place to another.
  • People made noise in ancient times to drive away the bugaboo from their home.
  • A king in ancient times had many slaves to work for him.



Not long ago

Meaning: During a recent period of time

Sample Sentences:  

  • It's not long ago that they arrived in Britain.
  • I remember seeing a brochure not long ago for a sport-utility vehicle.
  • The newly built railway line was brought into use not long ago.


In former times

Meaning: Preceding in time; prior or earlier.

Sample Sentences:  

  • Norocks, to our knowledge, are untouched by life in former times.
  • In former times parts of India were ruled by princes.
  • In former times the British army kept lots of soldiers at Fort William in Scotland.


Formerly

Meaning: In time past; in an earlier period or age; previously.

Sample Sentences: 

  • Algeria was formerly a French colony.
  • Some of our formerly >loyal members have fallen away.
  • This elegant hotel was formerly a castle.

In the old days

Meaning: In the old days means in the past, before things changed

Sample Sentences: 

  • In the old days, when someone died people wore ribbons and black armbands to signify they had lost someone.
  • In the old days I'd have worried if the manager wanted to see me.
  • In the old days we got a visit from the vet maybe once a year.
