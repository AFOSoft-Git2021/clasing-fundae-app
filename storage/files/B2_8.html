Sentence Connectors to present a conclusion 

This expresses consequences, conclusions, or direct results.

Therefore

Meaning: for that reason

Sample Sentences: 

  • We were unable to get funding and therefore had to abandon the project.
  • The defendant was depressed and therefore not fully responsible for her own actions.
  • We are moral, ethical people and therefore we do not approve of their activities.

Consequently

Meaning:  as a result:

Sample Sentences: 

  • I spent most of my money in the first week and consequently had very little to eat by the end of the vacation.
  • All the stores were closed, and consequently we couldn't buy any food.
  • Doctors are short of time to listen and consequently tend to prescribe drugs whenever they can.

Thus

Meaning:  As a result of the fact that you have just mentioned

Sample Sentences: 

  • The publishers claim that the book constitutes 'the first sequential exposition of events and thus of the history of the revolution'.
  • Thus the Romans left Britain.
  • Exercise made us more hungry and thus our food supplies ran out.

As a result

Meaning: Because of something

Sample Sentences: 

  • Profits have declined as a result of the recent drop in sales.
  • 750 manual workers will lose their jobs as a result of company cutbacks.

In summary

Meaning: A short, clear description that gives the main facts or ideas about something:

Sample Sentences: 

  • In summary, the whole affair was a fiasco.
  • In summary, they decided against the proposal.

Evidently

Meaning: In a way that is easy to see:

Sample Sentences: 

  • He was evidently upset by the news of the accident.
  • A thin although evidently pregnant woman
  • He had an accident, evidently because he was driving at excessive speed.

In the long run

Meaning: at a time that is far away in the future:

Sample Sentences: 

  • It seems like a lot of effort but I'm sure it's the best solution in the long run.
  • You may want to quit school now, but in the long run, you’ll regret it.
  • In the long run, stocks are considered a partial hedge for inflation risk.

Hence

Meaning: used for introducing something that is a result of the fact that has just been stated.

Sample Sentences: 

  • The prime minister was attending the conference, hence all the extra security.
  • He's just got a pay rise, hence the new car.
  • She's just found out she failed her exams, hence her bad mood.

MORE SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • There is inadequate childcare provision and consequently many women who wish to work are unable to do so.
  • The company is trying to reduce its costs; consequently, staff who leave are not being replaced.
  • His explanation was full of technical jargon. Consequently, nobody understood it at all!
  • She is always bad-tempered, and consequently doesn't have many friends.
  • She evidently dresses for comfort and not for style.
  • The president is evidently in a dilemma about how to tackle the crisis.
  • I've been called in to see the director, so evidently I'm in big trouble.
  • The audience evidently weren't enjoying the show and some people began to leave.
  • Evidently her injuries were more severe than it first appeared, and she is now in a coma.
