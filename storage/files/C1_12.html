Present perfect + progressive passive voice

Like most other verb forms in English, the present perfect-progressive passive is periphrastic, a term which means a “phrase of two or more words that perform a single grammatical function that would otherwise be expressed by the inflection of a single word.” 

Verbs in the present perfect-progressive passive are formed by the present tense form of the verb have plus the past participlebeen and the present participlebeingfollowed by a past participle ( regularorirregular).

Note that, as with other passive constructions, only transitive verbs (verbs that can take objects) and verbs with verb phrase complements may be conjugated into the passive voice.

  • The picture is believed to have been being painted for years. 
  • My living room has been being painted for two hours.

The verb phrase patterns for the present perfect-progressive passive are as follows:

  1. first person singular – have + been + being + past participle 
    1. I have been being treated like dirt.
  2. second person singular – have + been + being + past participle
    1. You have been being counted twice.

  3. third person singular – has + been + being + past participle 

    1. The package have been being delivered late.

  4. first person plural – have + been + being + past participle 

    1. We have been being given bad advice.
  5. second person plural – have + been + being + past participle

    1. You have been being controlled by your parents.
  6. third person plural – have + been + being + past participle

    1. The prototypes have been being developed in the lab.

Like the present perfect-progressive in the active voice , the present perfect-progressive passive expresses and emphasizes the consequences resulting from a previous but incomplete action or state that began in the past and continues into the present but may or may not continue into the future. 

Also like the present perfect-progressive active, the present perfect-progressive passive occurs most often in sentences that express actions that occurred recently and actions that continue up to the present.
