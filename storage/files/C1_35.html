Verbs Related to Crime: 

Aid and abet 

MEANING:   Help commit a crime. A lawyer redundancy since abet means aid, which lends credence to the old rumor that lawyers used to be paid by the word. 

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • It's illegal to aid and abet a thief.

  • Monetary policy goals are to aid and abet solid economic growth along with rising living standards.

  • The government has made it very clear all along that they requested from all the neighboring countries not to aid and abet criminals on the escape.

Arrest

MEANING: The act of arresting someone.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Two arrests were made, but the men were later released without charge.

  • She was stopped outside the store and placed/put under arrest.

  • The bomb attack was in retaliation for the recent arrest of two well-known terrorists.

  • His lawyer put him in the picture about what had happened since his arrest.

  • The US lodged a formal protest against the arrest of the foreign reporters.

Assassinate

MEANING:   to kill someone famous or important.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • A plot to assassinate the banker has been uncovered by the police.

  • The danger of psychopaths who freely cross state borders to poison our medicines or to assassinate our leaders is well known.

  • The intelligence service conceived a grand design to assassinate the War Minister.

  • The police claim to have uncovered a plot by terrorists to assassinate the president.

Ban

MEANING: to forbid (= refuse to allow) something, especially officially.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The council plans to ban circuses with performing animals.

  • The proposed new law would ban picketing.

  • The council is making a move to ban traffic in some parts of the city.

  • Certain chemicals have been banned because of their damaging effect on the environment.

  • He's been banned from driving for six months.

Betray

MEANING: to not be loyal to your country or a person, often by doing something harmful such as helping their enemies.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • When I tell someone I will not betray his confidence I keep my word.

  • We betray the ideals of our country when we support capital punishment.

  • She was terrified of saying something that would make her betray herself .

  • They offered me money if I would betray my associates.

Break-in

MEANING:   to enter a building illegally, usually by damaging a door or window, esp. for the purpose of stealing something.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • After the break-in, we installed a new security system.

  • Police said it did not appear that entry was made into the house, where the break-in occurred after 4:30 p.m.

  • Students were notified after each incident, and students' parents were notified via email after the third break-in.

Break out

MEANING: to escape from prison.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • They broke out of prison and fled the country.

  • After the break-out of the Anti-Japanese War, the national government conducted transportation insurance and on-land property insurance against war.

  • The authorities have urged people to stock up on fuel in case hostilities break out.

  • The authorities have urged people to stock up on fuel in case hostilities break out.

  • A group of prisoners rushed an officer and managed to break out.

Break the law

MEANING: to do something illegal;  means to fail to obey a law; to act contrary to a law.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Using confidential information acquired in their work, they play the market and break the law.

  • More and more drivers are tempted to break the law, and risk serious uncompensated injury to others, by driving uninsured.

  • The hope is to introduce regulation that will penalize companies if employees break the law or act unethically.

  • If they get hurt it's what they deserve, they shouldn't break the law.

Burgle

MEANING: to enter a building illegally and steal things.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Burglar alarms going off at any time of the day or night serve only to annoy passers-by and actually assist burglars to burgle.

  • The police pills were harmless placebos. But a local gang had been using the same technique to give people heavy sedatives and then burgle them.

Charge

MEANING:   (of the police) to make a formal statement saying that someone is accused of a crime.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The 19-year-old will be appearing in court on Thursday where she will face criminal charges.

  • The president responded angrily to the charge that she had lost touch with her country's people.

  • The judge reduced the charge to second-degree murder.

  • The tramp was arraigned on a charge of stealing.

Commit a crime

MEANING:   to do something illegal or something that is considered wrong.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • A police officer may stop and question any person who has reasonable grounds to be suspected of having committed or being about to commit a crime.

  • Legal mental capacity to commit a crime is essential to criminal responsibility.

  • If you commit a crime you can never escape being punished.

Commit arson

MEANING:   the act or crime of willfully, wrongfully, and unjustifiably setting property on fire often for the purpose of committing fraud.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Seven men, all from Bristol, admitted conspiracy to commit arson.

  • Last month Duriez and Tibbles were found guilty of conspiracy to commit arson.

  • He carries gas with him and wants to commit arson.

Commit bigamy

MEANING:   The crime of bigamy is committed by any person who shall contract a second or subsequent marriage before the former marriage has been legally dissolved.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • American law does this to prevent a person to commit bigamy by both declaring second marriages impossible and punishing those who attempt to do what is legally impossible.

Committing an offense

MEANING: to do something illegal or that is considered wrong: commit a crime/felony/offense.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • A person discovered committing an offense may be summoned verbally.

  • She later claimed that she did not realize she was committing an offense.

Desert / Deserted

MEANING: abandon (a person, cause, or organization) in a way considered disloyal or treacherous.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • We feel our public representatives have deserted us.

  • The emperor was deserted by his closest supporters before the end of the war.

Escape

MEANING: the act of successfully getting out of a place or a dangerous or bad situation.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • He made his escape on the back of a motorcycle.

  • The prisoners attempted a daring escape.

Forge

MEANING: to make an illegal copy of something in order to deceive.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • A number of forged works of art have been sold as genuine.

  • He was accused of forging his father’s signature on the check.

  • The Community was trying to forge a common foreign and security policy.

Hijack

MEANING: to take control of an aircraft or other vehicle during a journey, especially using violence.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Two men hijacked a jet traveling to Paris and demanded $125,000.

  • The hijack ended with the release of all the plane's passengers unharmed.

  • Space weapons can electronically hijack a cruise missile in flight and redirect it.

Hold-up

MEANING:   an occasion when someone steals from someone else using violence or the threat of violence.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • In the hold-up, a masked youth threatened the bank staff with a gun.

  • Paul was injured during an attempted hold-up of the gas station in which he worked.

Kidnap

MEANING:   to take a person away illegally by force, usually in order to demand money in exchange for releasing them.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The wife of a businessman was kidnapped from her home in London last night.

  • There are very few fathers of kidnap victims who aren't worried about their financial affairs being looked at too closely.

  • Despite a nationwide hunt for the kidnap gang, not a trace of them was found.

Murder

MEANING:   to commit the crime of intentionally killing a person.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Her husband was murdered by gunmen as she watched.

  • In the last year, terrorists have murdered several local journalists.

  • The judge reduced the charge to second-degree murder.

  • He swore vengeance on everyone involved in the murder.

Rob

MEANING:   to take money or property illegally from a place, organization, or person, often using violence.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Someone unknown tipped off the police about the plan to rob the bank.

  • Her father was shot by someone trying to rob his small retail store.

  • The police frustrated the bandit's attempt to rob the bank.

Shoplift

MEANING:   to take goods illegally from a shop without paying for them.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • We used to shoplift all the latest designer gear.

  • Many people wouldn't shoplift because they might get caught.

Smuggle

MEANING:   to take things or people to or from a place secretly and often illegally.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • She was caught trying to smuggle 26 kilos of heroin out of the country.

  • They managed to smuggle a video of the captive journalists out of the prison.

  • Four more Britons were jailed in France yesterday for trying to smuggle illegal immigrants into England.

  • Together they plotted to smuggle weapons into the troubled region.

  • They pleaded guilty to trying to smuggle $8 million worth of marijuana.

Steal

MEANING:   to take something without the permission or knowledge of the owner and keep it or use it.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • They broke into cars to steal the radios.

  • He never paid me back, so basically he ended up stealing a hundred dollars from me.

  • He knew it was wrong to steal, but the money just lying there was too great a temptation.

  • She stole the shoes from right under the assistant's nose.

Vandalize

MEANING:   to intentionally damage property belonging to other people.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • When I got back, my car had been vandalized.

  • Bored teens went around vandalizing phone booths.
