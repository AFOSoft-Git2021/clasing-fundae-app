Sentence Connectors to express Possibility


This type of connector lets us affirm that something is true or indicates the possibility that something can be true.

Obviously

Meaning: in a way that is easy to understand or see:

Sample Sentences: 

  • He was in tears and obviously very upset.
  • Obviously the school cannot function without teachers.
  • She's obviously very intelligent, but her lectures are rough going.


Surely

Meaning: Used to express surprise that something has happened or is going to happen:

Sample Sentences: 

  • Surely you don't expect me to believe that?
  • Surely you're not going out on a night like this?
  • This latest injury must surely mean that her tennis career is now at an end .
  • He's running so well, surely he'll take the gold.


Indeed

Meaning: really or certainly, often used to emphasize something

Sample Sentences: 

  • Indeed, it could be the worst environmental disaster in Europe this century.
  • Evidence suggests that errors may indeed be occurring.
  • We live in strange times indeed.


Apparently

Meaning: Used to say you have read or been told something although you are not certain it is true.

Sample Sentences: 

  • Apparently it's going to rain today.
  • Apparently he's had enough of England and is going back to Australia.
  • You know I told you Alice's party was on the 13th? Well I saw her last night and apparently it's on the 14th.


Possibly

Meaning: Used with "can" or "could" for emphasis

Sample Sentences: 

  • He can't possibly have drunk all that on his own!
  • We did all that we possibly could to persuade her to come.
  • He can’t possibly mean what he says.


Undoubtedly

Meaning: Used to emphasize that something is true:

Sample Sentences: 

  • She was undoubtedly the best candidate.
  • Undoubtedly, stress has contributed to her health problems.
  • She is an artist whose work will undoubtedly withstand the test of time.


MORE SAMPLE SENTENCES

  • You obviously didn't read the instructions properly.
  • There was obviously a great physical attraction between them.
  • You've obviously put a lot of work in your garden.
  • The report was obviously prepared with studious care and attention.
  • Greater international stability can surely only be to the good.
  • The actors are very good, but when all is considered the laurels must surely go to the director of the play.
  • Overeating is surely the main cause of obesity.
  • For such creatures, speed is not important - indeed it is counterproductive.
  • This is undoubtedly the best chocolate cake I've ever tasted.
  • He is undoubtedly England's best captain of recent times.
  • Undoubtedly, some will disagree.
