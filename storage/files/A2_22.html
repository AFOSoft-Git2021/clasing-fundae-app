Music & musical instruments

This list of music vocabulary, with example sentences, includes many of the words you'll come across when reading or talking about music. Most of the words are related to popular music, but many can also be used when talking about other types of music like classical or traditional folk music.

Vocabulary for Music

VOCABULARY DEFINITION EXAMPLES
acoustic without inbuilt electrical equipment to amplify the sound I can play acoustic guitar, but I can't play electric guitar.
album a collection of songs released as a digital download or a 12-inch LP record Do you have Michael Jackson's album Thriller?
artist a professional singer, musician or songwriter Taylor Swift has become one of music's best-selling artists.
beat the regular pulse in music that dancers move to and audiences clap to Dance music always has a strong beat.
boy band a group of three or more boys or young men who sing and dance My little sister loves that Korean boy band Super Junior.
breakdancing a style of street-dancing in hip hop culture The breakdancing in this Turf Feinz video is incredible.
breakdown section of a dance track with less singing and more percussion Does the track have a good breakdown?

genre a kind or style of music, movie, TV show, painting, etc. In music classes at school, the only genres we studied were classical music and jazz.
chorus the part of a song with the same melody and words each time it's heard I'll sing the verses, and you sing the choruses.

Types of Musical Genre

Rock is a broad genre of popular music that originated as "rock and roll" in the United States in the late 1940s and early 1950s,
Popular music Popular music is music with wide appeal that is typically distributed to large audiences through the music industry. These forms and styles can be enjoyed and performed by people with little or no musical training. It stands in contrast to both art music and traditional or "folk" music.
Pop music Pop music is a genre of popular music that originated in its modern form during the mid-1950s in the United States and the United Kingdom.
Hip hop music Hip hop music or hip-hop music, also known as rap music, is a genre of popular music developed in the United States by inner-city African Americans,
Rhythm and blues Rhythm and blues, frequently abbreviated as R&B or R'n'B, is a genre of popular music that originated in African-American communities in the 1940s.
classical music Classical music generally refers to the formal musical tradition of the Western world, considered to be distinct from Western folk music or popular music traditions.
Country music Country is a genre of popular music that originated with blues, church music such as Southern gospel and spirituals, old-time, and American folk music forms including Appalachian, Cajun, Creole, and the cowboy Western music styles of New Mexico, Red Dirt, Tejano, and Texas country.
Jazz Jazz is a music genre that originated in the African-American communities of New Orleans, Louisiana in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, with its roots in blues and ragtime. Since the 1920s Jazz Age, it has been recognized as a major form of musical expression in traditional and popular music.
Folk music It is a traditional music from a particular region or country
Blues Blues is a music genre and musical form which originated in the Deep South of the United States around the 1860s by African-Americans from roots in African-American work songs and spirituals.
Electronic music any music involving electronic processing, such as recording and editing on tape, and whose reproduction involves the use of loudspeakers.
Heavy metal Heavy metal is a genre of rock music that developed in the late 1960s and early 1970s, largely in the United Kingdom and United States.
Alternative rock Alternative rock, or alt-rock, is a category of rock music that emerged from the independent music underground of the 1970s and became widely popular in the 1990s.
Electronic dance music (EDM) Electronic dance music, also known as dance music, club music, or simply dance, is a broad range of percussive electronic music genres made largely for nightclubs, raves, and festivals.
Soul music Soul music is a popular music genre that originated in the African American community throughout the United States in the late 1950s and early 1960s. It has its roots in African-American gospel music and rhythm and blues.
Reggae is a music genre that originated in Jamaica in the late 1960s. The term also denotes the modern popular music of Jamaica and its diaspora.
Punk rock Punk rock is a music genre that emerged in the mid-1970s. Rooted in 1960s garage rock, punk bands rejected the perceived excesses of mainstream 1970s rock.
Latin music Latin music is a term used by the music industry as a catch-all arbitrary category for various styles of music from Latin America, Spain, Portugal, and the United States inspired by older Latin American, Spanish and Portuguese music genres, as well as music that is sung in the Spanish and/or Portuguese language.
Techno Techno is a genre of electronic dance music which is generally produced for use in a continuous DJ set, with tempo often varying between 120 and 150 beats per minute.
New-age music New-age is a genre of music intended to create artistic inspiration, relaxation, and optimism. It is used by listeners for yoga, massage, meditation, and reading as a method of stress management to bring about a state of ecstasy rather than trance, or to create a peaceful atmosphere in homes or other environments.
Musical theatre Musical theatre is a form of theatrical performance that combines songs, spoken dialogue, acting and dance.
House music House is a music genre characterized by a repetitive four-on-the-floor beat and a typical tempo of 120 beats per minute.
Disco Disco is a genre of dance music and a subculture that emerged in the 1970s from the United States' urban nightlife scene. Its sound is typified by four-on-the-floor beats, syncopated basslines, string sections, horns, electric piano, synthesizers, and electric rhythm guitars.

Types of Musical Instruments

accordion a musical instrument that's shaped like a box, you play it by pressing keys and buttons and pulling the sides of the box from each other and then pressing them back together
acoustic guitar a musical instrument made from wood, played by hitting or strumming six strings.
banjo an instrument with four or five strings and a circular body
bass guitar a guitar with four or five strings that produces very low tones
drums a set of drums and other percussion instruments, esp. in rock music etc.
electric guitar a guitar that needs electricity to be played
grand piano a large piano on three legs, the strings are in horizontal position, it is often used in concerts etc.
harp a musical instrument with strings, it is played by strumming or plucking the strings with both hands
tambourine a kind of small drum with metal disks fastened around it
trumpet a brass musical instrument played by blowing
violin a stringed instrument that is supported by the left shoulder and played with a bow
xylophone a musical instrument consisting of a set or sets of flat metal bars that make a sound when they are hit with a small hammer
flute a long thin musical instrument that is played by blowing
saxophone a brass musical instrument that is often used in jazz music
trombone a brass musical instrument with a tube that is slid in or out in order to change the sound
keyboards an electronic musical instrument that looks like a piano
