Qualifying adjectives?

The qualifying adjectives are a type of adjective that attributes to a given noun one or some set of specific properties, or that in any case accounts for some of its characteristics.

  • We always use qualifying adjectives before nouns.
  • Qualifying adjectives stay the same for singular and plural nouns.
Examples:
  • The beautiful houses (plural)
  • The beautiful house (singular)
  • The big men (plural)
  • The big man (singular)

simple easy to understand or do; not difficult The instructions were written in simple English.
hot having a high temperature Is the water hot enough for a bath?

intelligent able to learn and understand things quickly and easily: Annabelle is intelligent and hardworking
soft not hard or firm These potato chips are soft.
quick happening or done with great speed, or lasting only a short time I had a quick coffee and left the house.
sweet (especially of something or someone small) pleasant and attractive. (especially of food or drink) having a taste similar to that of sugar; not bitter or salty: They live in a sweet little house. The pineapple was sweet and juicy.

stupid having or showing a great lack of intelligence or common sense. I just made another stupid mistake.
beautiful pleasing the senses or mind aesthetically, very pleasant You did a beautiful thing in helping those poor children.
dirty marked with dirt, mud, etc., or containing something such as pollution or bacteria Dirty needles can cause serious infections.
rude behaving in a way that hurts other people’s feelings; not polite I apologized for Ted’s rude behavior.
curious interested in learning about people or things around you I’m curious to see what’s going to happen to his studies
ugly extremely unattractive in appearance He’s a really ugly dog.
slow moving, happening, or doing something without much speed She's a very slow eater.
salty tasting or smelling like salt This bacon is too salty for me.
shy uncomfortable with other people and unwilling to talk to them He was too shy to ask her to dance with him.
acid sour in taste The salad dressing has an acid taste.
sticky tending to stick, or covered with a substance that sticks You better wash your sticky fingers.
elegant graceful and attractive in appearance or behavior She looks elegant in black
sad unhappy or sorry I've just received some very sad news.
happy feeling, showing, or causing pleasure or satisfaction She looks so happy.
red of the color of fresh blood The dress was bright red.
yellow of a color like that of a lemon or gold or the sun He loves riding on his yellow car.
green of a color between blue and yellow; of the color of grass I wore that skirt with the green spots.
colorful having a bright color or a lot of different colors She is wearing a colorful blue and yellow dress
old having lived or existed for many years I saw an old man crying.
used be used to something/someone After a while you get used to all the noise.
friendly behaving in a pleasant, kind way toward someone He nodded to me in a friendly way.
dark with little or no light Our bedroom was very dark until we put a larger window in.
clear easy to perceive, understand, or interpret Our new television has a very clear picture.
Useless of no value; worthless A car is useless without gasoline.
talented having a natural ability or skill Peter is an exceptionally talented lawyer.
selfish (of a person, action, or motive) lacking consideration for others; concerned chiefly with one's own personal profit or pleasure. She calls me lazy and selfish.
kind generous, helpful, and thinking about other people's feelings She's a very kind and thoughtful person.
fair (of skin) pale, or (of hair) pale yellow or gold: She has fair hair and blue eyes.
strong powerful; having or using great force or control She cut through the water with her strong arms.

cloudy with clouds Scotland will be cloudy with wintry showers.
quiet making very little noise It's so quiet without the kids here.
