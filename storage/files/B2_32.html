Historical Milestone - Vocabulary

Absolution

The act of being formally forgiven.

  • Pope Leo gave him absolution.
  • The priest heard her confession and granted absolution.

Battle

An argument between two groups or against a situation that a group wants to change

  • Napoleon arrayed his troops for battle.
  • One may overcome a thousand men in battle, but he who conquers himself is the greatest victor.
  • They defeated the enemy in the battle.

Allies

Someone who helps and supports other people who are part of a group that is treated badly or unfairly, although they are not themselves a member of this group

  • Cuba became an ally of the Soviet Union in the 1950s.
  • If the violence continues, they risk alienating one of their most powerful allies.
  • NATO allies have decided to send peace-keeping troops to the troubled area.
  • The President and his allies are willing to shut down the federal government unless they get what they want.
  • You have been my ally and my confidant.

Architecture

The discipline dealing with the design of fine buildings

  • Modernist architecture tries to conquer nature instead of working with it.
  • This is a prime example of 1930s architecture.
  • Almost every visitor to Chicago is seduced by its splendid architecture.
  • The town's modern architecture is very well integrated with the old.
  • I like the clean-cut shapes of classical architecture.

Century

A period of 100 years

  • The city center has scarcely changed in over a century.
  • This sculpture must be centuries old.
  • Her medical career spanned half a century.

Civilization

A society in an advanced state of social development

  • Some people think that nuclear war would mean the end of civilization.
  • Cuzco was the center of one of the world's most famous civilizations, that of the Incas.
  • We spent a week visiting the wonders of Ancient Greek civilization.
  • I heard a radio program recently about the Aztec civilization in Central America.

Colony

A colony is a group of people who settle in a new place but keep ties to their homeland.

  • The people who founded the United States first came to America to live as part of a British colony.
  • Settlers established a new colony in the early 18th century.
  • They had thought that the new colony would be a paradise, but they were soon disillusioned.

Constitution

The act of forming or establishing something

  • Britain has no written constitution.
  • Clause 4 of the constitution is thought to be the most important section.
  • Until the constitution is amended, the power to appoint ministers will remain with the president.
  • East and West Germany united under article 23 of the Bonn constitution.
  • The drafting of a new constitution cannot be a monopoly of the white minority regime.

Culture

All the knowledge and values shared by a society.

  • She's studying modern Japanese language and culture.
  • This is one of the most ancient cultures in the world.
  • American culture has been exported all over the world.
  • She got some books out of the library and immersed herself in Jewish history and culture.

Democracy

The orientation of those who favor government by the people.

  • The government has promised to uphold the principles of democracy.
  • The early 1990s saw the spread of democracy in Eastern Europe.
  • Sweden is often held up as an example of a successful social democracy.

Dictator

A ruler who is unconstrained by law

  • The dictator took control of the country in 1933.
  • He had been a dictator and a warlord who had oppressed and degraded the people of the South.
  • After the death of the dictator there was an unseemly scramble for power among the generals.
  • The former dictator still keeps a tight grip on power.

Diplomacy

Negotiation between nations.

  • Diplomacy has so far failed to bring an end to the fighting.
  • Quiet diplomacy is sometimes better than public threats.
  •  UN mediators are conducting shuttle diplomacy between the two sides.

Documentary

A film presenting the facts about a person or event

  • The documentary took a fresh look at the life of Darwin.
  • They showed a documentary on animal communication.
  • We're compiling some facts and figures for a documentary on the subject.
  • The documentary went through all the graphic details of the operation in unsparing detail.

Empire

The domain is ruled by a single authoritative sovereign.

  • It used to be said that Britain ruled an empire on which the sun would never set.
  • The temple ruins are a distant reminder of a vanished empire.
  • This century saw the dismemberment of the empire.

Era

A period marked by distinctive character.

  • They had worked for peace during the long era of conflict.
  • The fall of the Berlin wall marked the end of an era.
  • The president's speech heralds a new era in foreign policy.
  • We are on the threshold of a new era in European relations.

Exile

The act of expelling a person from their native land

  • The king went into exile because of the political situation in his country.
  • The deposed leaders are currently in exile in the neighboring country.
  • After the military coup, the family left for self-imposed exile in America.

Hierarchy

A series of ordered groupings within a system.

  • He rose quickly through the political hierarchy to become party leader.
  • By reorganizing the church hierarchy, the king was able to secure the appointment of men whom he personally favored.
  •  The principle of hierarchy does not equate to totalitarian terror.

Legislative

Relating to a lawmaking assembly.

  • The European Parliament will have greater legislative powers.
  • They oppose most legislative reform.
  • He is an expert in legislative history.
  • They have too much legislative power.

Millennium

A span of 1000 years.

  • Imagine what the world will be like at the end of the next millennium.
  • The area has experienced the worst drought in half a millennium.
  • Acupuncture was practiced in China as long ago as the third millennium BC.
