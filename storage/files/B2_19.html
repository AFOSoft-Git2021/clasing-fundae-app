Alike ↔ Like 

Both indicate similarities between two things. Additionally, both come after linking verbs and the 'to be' verb. However,'like' is a preposition and comes before nouns, pronouns, and noun clauses.'Alike', on the other hand, is an adjective and indicates two things being the same. 'Alike' never comes before a noun. So, it is a predictive adjective.


Differences

'Like' is a preposition of manner and it indicates that two things are somehow similar or share some similar characteristics. It can come before a noun, a pronoun, or a noun clause. It mainly comes after linking verbs and the 'to be' verb.

 Look at the examples below:

  • My hair looks like my mom's, it seems.
  • How can anyone feel like I do?

'Alike', on the other hand, is an adjective. Since it never comes before nouns, it is called an attributive adjective. It also comes after linking verbs. It indicates that two things or people are the same. 

Take a look at the examples below:

  • If women continue having plastic surgeries, all of them will look alike in the near future.
  • It is important that twins be treated alike.
