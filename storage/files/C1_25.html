Phrases with at, in, on and other prepositions

PHRASES WITH AT 

at a glance

MEANING: immediately

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The rating system shows prospective buyers at a glance how people feel about the product.

  • She knew, at a glance, that her mother was upset with her.

at hand

MEANING: near in time or position

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • We want to ensure that help is at hand for all children suffering abuse.

  • We have no solutions at hand. 

at last

MEANING: after much delay.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • They've installed the new computer network at last.

  • You’re here at last! 

  • At last we successfully put through the business deal.

at least

MEANING: not less than; at the minimum;used to emphasize that something is good in a bad situation; used to say that someone should do something small, even if they do nothing else.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Fortune knocks once at least at every man's gate.

  • It's a small house but at least there's a yard.

  • Even if she didn't want to send a present, she could at least have sent a card.

  • The disease killed at least two million people.

at random

MEANING: happening, done, or chosen by chance rather than according to a plan.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The candidates are selected at random.

  • Ten workers performing the most menial tasks imaginable were picked at random from the processing line.

  • While he waited, he picked up a magazine, turned to a page at random, and started reading.

at risk

MEANING: something bad that might happen.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The whole area is at risk of flooding.

  • He puts his administration at risk if he doesn't come through on these promises for reform.

  • Lives are being put at risk because of staff shortages.

  • The disease is spreading, and all children under five are at risk.

  • All houses within 100 meters of the seas are at risk of flooding.

at times

MEANING: sometimes; on occasions.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • Life is sad at times, but it is up to you to make your own life happy.

  • The children are at times aggressive and cruel.

  • I was struggling to keep up some of the time and the scenery was going past way too fast and close at times.

PHRASES WITH IN

in advance

MEANING: before a particular time, or before doing a particular thing.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • If you're going to come, please let me know in advance.

  • I'd advise buying your tickets well in advance if you want to travel in August.

  • I should warn you in advance that I'm not a very good dancer.

in all

MEANING: with everything added together to make a total.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • There were sixty of them in all.

  • The courses will give the beginner personal tuition in all types of outdoor photography.

  • Canada will ban smoking in all offices later this year.

  • We watched the stars arriving for the awards ceremony dressed up in all their finery.

in charge

MEANING: being the person who has control of or is responsible for someone or something.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • I left Jack in charge of the suitcases while I went to get the tickets.

  • There were more than 30 people working on the project, with no single person in charge.

  • You are not in charge of the universe; you are in charge of yourself.

in common

MEANING: If two or more things have something in common, they have the same characteristic or feature.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The oboe and the clarinet have got certain features in common. 

  • In common with most Italian lakes, access to the shores of Orta is restricted.

  • We should be focusing on what we have in common rather than emphasizing our differences.

in a hurry

MEANING: rushed; in a rushed manner;eager to get a thing done quickly.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • She was in such a hurry that she forgot to take her passport.

  • Whoever is in a hurry shows that the thing he is about is too big for him.

  • We were in a hurry so we had to make do with a quick snack.

in the long run

MEANING: over or after a long period of time; eventually.

SAMPLE SENTENCES: 

  • The machine will save energy in the long run.

  • It will pay in the long run if the setting off of firecrackers is effectively banned in the cities.

  • If the government clamps down on the protestors, that will only serve to strengthen them in the long run.

in trouble

MEANING: they are in a situation in which a person in authority is angry with them or is likely to punish them because they have done something wrong.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • I was always getting in trouble for misbehaving at school.

  • Her marriage is in trouble and she is desperately unhappy.

  • It's not the first time that the paper has been in trouble for printing untruths about people's private lives.

in vain

MEANING: without success or a result.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • I tried to get in touch with him but in vain.

  • They attempted in vain to modernize these antiquated industries.

  • They endeavored to make her happy, but in vain.

  • Police searched in vain for the missing gunman.

Phrases with on

on average

MEANING: usually

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • On average, a person’s income is highest when they are in their mid-50s.

  • The city's police chief says they arrest, on average, 300 people each day.

  • Prices have risen, on average,￡100 per acre with some farms making 25 percent more than the asking price.

on board

MEANING: as part of a group or team, especially for a special purpose; on a boat, train, or aircraft

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • We met on board an ocean liner.

  • Let's bring Rob on board for the Saudi deal - he's the expert.

  • As soon as I was on board, I began to have second thoughts about leaving.

  • Roughly fifty crew members were on board the ship at the time.

on purpose

MEANING: Deliberately, intentionally.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • I spilled my drink on purpose. I needed an excuse to leave the room.

  • My films never pretend to be anything but artifacts - they're unnatural, contrived, fashioned, unrealistic, on purpose.

  • Yolanda, the third of the four girls, became a schoolteacher but not on purpose.

on second thoughts

MEANING: to change your opinion about something or start to doubt it.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • On second thoughts, she wished she had not uttered the last sentence.

  • He had intended to go, but on second thoughts, he gave up the idea.

  • On second thoughts, I'll do the experiment at once at any cost.

Phrases with other prepositions

by accident

MEANING: unintentionally; by chance.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • I broke Mary's pen by accident, and in revenge she tore up my school work.

  • I met Jacob by accident in the cinema.

  • The crucial breakthrough came almost by accident.

for a change

MEANING: contrary to how things usually happen; for variety.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • He was dressed quite presentably for a change.

  • We went to the seaside – just for a change.

  • Party members fought side by side with trade unionists for a change in the law.

  • Opinion polls indicated a feeling among the public that it was time for a change.

out of breath

MEANING: gasping for air, typically after exercise.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • I was out of breath after running from the city center to the station.

  • They completed their task barely out of breath and with smiles on their faces, raised over 600 for charity!

  • The boy laughed at them for running and being out of breath for nothing.

out of order

MEANING: If something someone says or does is out of order, it is unpleasant or not suitable and it is likely to upset or offend people; not operating because it is broken.

SAMPLE SENTENCES:

  • His behavior in the meeting was out of order.

  • The lift was out of order, so we had to use the stairs. 

  • The presiding officer ruled that the motion was out of order.

  • Not that the machine is out of order, but that I have not learned to operate it.
