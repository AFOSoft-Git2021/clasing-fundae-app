Like & Dislike

There are a lot of ways to talk about preferences in English but one of the easiest is by using the verb ‘to like‘.

Likes

‘Like’ is a very versatile regular verb in English and can be used express preferences.

I like ice cream.

I like going to the movies.

I like you!

You can also use it in the negative with ‘do not‘

I don’t like ice cream.

I don’t like going to the movies.

I don’t like you!

To ask other people questions about their preferences:

Do you like ice cream?

Do you like going to the movies?

Do you like me?

Dislike

If you want to use ‘dislike‘ to express a negative preference it is also very easy but perhaps not as usual in spoken English.

I dislike ice cream.

I dislike going to the movies.

Do you dislike me?

Things to remember about likes and dislikes:

1. When these expressions are followed by a verb, the latter is put in the -ing form.

  • "I like listening to music."
  • "I hate wearing sunglasses."

2. Note that" very much" & " a lot" always come after the things you like.

CORRECT: I like basketball very much.

CORRECT: I like basketball a lot.

WRONG: I like very much basketball.

WRON

G: I like a lot basketball.

3. Be careful when you use "I don't mind...". Although it's in a negative form, it means that you neither like nor dislike something

  • SPEAKER 1: Do you mind playing football?
  • SPEAKER 2: No, I don't mind.
