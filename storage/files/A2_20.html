Traveling

The travel-related words below are the most important terms to know when talking about travel or taking vacations. Words are categorized into different sections depending on the type of travel.

VOCABULARY DEFINITION SAMPLE SENTENCE
Airport a place from which aircraft operate that usually has paved runways and maintenance facilities and often serves as a terminal. I went to the airport to catch a flight to San Francisco.
Check in register someone upon their arrival at a hotel or airport. Make sure to get to the airport two hours early to check in.
Fly to travel by plane I like to fly on the same airline to get mileage points.
Landing bringing to a surface (such as land or shore) after a voyage or flight. The landing took place during a storm. It was very scary!
Plane / Airplane a powered flying vehicle with fixed wings and a weight greater than that of the air it displaces. The plane is packed with 300 passengers.
Take off (of an aircraft or bird) become airborne. The airplane is scheduled to take off at 3:30 p.m.
Bicycle also called bike, two-wheeled steerable machine that is pedaled by the rider’s feet. One of the best ways to see the countryside is to ride a bicycle.
Bus a large motor vehicle carrying passengers by road, typically one serving the public on a fixed route and for a fare. You can catch a bus for Seattle at the bus station.
Bus station a terminal where buses arrive and depart. The bus station is three blocks from here.
Car a four-wheeled road vehicle that is powered by an engine and is able to carry a small number of people. You might want to rent a car when you go on vacation.
Motorcycle a two-wheeled vehicle that is powered by a motor and has no pedals. Riding a motorcycle can be fun and exciting, but it's also dangerous.
Freeway an express highway, especially one with controlled access. We'll have to take the freeway to Los Angeles.
Highway a main road, especially one connecting major towns or cities. The highway between the two cities is quite lovely.
Railway a track made of steel rails along which trains run; a railroad. The railway station is down this street.
Taxi / Taxi cab an automobile that carries passengers for a fare usually determined by the distance traveled I got in a taxi and went to the train station.
Traffic vehicles moving on a road or public highway. There's a lot of traffic today on the road!
Train a railway engine connected to carriages for carrying people or to wheeled containers for carrying goods I like riding on trains. It's a very relaxing way to travel.
Underground situated beneath the surface of the ground. You can take the underground in many cities throughout Europe.
Subway an underground electric railroad. You can take the subway in New York.
Boat a small vessel propelled on water by oars, sails, or an engine. Have you ever piloted a boat?
Cruise a voyage on a ship or boat taken for pleasure or as a vacation and usually calling in at several places. We will stop at three destinations during our cruise through the Mediterranean.
Ferry/ferries a boat or ship for conveying passengers and goods, especially over a relatively short distance and as a regular service. Ferries allow passengers to take their cars with them to their destination.
Port a town or city with a harbor where ships load or unload. There are all kinds of commercial ships in the port.
Sailboat a small boat with sails The sailboat requires nothing but the wind.
Set sail begin a voyage. We set sail for the exotic island.
Ship a vessel larger than a boat for transporting people or goods by sea. Have you ever been a passenger on a ship?
Voyage a long journey involving travel by sea or in space. The voyage to the Bahamas took three days.
Arrives reach a place at the end of a journey or a stage in a journey. We arrived at his house and knocked at the door.
Destination the place to which someone or something is going or being sent. A popular destination for golfers.
Lane a division of a road marked off with painted lines and intended to separate single lines of traffic according to speed or direction. The car accelerated and moved into the outside lane.
flight the action or process of flying through the air. I will miss my flight if I don't hurry up.
luggage suitcases or other bags in which to pack personal belongings for traveling. Upon landing, we waited and waited for our luggage.
commute is to make the same journey regularly between work and home. It's exhausting commuting from Brighton to London every day.
journey is traveling from one place to another, especially in a vehicle. I love going on long journeys.
Attractions Somewhere or something you can visit that is interesting or enjoyable The church is the town's main tourist attraction
Guide A person whose job is to look after a group of people who are visiting a place This lady is going to act as our guide for the rest of the tour.
Tourist Someone who is visiting a place on holiday The pyramids have drawn tourists to Egypt.
