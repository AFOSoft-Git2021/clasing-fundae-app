Connectors

‘Connectors’ are used to link large groups of words: phrases and sentences. You can also use them to connect paragraphs to give them coherence. Sentence connectors are usually placed at the beginning of a sentence.

CONNECTORS MEANING SENTENCE
But used to introduce a phrase or clause contrasting with what has already been mentioned. I wanted to go swimming but I am so tired.
However used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously. The film received good ratings; however, it was very long.
On the other hand used to introduce a contrasting point of view, fact, or situation. I was eager to join them for the vacation but on the other hand, my exam dates were getting closer.
Otherwise in circumstances different from those present or considered; or else. Remind them beforehand otherwise, they might leave without taking the tickets.
Unlike different from; not similar to. Zoey loved hot chocolate, unlike John.
And clauses, or sentences, that are to be taken jointly. He and other members went to the party.
Also in addition; too. He was told to study and also to take care of the dog.
as for used for introducing a subject that is related to what you have just been talking about As for the children, they were happy enough to spend all day on the beach.
 in comparison with when compared. My shoes are small in comparison with my sister's.
At the same time simultaneously; at once. She felt guilty and depressed at the same time.
In spite of without being affected by the particular factor mentioned. In spite of heavy rainfall, he took out his scooter and headed towards the hospital.
Even though despite the fact that. He could manage the cooking part even though that would not taste awesome.
Nevertheless in spite of that; notwithstanding; all the same. .What you said was right but nevertheless harsh.
On the contrary used to intensify a denial of what has just been implied or stated by suggesting that the opposite is the case Mother thought John was studying but on the contrary, he was sleeping.
Whereas in contrast or comparison with the fact that. All of her daughters are engineers whereas the younger one chose to be a teacher.
Therefore for that reason; consequently. I slept very late last night, therefore, I missed the morning prayer.
So and for this reason; therefore. The blue bag was full so he kept the packet in the yellow bag.
Due to attributable to or caused by The match got canceled due to the heavy rain.
As a result  because of something As a result of the heavy rain, the match got canceled.
Seeing that used to introduce a statement that explains the reason for another statement Seeing that he was losing the fight, he started blaming her mother.
Then after that; next; afterward. Once you reach the office, then make a call to the supervisor.
Next coming immediately after the present one in order, rank, or space. The next episode is going to be very interesting.
Soon used to indicate one's preference in a particular matter. They are soon to graduate.
Finally as the last in a series of related events or objects. Finally, the war came to an end,
Second/ Secondly in the second place (used to introduce a second point or reason). Secondly, you have to manage the team.
At first sight After an initial impression (which is then found to be different from what is actually the case). At first sight, he appeared to be very arrogant.
First coming before all others in time or order; earliest; 1st. First, you should go to the washroom and take a bath.
First of all before doing anything else; at the beginning. First of all, they are not my family and I can not be held responsible for their immature behavior.
In the first place at the beginning; to begin with (especially in reference to the time when an action was being planned or discussed). In the first place, take care of the pets and then do the rest of your work.
To start with as the first thing to be taken into account. To start with, I asked her to tell us about her hobbies.
Lastly in the last place (used to introduce the last of a series of points or actions). Lastly, we had our dinner and left.
And finally used to introduce a final point or reason. And finally, the day of the verdict came.
In conclusion lastly; to sum up. In conclusion, it is always better to take prevention than cure.
In order to in the correct condition for operation or use. In order to prove him wrong, he started shouting.
So as to with respect to; concerning. You have to study hard so as to improve your career.
So that to the extent that. Give me your resume so that I can forward it to the right department.
For example used to introduce something chosen as a typical case. For example, take the rising graph of air pollution.  
For instance for or as an example I like a few vegetables, for instance, potato, cabbage etc.
More or less approximately. There will be more or less 30 guests.
In general as a whole. Old people, in general, like to spend time with their grandkids.
Especially to a great extent; very much. It’s tough to sleep, especially after the strong coffee we drank.
In particular especially (used to show that a statement applies to one person or thing more than any other). All women, in particular, have to be strong and determined.
Essentially used to emphasize the basic, fundamental, or intrinsic nature of a person, thing, or situation. An electric kettle is useful essentially for the winter months.
Basically used to indicate that a statement summarizes the most important aspects, or gives a roughly accurate account, of a more complex situation. The cake is basically full of chocolates.
In other words Used to introduce a statement that repeats what has been said in a different and usually a simpler or more exact way The problem, In other words, is that we have to re-write the whole script.
In short to sum up; briefly. The outing, in short, could not lift up my mood.
Above all more so than anything else. The idea behind the meeting is, above all, to make the employees aware of the current financial crisis faced by the company.
At least if nothing else (used to add a positive comment about a generally negative situation). At least now when you know that we are here, you can jump in any day.
Instead as a substitute or alternative to; in place of. I didn’t want to take a side in the argument. Instead, I put my headphones on and listened to some smooth jazz.
