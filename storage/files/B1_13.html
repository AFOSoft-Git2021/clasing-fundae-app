Can & Could

Definition of Can                        

In simple words, ‘can’ means to be able or permitted to do something. It is used with the first form or the base form of the verb. Basically, it determines the ability or capability of someone or something.                        

                        

1. To define ability.        

  • I can drive a truck.
  • Anirudh can speak 20 languages.

                        

2. To give and seek permission.                                

  • Can I have your number?
  • Can you help me in finding the purse?
  • You can go on the trip.
  • You can play with your friends after doing homework.

                        

3. To make a request.

  • It would be great if you can deliver the message to Sofia.        
  • Can you get me a glass of water, please?                        

                

4. To express some possibility.        

  • Drinking alcohol can damage your liver.                        
  • You can get the monsoon details from the meteorological department.                        

Negative Form for Can

We use the negative can’t or cannot to show that something is impossible:

  • That cannot be possible.
  • That can’t be true.
  • That can’t happen
  • You cannot be serious

                        

Definition of Could                        

Could is the past participle form of the verb ‘can‘ which determines the past ability of someone or something. It is also used when someone is permitted to do something.                        

1. To denote that someone has the ability to do something.

  • You could shout when the purse got snatched.
  • I could help you if I know you are in trouble.                

                        

2. To seek permission, especially a polite one.

  • Could I make a call?                        
  • Could I go and meet them?                        

                        

3. To make a request

  • Could you wait for a moment, please?                        

                        

4. To give suggestion or advice        

  • You could go to Delhi for further studies.                        

                        

5. In imaginary situation or wishes

  • I wish I could meet Amitabh Bachchan.                        

                        

6. To express a slight possibility

  • You could have passed the examination                        

                        

7. In conditional sentences.

  • If you arrange some money, you could start your own business
  • We could save the child if you have brought him to the hospital on time.                

Negative Form for Could

We use couldn’t/could not to talk about the past:

Its structure, in the negative form, is:

Subject + could + not + verb + …

Short version of the negative form is couldn’t.

SUBJECT COULD + NOT VERB
I/You could not work
He/She/It could not play
We/You/They could not swim
