>Questions: TSC-C1_34_16 until C1_34_30

Criminals; the sentence they face

Accomplices can face the same penalties, including prison time.

Arsonists may face sentences somewhere between discharge and 8 years’ imprisonment.

Assailants may face 10 years' imprisonment and a misdemeanor punishable by one year's imprisonment.

Assassins may face sentences ranging from as little as no incarceration to 10 years if no injury or death occurs to being eligible for the death penalty if death actually occurs.

If found guilty, bigamists shall be sentenced to imprisonment within the range of six years and one day to twelve years.

The maximum sentence a blackmailer may face is 14 years’ imprisonment.

Bombers might face a penalty of imprisonment for a term not exceeding 3 years.

Bribers might receive heavy fines or several years in prison.

Depending on the state and the circumstances of the case, a burglar may face 20 years or more in prison.

Pedophiles may face imprisonment for not more than 10 years, a fine, or both.

Deserters carry a maximum punishment of dishonorable discharge, forfeiture of all pay, and confinement for five years.

The punishment drug dealers face ranges from up to seven years for a first-time offense of selling any drug.

Forgers face a range of penalties, including jail or prison time, significant fines, probation, and restitution.

A gangster shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which shall not be less than two years and which may extend to ten years.

Hackers might face up to a year in federal prison for lesser offenses, between 10-20 years for more serious offenses, and even life in prison if the hacking resulted in someone's death.
