Questions: TSC-B1_4_21 until B1_4_30

There are some things that good language learners do and some things they don't do.

Here are some of the most useful suggestions from studies.

  • Don't be afraid of making a lot of mistakes. People often get things wrong. Good language learners notice their mistakes and learn from them.
  • Do group activities. People use language to communicate with other people.
  • A good language learner always looks for the opportunity to talk with other students.
  • Make notes during every class. Notes help you to remember a new language. Look at your notes when you do your homework.
  • Good language learners often use a dictionary to check the meaning of words they don't know. Some non-native language learners use a web translator if they don’t know the meaning. 
  • Think in the language you're learning outside the classroom. 
  • When you're walking down the street, remember the useful words and phrases you are learning. Sometimes, when you're at home, read a sentence in a book to practise your pronunciation.
  • Do extra practice. Test and improve your language, reading and listening skills with self-study material. You can find a lot of language materials online. 
  • Imagine yourself speaking in the language. You may use a recording device to hear yourself. 
  • Enjoy the process. To have fun learning the language, watch a TV series or film, listen to songs, play video games or read a book. It's never too late to become a good language learner.
