Camping is a great way to get outside with family and friends or even by yourself.

As you become a more experienced camper, you may find that part of the fun is figuring out what to bring along to meet your needs.

While you’re packing, use this handy camping checklist to make sure you don’t forget anything important.

  • The first thing you must bring is a tent. It is a must if you are staying overnight. You should save you and your guests from the rain.
  • Dealing with changing temperatures, campfires and forest bugs can be a nightmare, that’s why it is a must to have a sleeping bag.
  • You should also bring a flashlight because it can be a help to make a signal during the night.
  • An extra battery is a must, because you never know when you could experience a delay.
  • Camping pillows also feature a layer of insulation, it should help your head stay warm while you sleep in cold weather.
  • A camping chair makes it much easier to sit around the campfire. It is a must to purchase one that is durable and built to last.
  • Whether you need a surface for family game night, a folding table makes camping easier. You should have extra space for prepping meals.
  • This is optional but you should consider bringing a hammock. It is good for more comfort and easy to set up.
  • You must pack multi-tools too. It eliminates the need to select separate tools.
  • Every camper should have duct tape. You can use it as a band-aid.
  • Both lighters and matches are small and lightweight items you should have in your survival kit.
  • Bacteria will spread easily in an unclean environment. You must prepare a health and hygiene kit.
  • One of the major benefits of wearing a natural sunscreen is that it helps prevent sunburn. You should wear a sunscreen that's broad-spectrum with an SPF of at least 30.
  • You must not forget to bring insect repellent. It is a must in keeping pesky blood suckers at bay.
