Questions: TSC-C1_37_1 until C1_37_15

List of movement related words

1.Paddle - Paddle means to push a pole with a wide end through the water in order to make a boat move.

2.Stumble - Stumble means to step awkwardly while walking or running and fall or begin to fall.

3.Hobble - Hobble means to walk with difficulty in an awkward way, or to cause someone to walk in this way, usually because of an injury to the feet or legs.

4.Amble - Amble means to walk in a slow and relaxed way.

5.Skid - Skid means to slide along a surface so that you have no control.

6.Sprawl - Sprawl means to spread the arms and legs out carelessly and in a sloppy way while sitting or lying down.

7.Canter - If a horse canters, it moves at quite a fast but easy and comfortable speed.

8.Stride - Stride means a long step when walking or running.

9.Limp - Limp means to walk slowly and with difficulty because of having an injured or painful leg or foot.

10.Trample - Trample means to step heavily on something or someone, causing damage or injury.

11.Glide - Glide means to move easily without stopping and without effort or noise; to move or progress without difficulty or effort.

12.Stagger - Stagger means to walk or move with difficulty as if you are going to fall.

13.Wade - Wade means to walk in water that is not very deep.

14.Tiptoe - Tiptoe means on your toes with the heel of your foot lifted off the ground.

15.Shuffle - Shuffle means to walk by pulling your feet slowly along the ground rather than lifting them; the act of moving things around from one position to another.
