Crime; ripple effect

Kidnapping - Depression, anxiety, and PTSD are the negative psychological effects of being kidnapped.

Identity theft - Emotional distress and financial loss are the negative impacts of identity theft.

Genocide - A variety of outcomes include depression, anxiety, schizophrenia, post-traumatic stress as well as post-traumatic growth.

Fraud - People affected by fraud against public bodies suffer from social problems such as loss of reputation, feelings of vulnerability, isolation and exposure.

Gangsterism - Gangs increase the levels of crimes in communities and community members face heightened fear that they, their families, schools, or businesses, will become victims of theft and/or violence.

Espionage - Theft of state secrets can weaken the nation's armed forces and lessen the efficiency of its defense against a foreign foe.

Domestic violence - Domestic violence Survivors can face ongoing and challenging effects after enduring physical, mental, and emotional abuse.

Cybercrime - The ill effects of cybercrime often lead to extreme financial ruin and reputational loss for both businesses and individuals.

Corruption - Our faith in government to work in our best interests is damaged by corruption.

Child abuse - Physical consequences range from minor injuries to severe brain damage and even death.

Bribery - Businesses that have poor anti-bribery and corruption compliance attract less business.

Bombing - Flash burns, a susceptibility to leukemia, cataracts and malignant tumors are some of the effects.

Bigamy - A bigamy conviction can result in the revocation of a person's medical license and make a person ineligible for public office.

Arson - This crime results in increased taxes to support law enforcement and fire departments.

Assault - The emotional effects may involve confusion, shock, shortness of breath, and changes in sleeping and eating patterns.
