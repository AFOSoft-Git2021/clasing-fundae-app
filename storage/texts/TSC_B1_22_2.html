My friends and I

I was sitting on the curb, drinking a Slurpee. It was a summer afternoon, I wanted to end it all; I want to end my life.

Since the boy I had loved left me, life had been nothing but a nightmare. Well, almost a complete nightmare.

I still had people that cared about me, like Jennifer and Carah and Anna. They were the only good friends I had left.

“Hey Ashley,” said Carah, noticing that I was staring out to the sunset by myself with my eyes starting to water, “Whatcha doing?”

“Hmm,” I said. I normally didn’t talk back then, not since he had left, “Just thinking, it’s okay. Nothing to worry about Carah,” and I had to fake my smile to Carah.

“Well, ok,” said Carah, still worried about me.

Sometimes I wonder why everyone is trying to be there for me. I wasn’t popular. I wasn’t rich.

So why did all those people want to be around me? I took another sip from my drink. I looked up as a car beeped. It was Anna’s mom indicating that she was about to take off.

Jennifer walked toward me from across the street as she finished saying goodbye to Anna.

“Hey Ashley,” said Jennifer trying to cheer me up. “You want to go to 7-eleven to get some Shark Gushers?”

“Hmm,” I said then Carah got up from my front lawn and stumbled over to me and Jennifer.

“Whoa! Oh my God, my leg fell asleep. Ha-ha,” said Carah, smiling even though her leg would end up causing her some damage, “Were you guys about to go to 7-Eleven without me?”

Jennifer turned to me smiling, waiting for me to smile back. I felt bad. I hadn’t smiled a real smile since he had left, so I decided to pity myself and my friends by cracking a smile.

I am glad that after a heartbreak, I have myself real friends to be with.
