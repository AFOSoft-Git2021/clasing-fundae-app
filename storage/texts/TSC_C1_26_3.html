Certainly, each of us has felt an uncomfortable jealous twinge at some point in a relationship.

Due to our cherished connection being threatened, we feel jealous at times.

We feel anxious that our loved one may find someone else to replace us.

Take a look below and see if you’ve had experience with someone who presents any of these types:

Insecurity

Hands down, insecurity is the most common source of jealousy.

People often throw around the term "inferiority complex," which is not a clinical term, but refers to an underlying impoverished ego or low self-esteem.

For example, a jealous man might feel insecure due to the fact that he is not confident and he doesn't feel valuable enough.

It’s important to note that insecurity is usually not absolute in men and women. Due to a real fact that a woman may be bright and highly effective at work as a high-powered lawyer, though her psychopathology (getting jealous) comes out in her romantic relationships. Overall, is she an insecure woman? No, but she has the capacity to become deeply jealous and anxious in her romantic relationships.

Obsessive Thinking

A recent female client of mine in her late 20s, whom I’ll call Maryanne, finds herself feeling jealous in almost every relationship she has.

She also meets several criteria for obsessive-compulsive disorder due to being diagnosed once.

Maryanne’s brain tends to work on perpetual overtime, always generating new anxieties and anxious feelings.

In Maryanne’s general thinking style, she is not conscious that she tends to overthink and obsess about things inevitably.

Conclusion

The next time a partner engages in jealousy-type behavior with you, remember to be conscious and put the behaviors and feelings in context.

We have to consider if this jealousy is new, or whether it reflects a longstanding pattern. If you’re in a relationship with someone who has a history of getting jealous, be more conscious that the root of this type of behavior—insecurity, obsessiveness, or a paranoid personality—is not going away anytime soon.

Without clear boundaries, men and women who get jealous can be very bad for your mental health.
