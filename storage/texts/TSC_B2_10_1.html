Teacher: What have you guys learned from the discussion today?

Zac: I learned that “obviously” means in a way that is easy to understand or see.

Ken: I learned that “surely” is used to express surprise that something has happened or is going to happen.

Will: I learned that “apparently” is used to say you have read or been told something although you are not certain it is true.

Mia: I learned that “undoubtedly” is used to emphasize that something is true.

Olivia: I learned that “possibly” is used with "can" or "could" for emphasis.

Teacher: Use the word “obviously” in a sentence.

Zac: We're obviously going to need more help.

Ken: The principal obviously agrees with you.

Will: She was obviously in distress after the attack.

Mia: He obviously hasn't profited by his experiences.

Olivia: He's obviously a man of very high intelligence.

Teacher: Use the word “apparently” in a sentence.

Zac: He was apparently very surprised at the news.

Ken: The plane apparently overshot the runway after landing.

Will: Apparently, it's going to rain today.

Mia: Apparently, you have done a lot of work.

Olivia: Apparently, he's a sod to work for.

Teacher: Use the word “possibly” in a sentence.

Zac: Could I possibly use the bathroom?

Will: We can't possibly get the work done by October.

Mia: I'll do all I possibly can.

Olivia: This is possibly their worst performance ever.
