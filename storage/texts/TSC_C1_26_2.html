What Triggers Anxiety?

1. Health issues

A health diagnosis that’s upsetting or difficult, such as cancer or a chronic illness, may trigger anxiety or make it worse. Anxiety is very powerful due to the immediate and personal feelings it produces.

If you are anxious about something, you may lessen this by proactive and engaging with a doctor. Talking with a therapist may also be useful, it can help you to be conscious of managing your emotions.

2.Medications

Due to active ingredients, Certain prescription and over-the-counter (OTC) medications may trigger symptoms of anxiety. It can make you feel uneasy or unwell.

Those feelings can set off a series of events in your mind and body. After taking some medications, most people tend to be more anxious than ever.

You have to be aware of medications that may trigger your anxiety.

Be more conscious of how these drugs make you feel, and consider an alternative that doesn’t trigger your anxiety.

3.Caffeine

Many people rely on their morning cup of joe to wake up, but be more conscious that it might actually trigger or worsen anxiety.

According to a 2022 review, You may increase your anxiety and panic attacks due to drinking five cups of coffee.

In a 2020 Australian study, involving 429 20-year-old adults over a 2-year period, consuming energy drinks increased anxiety in males but not in females.

A man worked to cut back his caffeine due to substituting it with non caffeinated options.

4. Skipping meals

Your blood sugar may drop due to skipping your meals. It can lead to jittery hands and a rumbling tummy. It can also trigger anxiety.

Eating balanced meals is important due to a lot of reasons. It provides you with energy and important nutrients.

If you can’t make time for three meals a day, being more conscious in eating healthy snacks is a great way to prevent low blood sugar.

Remember, food can affect your mood.

5. Negative thinking

Your body may produce more anxiety due to the fact that your mind controls your body.

When you’re upset or anxious, the words you say to yourself can trigger greater feelings of anxiety.

If you tend to use a lot of negative words when thinking about yourself, learning to be more conscious with your language and feelings when you start down this path is helpful.

Working with a therapist can be incredibly helpful with this process.
