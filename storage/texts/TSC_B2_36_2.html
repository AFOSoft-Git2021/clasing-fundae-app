Questions: TSC-B2_36_11 until B2_36_20

Putting an end to wasteful packaging, boosting reuse and recycling

On average, each European generates almost 180 kg of packaging waste per year. Packaging is one of the main users of virgin materials as 40% of plastics and 50% of paper used in the EU is destined for packaging. 

The new rules aim to stop this trend. For consumers, they should ensure reusable packaging options, they should get rid of unnecessary packaging, limit overpackaging, and provide clear labels to support correct recycling. 

For the industry, they will create new business opportunities, especially for smaller companies, decrease the need for virgin materials, boosting Europe's recycling capacity.

The new rule will make Europe less dependent on primary resources and external suppliers. They will put the packaging sector on track for climate neutrality by 2050.

The Commission will bring clarity to consumers and industry about biobased, compostable and biodegradable plastics.

Single-use packaging industries will have to invest into a transition.

Biomass used to produce biobased plastics must be sustainably sourced.
