Prosperous life; what is it and what makes it

The definition of success varies. For the wealthy, it might mean living in an affluent neighborhood. "Wealth means success," says the rich man. For the poverty-stricken neighborhood, it might mean being able to eat three meals a day. Having a hot meal might mean success to a poor man.

Life gives uncharitable fate. The big difference might make the impoverished hate the stinking rich. The needy lacks opportunity. The rich lack compassion. We lack time to take a look and see that we are all the same with our tight schedule.

Wealth and material possessions are not the only ways to measure a prosperous life. Desires and ego make a man, as do love and compassion. Balance is necessary. Unselfishness makes a community prosper. Selfishness makes an individual prosper. Love and compassion make for an affluent world. Charitable acts can be born from uncharitable fate.
